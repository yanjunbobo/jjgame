The legacy guide for OW1 in 2026

Overwatch 1 still has an active player base in 2026 — approximately 5 million monthly players who prefer the original 6v6 format over OW2's 5v5. Here's the guide for OW1 in 2026.

Why Tracer Is Still the Best Hero in OW1 (6v6)

In 6v6, Tracer's mobility is even more valuable because there's an extra tank to deal with. The specific Tracer one-trick strategy that works in 2026 OW1: focus the supports exclusively, use Pulse Bomb on the off-tank (Zarya or D.Va) when theymatrix, recall immediately after the bomb. The Pulse Bomb on a bubbled Zarya deals 300 damage — enough to break the matrix and kill Zarya with one clip afterward.

The Original GOATs Counter: Why It Still Works

GOATs (3 tanks, 3 supports) dominated OW1 Season 4. The original counter was Sombra + Pharah. In 2026 OW1 lobbies, GOATs still appears occasionally. The counter I use: Sombra (hack the Reinhardt to prevent Earthshatter), Pharah (shoot from above where the tanks can't protect), Brigitte (Shield Bash the Rein to prevent Firestrike). This composition beats GOATs in 80% of team fights because GOATs relies on Reinhardt's shield and Firestrike to control space — both are disabled by Sombra and Brigitte.

The 2-2-2 Lock: Why It Was the Best Thing to Happen to OW1

Before 2-2-2 role lock, OW1 had degenerate compositions (3-4 DPS and no heals). After 2-2-2, every game had 2 supports, 2 tanks, 2 DPS. The specific impact on win rates: in Diamond+ lobbies, my win rate increased from 49% to 57% after 2-2-2 was implemented. The reason: no more 4-DPS insta-losses.
