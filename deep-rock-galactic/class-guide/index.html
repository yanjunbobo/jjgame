Every class's role and when to pick each

Class Tier & Role Guide — When to Pick Each Class | 2026

Deep Rock Galactic has 4 classes and every one is essential. But in public matches, certain compositions win more consistently. The key isn't "best class" — it's "correct class for this mission + hazard level." Here's the definitive class guide.

📊 Class Roles & When to Pick

Scout (The Illuminator)
Role: Exploration, mining hard-to-reach minerals, lighting caves. Why Scout is essential: minerals in the ceiling (gold, nitra, mushrooms) cannot be reached by any other class without platforming. Without Scout, you WILL leave 30% of resources behind. Pick Scout when: any mission with "high ceiling" terrain, Escort missions (Scout lights the refueling path), or Point Extraction (fast mineral grab).
Gunner (The Heavy)
Role: Crowd control, heavy damage, shield generation. The shield grenade is the most valuable utility in the game — a 5-second invincibility bubble for the entire team. In point extraction missions with swarm modifiers, Gunner's shield is what prevents wipes. Pick Gunner when: high swarm modifiers, low-player-count missions, or when you need to hold a position.
Driller (The Digger)
Role: Terrain manipulation, chokepoint control, crowd damage. Driller's C4 is the fastest way to mine through rock and create chokepoints against swarms. In Escort missions, Driller digs the shortest path to the minehead. Pick Driller when: complex terrain (lots of narrow passages), Escort missions, or you're playing with randos who don't communicate.
Engineer (The Builder)
Role: Area denial, platforming support, turrets. Engineer's platforms create walkable paths over pits, platforms for Scout to stand on for ceiling minerals, and the turret provides passive swarm damage. Pick Engineer when: the cave has lots of verticality, any mission with flying enemies (turrets shoot flyers), or Salvage Operations (defense phases).

⚠️ The 5 Class Mistakes

Mistake 1: 4 of the Same Class
In Haz 5 (highest difficulty), a balanced team (Scout + Gunner + Driller + Engineer) has 95% win rate. A 4-Scout team has 30% win rate. The reason: every class covers a gap the others can't. Without Driller, you can't create chokepoints. Without Engineer, you can't light ceilings. Without Gunner, you have no shields. Without Scout, you mine 60% of resources. Always play a balanced composition.
Mistake 2: Not Calling Resources Down
In the mine, press V to call down a supply pod for: nitra (for ammo), red sugar (for health), and in emergencies. Most greenbeards don't know this. Always call nitra when you find a large vein — you need 80+ nitra for every Uplink or Salvage mission's resupply. If you run out of ammo before completing an objective, it's because you didn't call nitra early enough.
Mistake 3: Fighting in Open Spaces
Against swarmers (small bugs that come in 20+), open space = death. The optimal position is always a chokepoint (doorway, tunnel, behind a Driller's created wall). If you're caught in open terrain, Driller creates a wall behind you, Gunner drops a shield, Engineer places a turret, Scout uses his flare to see and grapple away. Every class has a panic "get to cover" option — use it immediately.
Mistake 4: Ignoring Mission Objectives Until Last Minute
In Extraction missions, the timer starts when the first object is collected. If you spend 15 minutes mining everything before starting the objective, you give the swarm more time to spawn. Start the objective within 5 minutes of landing. Mine the BONUS minerals after the objective is complete.
