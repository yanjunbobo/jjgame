PvP Guide — When to Use AR vs Bolt vs SAR | 2026

The #1 question in Rust PvP: "What weapon should I use?" The answer is always situational. But beginners default to AR in every situation, and it's wrong 60% of the time. Here's the decision framework used by top Rust PvPers.

📊 Weapon Decision Framework

Use AR (Assault Rifle) when:
- Engagements are under 50 meters
- You have numbers advantage (2v1, 3v1)
- The enemy is stationary (building, looting)
- You have armor (半甲 or above)
The AR's 30-round magazine and automatic fire wins close-to-mid range fights. But at 100+ meters, the SAR outperforms due to accuracy.
Use Bolt (Bolt Rifle) when:
- Long-range map control (over 100 meters)
- 1v1 open field fights
- Counter-sniping against other bolts
- You're playing solo against groups (hit and run)
Bolt = 1 shot, 1 kill at any range if you hit the head. But you have 1 shot before they hide. If you're against armor, bolt body shots do 55 damage — you need a follow-up. Don't engage armored targets at range unless you have a headshot angle.
Use SAR (Semi-Auto Rifle) when:
- Mid-range fights (50-100 meters)
- You're solo against multiple targets
- Close-range building fights (below 30 meters)
SAR = the most versatile weapon in Rust. It's not the best at any range but it's never the wrong choice. 75 damage per body shot, 2 shots to kill armored targets. 3-tap headshot. It shoots twice as fast as bolt (semi-auto vs bolt action). This is the default choice for 80% of fights.

⚠️ The 5 PvP Mistakes

Mistake 1: Spraying at Long Range
AR spray at 100+ meters = 80% of bullets miss. The enemy hears you shooting, knows your position, and has 3-5 seconds to pre-aim you. At 100+ meters, use SAR single shots. Each shot should be deliberate and aimed. Spray only below 50 meters.
Mistake 2: Not Checking Your Surroundings Before Fights
Most players die to third parties — 40% of PvP deaths in Rust come from a third player who wasn't in the original fight. Before committing to any engagement, spend 10 seconds checking 360 degrees. If you hear a third player's footsteps, disengage and reposition.
Mistake 3: Fighting Near Your Base
If you fight within 100 meters of your base, you're telling every nearby player "there's activity here." They will come to investigate, find you low on ammo/health, and third-party you. Fight 200+ meters from your base. Use cover to reposition after fights. If you absolutely must fight near base, build a fighting tower 150m away first.
Mistake 4: Not Having an Exit Strategy
Before every fight, know your exit path. If you're on the road, what's your cover if you start losing? A rock, a tree, a car? If you're on monument ground, where's the nearest building to retreat into? Players who win fights in Rust aren't always the best shooters — they're the best at knowing when to fight and when to run.
