⚠️ The 5 Settings Mistakes

Mistake 1: Running Above 144Hz Without G-Sync/Freesync
If your monitor is 144Hz and you're getting 200 FPS but don't have G-Sync, you get screen tearing that hurts tracking. Either cap FPS at 144 or turn on G-Sync.
Mistake 2: Using Default FOV (70)
Default FOV of 70 is 30% narrower than 90. At close range, enemies appear 22% smaller at 70 FOV vs 90. Always set to 90-95. Some pro players use 110.
Mistake 3: Having Aim Assist on OFF (Controller)
Aim Assist on PC is critical for controller players. "Medium" gives the best balance of tracking help without over-aiming. "Classic" gives stronger close-range magnetism.
Mistake 4: Not Rebinding Keybinds
Default "E to interact" is 3cm from WASD. Remap to a thumb button or mouse side button. Every millisecond of reaction time counts. The best pro players have all abilities on easily accessible keys.
Mistake 5: Not Adjusting Audio Settings
Enable "Boost High Altitude" and "Surround Sound" in Windows. Disable "Volume Modes." Footsteps in Apex are directional — good audio settings give you 200ms of reaction time advantage over players with default audio.
