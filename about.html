About PlayVault

Last updated: May 2026

PlayVault started as a side project between two friends who kept losing track of game mechanics. One would discover a better build in a Discord channel, forget it by the next patch, then spend twenty minutes searching old Reddit threads to find it again. That frustration turned into a habit of writing things down — and then into this site.

We cover 70 games and growing. Not every game gets equal attention — if a title doesn't have genuine search demand or we haven't personally spent time with it, we won't pad the catalog just to hit a number. Each game that does make it onto PlayVault has at least one main guide and at least one focused sub-guide targeting a specific question players actually Google.

What Makes This Different

Most game guide sites scrape patch notes, rephrase wiki entries, and call it content. We've been on the receiving end of that — reading a "tier list" that hasn't been updated since launch, or a "best build" guide that references mechanics that changed six patches ago.

Every page on PlayVault is written around a specific question someone typed into a search bar. The data comes from a mix of personal playtime (some of our team has 2,000+ hours in specific titles), community-sourced statistics from tracker sites and competitive forums, and patch note analysis. When we say a weapon is used by 68% of Diamond-ranked players, we mean it — and we'll link to the source.

We don't accept payment from game studios to promote specific content. Rankings and recommendations are based on competitive data and practical testing, not sponsorships.

The Content Process

Here's roughly how a guide gets written: we identify a gap — a game that has search demand but no satisfying answer ranking in the top results. Then we look at what the existing top results are saying, find the gaps in their coverage, and write something that fills those gaps with specifics. Numbers, timings, real scenarios.

Guides get updated when patches change the meta. If a major update drops for a game we cover, we revisit the relevant pages within a few days. Old guides that become outdated get a visible "Last Verified" note at the bottom — we don't let stale content sit alongside current patch information.

What We Don't Do

We don't copy content from other guides. We don't use template paragraphs that apply to every game in a category. We don't write a guide to a game we haven't played, and we don't publish a tier list based entirely on secondhand data without testing it ourselves first.

We also don't run intrusive ads, pop-ups, or auto-playing video. The only monetization is a single AdSense block at the top of pages. If that ever changes, we'll be transparent about it here first.

The Team

We're a small operation. No bylines, no influencer accounts — we're just two people who like building things and playing games. The site is hosted on GitHub Pages and maintained in our spare time. It's not a business, it's a project.

If something on the site is wrong — a stat is outdated, a link is broken, a guide is genuinely bad — the fastest way to reach us is through the contact form. We read everything.
