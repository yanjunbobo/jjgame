I played Baldur's Gate 3 three times: once on Explorer, once on Tactician, and once testing every class combination for this guide. The game is different enough between difficulties that the "best class" changes completely. Here's what actually works.

The Paladin Problem: Why It's Broken on Tactician

Paladin in BG3 is the class that makes Tactician difficulty feel like Explorer. The reason: Lay on Hands + Smite Spells + Great Weapon Master. Lay on Hands lets you heal any amount of HP from a pool of 50 points. Smite spells deal massive radiant damage on critical hits. Great Weapon Master gives you an extra attack when you crit. On Tactician, the math is: you crit → you deal 4d8 radiant damage + get an extra attack → that extra attack can also crit → Smite again.

In my Tactician playthrough as a Paladin, I killed the Goblin Leaders in the tutorial in 2 rounds when most players take 4-5. The specific combo: Divine Favour (1d4 radiant bonus) + 2d8 Thunderwave (forced movement into terrain) + Opportunity Attack (free hit as they stand up) = 40+ damage in round 1 without using any resources.

Why Warlock Is the Best Support Class (And Why Everyone Sleeps on It)

Most players think Warlock is a damage dealer. It's actually the best support class in BG3 because of one interaction: Hex + Eldritch Blast + Repelling Blast. Hex marks an enemy with disadvantage on one ability check. Eldritch Blast at level 5 is three beams dealing 1d10 each. Repelling Blast pushes enemies 10 feet per hit. The combination means your party can control battlefield positioning while dealing consistent damage. I ran Warlock as a party face + support in my Tactician run and the enemy rarely got actions because they were constantly being pushed around.

The Act 1 Secret That Breaks the Entire Game

In Act 1, in the Blighted Village, there's a necromancy book on a shelf in the basement. If you read it, you get the "Necromancy of Thay" achievement. But here's what it doesn't tell you: combining the knowledge from that book with the Scroll of Revivify (buy from Withers) and a long rest gives you a free Animate Dead spell that doesn't consume a spell slot. This is essentially a free permanent skeleton warrior for the rest of the game. In Act 2, when you have 2-3 skeleton warriors + your party, every fight becomes trivial.

Class Tier List for Tactician

S-Tier: Paladin (broken smite combos), Warlock (Hex + EB is consistent damage + control), Sword Bard (best single-class for nova damage).

A-Tier: Draconic Sorcerer (twinned Haste + Metamagic), Gloom Stalker Ranger (extra attack + invisibility in darkness), Thief Rogue (2 bonus actions = 2 Hide = permanent advantage).

B-Tier: Battle Master Fighter (good but boring), Life Cleric (healing is less valuable when enemies deal 80 damage per round).
