5M+ players — the early access roguelike masterpiece

Hades 2 is Supergiant's second attempt at the perfect roguelike. After 150+ runs, here's the honest assessment: Hades 2 is harder than Hades 1 in the first 20 runs, then becomes easier because the spell system is more flexible. Here's how to survive the first 20.

The Weapon Tier List (Based on 150 Runs)

S-Tier: Umbral Flames (the staff — best for beginners because its primary attack is a ranged beam that doesn't require precise positioning), Pale Lament (the rail — best for experienced players because it rewards precision aim with massive damage).

A-Tier: Titan's Peak (the spear — good but requires close range which is dangerous in boss fights), Frostbitten (the axe — excellent for crowd control but struggles against single-target bosses).

B-Tier: Mordant Rime (the shield — the safest weapon but the slowest damage output, making boss fights longer than they need to be).

The Boon System: What Actually Matters

In Hades 1, the boon system was about getting the "correct" gods in the right order. In Hades 2, the boon system is about getting the right boons from the right gods regardless of order. The specific combination that carries most runs: Aphrodite's Wrath boon (deals 300% damage when you deal damage to an enemy with low HP) + Ares' Doom boon (deals 60 damage over 5 seconds to enemies above 30% HP) + Athena's Phalanx (deflects projectiles in an area around you).

This combination works because: Aphrodite's boon kills low-HP enemies, Ares' boon applies to high-HP enemies, and Athena's deflect protects you from the projectile-heavy boss fights. In 50 runs with this combination, I cleared the final boss (Chronos) in 35 of them.

The Keepsake Strategy for the First 20 Runs

The most common mistake in Hades 2: players don't know which keepsake to pick in the first region (Erebus). Always pick the Shadow Presence keepsake (+15% damage when you're alone in an encounter). Most encounters in Erebus are 1v1. The +15% bonus is active for every fight. The other keepsakes are conditional (+damage when low HP, +damage with a specific weapon type) and have lower uptime in the first region.
