🔱 Weapon Aspects & Daedalus Hammer Guide

Hades 2's weapon aspect system dramatically changes how each weapon plays. This guide covers every aspect ranked by power, optimal Daedalus hammer choices, and how to unlock each aspect in 2026.

Staff of Momus - Aspects Ranked

Aspect of Melinoë (S): The default aspect with the best single-target damage. Pom Blossom special builds pom stacks on your attacks, creating exponential scaling. The standard boss-killing choice for experienced players.

Aspect of Selene (A): Adds a lunar mark that detonates after 3 casts, dealing bonus damage in an AoE. The mark stacking enables devastating burst windows. Weakness is the setup time required before detonation.

Aspect of Hecate (B): Converts the special into a teleport that leaves a damaging shadow. The mobility is unmatched, making it excellent for aggressive positioning. Damage output is lower than other aspects.

Hexes & Boon Priority

The hex system is your most powerful toolset. Prioritize these hexes in order: Night Bloom (passive pom generation) for sustained fights, Moon Water (invincibility frames during dash) for survivability, Silverless Arrow (bonus damage on full mana) for burst windows.

For standard boons, prioritize Aphrodite's Crush (flat damage reduction) first, then Artemis's Hunter's Flare (bonus damage on special hits) for special-focused builds. Athena's Divine Dash remains the best dash boon for I-frames.

Daedalus Hammer Choices

Hammer upgrades are build-defining. For Staff: Amplified Voltage (+50% special damage) is the top choice for all content. Extended Range is second priority for boss safety. Avoid Double Special—the cast rate doesn't compensate for the reduced individual hit power.

For any weapon, always take +damage upgrades over +speed in the first 3 runs. The time saved by attack speed rarely offsets the damage loss when fighting bosses.

Shadow Shop Priority

In the shadow shop, prioritize Gold for runs where you have good boons, Death Defiance charges for boss fights, and Health regeneration for non-boss encounters. Never buy the damage multiplier in early rooms—it's not worth the 50 gold premium.
