🏆 Current S-Tier Meta Decks (May 2026)

Deck #1: Pikachu EX + Raichu EX (Electric Aggro)
Win rate: 58%. Core: 4x Pikachu EX, 2x Raichu EX, 4x Electro Ball. Strategy: flood the board with small Pokemon, evolve Raichu, use Raichu EX's 130 damage nuke to close games. Best for: fast matches, climbing quickly.
Deck #2: Mewtwo EX + Gardevoir EX (Psychic Control)
Win rate: 55%. Core: 3x Mewtwo EX, 2x Gardevoir EX, 4x Professor's Research. Strategy: use Mewtwo EX's damage cap removal + Gardevoir's damage boost = 200+ damage per turn. Best for: late-game oriented players.
Deck #3: Charizard EX + Arcanine EX (Fire Burn)
Win rate: 52%. Core: 3x Charizard EX, 2x Arcanine EX, 4x Rare Candy. Strategy: accelerate to Charizard by turn 3, use Burn Stadium to deal 30 damage per turn passively. Best for: meta counters (burn counters Pikachu EX).

⚠️ The 5 Deck Building Mistakes

Mistake 1: Not Having a Consistent 1st Turn
Pokemon TCG Pocket is draw-heavy. Your ideal first turn should be: Evolution (attach energy) + Supporter (draw 2). If your deck doesn't allow this consistently, you'll lose 50% of your opening hands.
Mistake 2: Running Too Many EX Pokemon
EX Pokemon give your opponent 2 Prize Cards when knocked out. If you have 6 EX Pokemon and your opponent knocks out 3, they win. Keep EX count at 4-5 maximum, and always have 1-2 non-EX as your primary attackers.
Mistake 3: Not Adjusting for the Meta
If everyone in your bracket plays Pikachu EX, run a Gyarados or Starmie deck. Water-type deals 2x damage to Electric. Meta-adjusted decks win 15-20% more than tier lists alone.
Mistake 4: Using Ranked Decks in Casual
Practice new decks in Casual first. Every misplay in Ranked costs you 10-20 rank points. Test your deck for 10 casual matches to build muscle memory before climbing.
