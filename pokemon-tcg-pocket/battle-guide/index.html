1. Counter Decks for S-Tier

Beat the meta. These decks are specifically designed to counter Charizard EX and Mewtwo EX.

🛡️

Steelix EX Counter Deck (Anti-Charizard)

Win Rate vs Charizard: 68%

Core Pokémon

  • 3x Steelix EX (180 HP, Steel typed)
  • 2x Metang (100 HP, recovery)
  • 4x Roggenrola (costs 1 to retreat)
  • 2x Onix (80 HP, cheap wall)

Key Trainers

  • 4x Professor Birch (draw)
  • 4x Giovanni (damage boost)
  • 4x Lt. Surge (energy retrieval)
  • 4x Celadon City (deck thinning)
Strategy: Steelix is resistant to Fire (Charizard deals 120 but Steelix takes 80). Stack Giovanni on Steelix for +30 damage. Charizard can't keep up.
🌀

Sableye Disruption (Anti-Mewtwo)

Win Rate vs Mewtwo: 72%

Core Pokémon

  • 4x Sableye (60 HP, can use opponent's deck)
  • 2x Garbodor (blocks abilities)
  • 2x Trubbish (Garbodor setup)

Disruption Package

  • 4x Junk Hunt (Sableye's attack: shuffle opponent's hand)
  • 4x Giovanni (boss)
  • 4x Professor Oak (full hand reset)
Strategy: Use Sableye's Junk Hunt to force opponent to shuffle their hand back. Then Sabrina their active to lock their key card. Mewtwo can't function without setup.

3. Advanced Battle Tips

🎯 Turn 1 Priority: Energy Attachment

Always attach 1 energy on Turn 1 if you're playing an EX deck. This sets up your Bennet + turn 2 attack combo. A turn 1 pass with Charizard means you're turn 4 before you deal damage instead of turn 3.

🎯 The Sabrina Trap

Most players use Sabrina defensively — switching your damaged active out. Use it offensively. Switch their EX into the active spot when they're 1 turn away from KOing you. Their retreat cost 3 energy = they lose a turn.

🎯 Prize Card Manipulation

When taking a prize from an EX knockout, you draw first then your opponent retreats. Sequence matters: If you KO their last prize pokemon, you take 3 prizes and win immediately. Don't give them a chance to draw into a comeback card.

🎯 Coin Flip Consistency

Mewtwo's damage is coin-flip based. Over 10 matches, the math evens out. Don't tilt on bad flips. A single bad flip doesn't mean the deck is bad — the 58% overall win rate is the data point that matters.

4. Ranked Climbing Strategy

ROOKIE

Play any deck. Practice fundamental mechanics. Don't worry about win rate.

GREAT

Meta decks start appearing. Build your first S-tier deck (Charizard or Mewtwo). Focus on energy attachment timing.

ULTRA

Players know matchups. Sideboard strategy matters. Swap tech cards based on what you're facing.

MASTER

Bait and switch. Predict opponent's Sabrina, play around counters. This is where deck mastery matters more than deck quality.

LEGEND

The top 500. Matchup knowledge + sequencing is everything. Counter decks become viable. A well-tuned Steelix deck at Legend can reach Top 100.
