🃏 Best Jokers Tier List

Balatro's Joker system is the core of every run's strategy. This tier list ranks every Joker by effectiveness, covering the most impactful additions to any deck archetype in 2026.

S-Tier Jokers

Photographic Printer: The most broken Joker in the game. Copies the last Joker played, effectively doubling any Joker's effect. Synergizes with everything. Finding this in the first shop should dictate your entire run strategy.

Fibonacci: +4 chips per hand played, stacking. At high hand counts, Fibonacci provides more chips than any other Joker. Works in every deck without specific requirements. The consistent chip output makes it essential for scoring builds.

Steel Joker: Multiplies chip output by the number of Steel Cards in hand. When combined with Steel Cards, this becomes the highest chip multiplier in the game. The synergy is so powerful that building around Steel becomes a dominant strategy.

Lucky Card: 20% chance per hand to add a random card with a permanent buff. The random element is offset by the consistent value generation. Over a full run, Lucky Card provides more total chips than any flat multiplier Joker.

A-Tier Jokers

Joker Stencil: +8 multiplied by number of Jokers. Scales infinitely with Joker count. Early game weaker than Fibonacci but late game becomes the highest chip multiplier. Essential in Joker-heavy builds.

Blue Joker: +4 chips for each card below 5 in hand. Works best in high-card and pair decks that naturally include many low cards. The condition is easier to trigger than most conditional Jokers.

Dusk: +2 chips for each hand remaining. The condition triggers once at end of round, making it weaker than per-hand triggers. Still provides solid value in longer rounds.

Red Card: Adds a random card to hand each round. The randomness can brick hands but the card advantage is powerful. Card disadvantage can be mitigated with discards to control the random addition.

B-Tier Jokers

Two Club: +2 chips per Club card. Condition is easy to meet but the output is modest. Works in Club-heavy decks but not worth forcing.

Half Joker: Multiplies chips by 4 when hand has 3 cards or fewer. The condition is restrictive but the multiplier is significant. Works in Flush-focused builds that naturally have fewer cards.

Joker's Wild: Adds 4x mult if all cards in hand are different ranks. The condition is challenging to meet consistently. Only worth using in highly specialized decks.

Scoring Formula Priority

The scoring formula is: (Base Chips + Card Chips) × Multiplier + Bonus Mult. Understanding this formula determines Joker priority. Chips come from cards and chip Jokers. Multipliers come from mult Jokers. Bonus Mult comes from mult Jokers that add rather than multiply.

For maximum scoring, balance chip sources with multiplier sources. Running all chip Jokers without mult results in low scores despite high chip counts. Running all mult Jokers without chips results in low base scores that multipliers can't fix.
