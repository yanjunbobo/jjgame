Why Runs Fail at Ante 4: The Scaling Problem

After tracking 100+ runs: the #1 run killer is not having a scaling win condition by Ante 3. Most players pick jokers that are good early but don't scale. A +30 chips joker is fine in Ante 1 (where you need 300 chips). It's worthless in Ante 7 (where you need 15,000 chips).

The Scaling Math

Every ante doubles the chip requirement. Ante 1 = 300 chips required. Ante 2 = 600. Ante 3 = 1,200. Ante 4 = 2,400. Ante 5 = 4,800. Ante 6 = 9,600. Ante 7 = 19,200. Ante 8 = 38,400.

A +30 chips joker is +30 chips every hand. That covers Ante 1. By Ante 4, you need 2,400 chips — the +30 joker is providing 1.25% of your requirement. You need a joker that scales — either with the hand size (more cards played = more chips) or with the round (later rounds = more chips).

The Scaling Joker Categories

Per-hand scalers: Jokers that gain chips based on cards played or hand type. Examples: Joker (deals 4 damage per card played), Photographic (doubles chips on the first hand), Gros Michel (doubles chips on High Cards).

Per-round scalers: Jokers that gain value over time. Examples: Fly Me to the Moon (gains +2 chips per round, permanently), Walkie Talkie (gains +1 chips per hand, permanently).

Per-score scalers: Jokers that multiply based on your current score. Examples: The Soul (when score exceeds threshold, gain X mult), Matador (gain +8 mult when hitting the exact score needed).

Joker Tier List: Score Contribution Analysis

Based on average score contribution across 20 tracked runs with each joker.

S-Tier Jokers

Photographic (x2 Mult) — The best scaling joker in the game. Starts at x2 mult, doubles every round if you play one hand per round. By round 10, it's x1024 mult. The condition is restrictive (one hand per round) but the payoff is game-winning. When you have Photographic, play one hand per round — it maximizes the scaling.

Fly Me to the Moon — +2 chips per round, permanently. The most reliable chip scaler. By round 15, it's +30 chips per round. The chip count applies to every hand. Unlike Photographic, this joker doesn't require a condition — it just works.

Fibonacci — Every hand played adds +8 chips. The chip accumulation is faster than Fly Me to the Moon in the early rounds. Fibonacci + a high-chip hand type (like Flush) = massive chip explosion. The weakness: it's purely chip-based, not mult, so it needs a mult joker to convert the chips into scores.

Hologram (x2 Mult) — Requires holding 4 of the same suit. The x2 mult is the highest base mult in the game. The condition is difficult in 4-player Joker types (where you need a 4-card flush to activate). The reward is worth the constraint.

A-Tier Jokers

Joker (5 chips per card played) — The default standard. The chip per card is consistent and reliable. A 5-card hand = 25 chips. A 4-of-a-kind (best hand) = 20 cards played = 100 chips. Not flashy, but consistent.

Misprint (+15 chips) — Random chip bonus. Sometimes you get +5, sometimes +30. The variance is high but the average is strong. The trick: reroll it in the store if you get less than +10.

Bull (+4 chips and +1 damage per 10 chips held) — The chips held mechanic means it scales with money. In runs where you have high money (100+), Bull becomes a +20 chip joker. In low-money runs, it's weak.

Drell, Spare — Gain X mult when scoring cards are hearts. The scoring cards requirement is restrictive but the mult is high (up to x8 at 16 scoring hearts). Best in Heart Flush builds.

B-Tier: Situational

Ramen (+2 Mult, -1 hand size) — The minus hand size is devastating. You can only play 4 cards per hand instead of 5. The x2 mult compensates for most builds but is a pure loss for Straight builds (which require 5 specific cards). Only use in Flush or Pair/Triple builds.

Castle (per successful hand) — Scales with the number of times you've played that hand type. In a Hand Trap build (playing the same hand type repeatedly), Castle becomes one of the highest chip jokers in the game. In a variable hand build, it's weak.

Ante Strategy: How to Win Every Time

Ante 1-2: Establishing Your Build

In the first two antes, you're looking for two things: a chip joker (to hit the chip requirements) and a mult joker (to convert chips to scores). Don't commit to a specific hand type build yet — the early game is about finding what the run offers.

The optimal approach: buy 2 jokers in Ante 1 (the shop reroll is free, so use it if the jokers are bad). Prioritize jokers with scaling potential (Fibonacci, Fly Me to the Moon, Misprint with high values). Avoid jokers with restrictive conditions (Hologram, Drell) in Ante 1 — you don't know if you'll have the cards to support them.

Ante 3-4: Committing to a Build

By Ante 3, you should have a clear build direction. The three viable build types:

  • Mult Build: Focus on xMult jokers (Photographic, Hologram, etc.). The mult compounds, giving exponential score growth. Best for: players who want to win by killing bosses with one hand.
  • Chip Build: Focus on chip jokers (Fibonacci, Fly Me to the Moon, Joker). The chips give you the score floor to survive the chip requirements while you build mult.
  • Economy Build: Focus on money jokers (Credit Card, Diamond, etc.). The extra money lets you buy the jokers you need in later antes. Best for: players who want to stabilize before committing.

Ante 5+: The Endgame

By Ante 5, your build should be complete. At this point, you're not looking for new jokers — you're looking for vouchers (the shop upgrades that permanently improve your run). The two most important vouchers:

Hieroglyph: Adds a second joker slot (6 total). The extra slot is worth 200+ chips of value over the run because you can run two mult jokers simultaneously.

Tarot Cloth: Adds a card craft slot (2 slots). The ability to upgrade cards (make a 2-card into a 5-card Wild) is the difference between having the exact hand you need and not.

The 1 Billion Chip Run: Exact Breakdown

A 1 billion chip run requires the right combination of jokers, cards, and luck. Here's the skeleton of the run.

The Core Jokers (Required)

1. Photographic (x2 Mult) — The primary scaling mult. Play 1 hand per round starting Round 1. By Round 20, it's x1,048,576 mult.

2. Fly Me to the Moon (+2 chips/round) — The chip scaler. By Round 20, it's +40 chips per round.

3. Fibonacci (5 chips per hand played) — The chip multiplier for high-card hands. A 5-card hand = 25 chips × 5 = 125 chips base, then multiplied by all chip jokers.

4. Joker (4 chips per card played) — Secondary chip joker. 5 cards = 20 chips × 2 (Fly Me) × 3 (Fibonacci multiplier).

The Score Formula (Endgame Round)

At Round 20 with the above jokers, one High Card played:

  • Base chips: 20 (standard High Card)
  • + Fly Me to the Moon: +40 (Round 20 bonus)
  • + Fibonacci: +25 (5 cards × 5)
  • + Joker: +20 (5 cards × 4)
  • Total chips: 105
  • × Photographic: ×1,048,576
  • = 110,100,480 chips

That's 110 million chips from ONE High Card. With a Flush (5 cards of the same suit), the base chips are 30, and the final score is 165 million chips. With a Full House (3+2 cards), the base chips are 40, and the final score is 220 million chips. With the Stone Card (×3 mult on all cards), the scores triple again.

Card Upgrades: The Most Valuable Combinations

Wild Card (Upgrade 5 Cards to Joker)

The Stone Card (×3 mult on scored cards) is the best upgrade in the game for a mult build. With Photographic providing x2 mult per round, Stone Card triples every score — effectively x6 mult from one card upgrade.

The Buffoon Pack Exploit

At certain rounds, the shop offers Buffoon packs (free random jokers). The trick: if you get a bad joker from a Buffoon pack, you can sell it immediately for partial value. If you get a Photographic, keep it. This is the only way to get jokers that wouldn't appear in the normal shop — the Photographic is common, but getting it for free from Buffoon means you can spend your money on vouchers instead.
