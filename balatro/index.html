🃏 Stake-Specific Strategy & Joker Combos

Balatro's stake difficulty fundamentally changes your strategy. This guide covers stake-specific approaches, the most powerful joker combo chains, and how to adapt your game plan for higher stakes in 2026.

Stakes Ranked by Difficulty

White Stake (Default): Standard roguelike. No modifiers. Focus on building any synergistic deck. No special strategy required.

Green Stake: Boss blinds cost 4x ante. Prioritize boss blind rewards over small/blind economics. The boss reward is worth the ante cost.

Black Stake: Jokers cannot be sold. Commit to your joker choices early. Don't pick jokers that seem good but don't fit your deck archetype.

Gold Stake: Starting money reduced to $3. Economy vouchers become mandatory. Skip smaller vouchers to afford economy boosters. Your first two shop visits determine win probability.

Purple Stake: Rarer jokers cost 2x. Force budget jokers to carry you. The best joker is often the one that synergizes with your deck rather than the highest rarity.

Red Stake: Joker slots reduced to 4. Quality over quantity becomes critical. Every slot must earn its place.

Blue Stake: Maximum hand size is 7. High-card and pair decks suffer. Flush and Straight become relatively stronger since they use fewer cards.

Orange Stake: Start with -2 random cards. Start with negative economy. Play aggressively early to establish board presence before falling behind.

Pendant Stake: Endless mode only. All other stakes become irrelevant.

Joker Combo Chains

Fibonacci + Blue Joker + Joker Stencil: The classic chip stack. Fibonacci provides flat chips, Blue Joker adds cards-below-5 chips, Joker Stencil multiplies by joker count. This combo alone can reach 10,000+ chips without any hand.

Steel Joker + Photograph + Perkeo: Steel card multiplication compounds with photograph copies. Perkeo provides infinite photo copies if you're lucky. This combo is the highest theoretical score in the game.
