Deflection is the core of Sekiro combat

Combat Guide — Deflect, Posture & Shinobi Execution | 2026

Sekiro is NOT Dark Souls. The roll-and-hit approach kills you in Sekiro. The core mechanic is deflection — timing your block to turn enemy attacks into counter-posture damage.

🛡️ The Deflection System

The 3 Block Types:

  1. Normal Block (L1): Reduces damage by 40% but breaks posture slowly. For light attacks you can't deflect.
  2. Deflection (perfect timing, 0.3s before hit): Reduces damage to 0 AND deals posture damage to the enemy.
  3. Micro-step (moving into attack): Some attacks are undodgeable but can be stepped through. Moving into a thrust attack while pressing forward = the attack passes behind you.

The deflection window is approximately 0.3 seconds before impact. The audio cue: a metallic "ting" sound when you deflect correctly. If you hear a "thud," you blocked normally.

The Posture Rule: Every enemy has a posture bar. When the bar is full, the enemy is stunned for 1.5 seconds. Deflecting fills the posture bar 3x faster than normal blocking.

⚠️ The 5 Combat Mistakes

Mistake 1: Relying on Dodge Instead of Deflect
Against sweep attacks, dodge. Against thrust attacks, use Mikiri Counter (dodge forward + attack). Against normal attacks, DEFLECT. Most beginners dodge everything and wonder why the boss's posture bar never breaks.
Mistake 2: Healing in Front of the Enemy
Using the Healing Gourd takes 1.5 seconds. Heal when the enemy is at range, stunned, or executing an animation. After a successful deathblow, you have 3 seconds to heal with zero risk.
Mistake 3: Not Using the Shinobi Firecracker Early
The Shinobi Firecracker stuns enemies for 2 seconds and deals 40 posture damage. Against mini-bosses, using Firecracker at the start of every fight = free deathblow.
Mistake 4: Fighting General Tamura Instead of the Apemen First
At Ashina Castle, go left at the castle gate to find the Apemen cave. They drop Mibu Balloon of Wealth (2x XP) and Mibu Balloon of Soul (2x spirit emblem XP).
Mistake 5: Not Reading Enemy Attack Colors
Red = unblockable (use mikiri or dodge). Orange = deflect slightly late. Yellow = sweep (jump + stamp). Blue = thrust (mikiri counter).
