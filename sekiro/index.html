Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice — 2026 Complete Guide

The deflection timing window (0.3s), block vs dodge decision tree, color-coded attack system, Isshin Sword Saint phase breakdown, and prosthetic tool tier for every boss.

The Deflection Timing Window

After 500+ hours: deflection timing window is ~0.3 seconds before impact. This is not a visual cue — it's a feel that develops with muscle memory. The visual animation of the attack starting is about 0.3 seconds before the hit connects. If you press block after you see the visual, you're too late by 0.2 seconds.

The Color-Coded Attack System

Attack Type Reference

Red (Unblockable): Cannot be blocked or deflected. Must dodge away or use Ichimonji double (the counterattack that deals posture damage). Examples: Isshin's thrust in Phase 3, Owl's fire flask throw.

Orange (Late Deflectable): The wind-up is longer than it appears. Wait for the visual start of the attack, then deflect. If you deflect early (on the wind-up animation), you'll still take damage. Wait for the blade to start moving forward.

Yellow (Sweep): The attack sweeps low. Jump to avoid and land on the enemy for a jump attack (bonus posture damage). Examples: Lady Butterfly's sweep combo, Guardian Ape's ground sweep.

Blue (Thrust): The attack is a forward thrust. Use Mikiri Counter (dodge forward into the thrust while pressing attack) to deal massive posture damage. If you block a thrust, it fills your posture at 2x normal rate.

Purple (Grab): The enemy reaches for you. Dodge away — blocking doesn't work. Examples: Minister's grab, Isshin's mortal draw in Phase 4.

Isshin Sword Saint: The 4-Phase Fight

Phase 1 (100-75% HP): Learn the Rhythm

Isshin opens with a 4-hit combo: thrust → slash → slash → overhead. The sequence: Mikiri Counter the thrust, deflect the two slashes, dodge the overhead. After the combo, he has a 2-second recovery window. Hit him once, retreat. Repeat. Phase 1 is teaching you the basic rhythm — if you can survive here, you can win.

Phase 2 (75-50% HP): The Spear Phase

Isshin pulls out the spear. The key difference: the spear has longer range, so your sword can't reach him during his recovery windows. Use the Flame Vent prosthetic (deals damage over time and interrupts his attacks) to create openings. The fire damage also builds his susceptibility (the hidden bar that, when full, staggers him).

Phase 3 (50-25% HP): The Lightning Reversal

Isshin gains the bow and the lightning attacks. When he shoots lightning at you: jump and press attack while in the air to catch the lightning and reverse it back at him. This deals massive damage to him and heals you. The timing: press attack at the apex of the jump (when you're highest). If you press too early, you catch it but can't redirect. If you press too late, you get hit.

Phase 4 (25-0% HP): The Sword Saint Returns

Isshin at 25% HP uses his full arsenal from the earlier fight. The critical rule: one hit, then retreat 3 steps. Don't get greedy. The Ichimonji double (R1 × 2) is the safest damage option — it has forward movement (catches if he tries to retreat) and good posture damage. Hit, retreat. Wait for his combo. Deflect. Hit, retreat. Repeat. 4 cycles and he's dead.

Prosthetic Tool Tier

S-Tier Prosthetic Tools

Shinobi Firecracker (S-Tier): Stuns enemies for 3 seconds, during which you can deal free hits. Works on every humanoid boss except Isshin (immune). Best for: Guardian Ape, True Monk, Owl.

Mikiri Counter (S-Tier): Not a tool — a technique. Unlocks at Genichiro. Use it on every blue thrust attack. The posture damage from a successful Mikiri is 3x what a deflect does.

Flame Vent (A-Tier): Deals DoT (damage over time) and interrupts enemy attacks. Best for: bosses that have long combo sequences where the fire keeps ticking during the recovery window.
