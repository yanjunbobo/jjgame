⚠️ The 5 Sensitivity Mistakes That Hurt Your Aim

Mistake 1: Copying Someone Else's DPI
Pro sensitivity is personal. What works for a 3-finger player won't work for 4-finger. Start with our settings, then adjust: if you're overshooting targets, reduce by 10%. If you're under-tracking, increase by 10%.
Mistake 2: Having Same Sensitivity for All Scopes
4x and 6x require lower sensitivity than red dot. If your red dot is 50% and 4x is also 50%, you will never be able to make fine adjustments at range. The 4x should be 40-50% of your red dot sensitivity.
Mistake 3: Turning Off Gyroscope
Gyroscope is not an aim assist — it's a precision tool. In the 2026 meta, top 100 players all use gyro. Turn it on at 300% for ADS, lower for scoping. It eliminates the "snap" that happens when you release a thumb stick.
Mistake 4: Not Adjusting Fire Mode Sensitivity
Fire mode sensitivity should be HIGHER than camera sensitivity because your thumb moves faster during full-auto spray. If your spray feels uncontrollable, increase fire mode by 20%.
Mistake 5: Changing Settings Too Often
Give each sensitivity setting 3 days (50+ matches) before adjusting. If you change every day, you never build muscle memory. Set it, trust it, play it.
