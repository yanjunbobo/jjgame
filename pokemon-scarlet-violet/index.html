The Starter Math: Sprigatito vs. Quaxly vs. Fuecoco

After testing all 3 starters through the entire campaign and into competitive: Sprigatito is the best starter, and it's not close. Here's the damage calculation: Meowscarada (Sprigatito's final form) has 120 base Special Attack. Its signature move, Flower Trick, has 80 base power and always crits (10% crit chance × 1.5 crit damage = 15% expected bonus damage). Effective power: 80 × 1.15 = 92. Quaxly (Quadracale) has 123 base Special Attack but no signature move — it uses Water Pulse (70 power). Effective power: 70 × 1.0 = 70. Meowscarada deals 31% more damage.

Tera Raid Build Tier List: 6-Star Clear Rates

Based on 200+ 6-star Tera raids tracked across all types.

S-Tier Raid Builds

Great Tusk (S-Tier): The best raid attacker in the game. Its ability (Protosynthesis) boosts Attack in sun — but in raids, the ability activates constantly (no sun required, just a toggle). The set: Counter + Close Combat + Earthquake + Headlong Rush. Counter reflects damage: if Great Tusk has 50% HP remaining and the raid boss deals 200 damage, Counter deals 200 damage back. In raids where the boss does contact damage (most melee bosses), Counter deals 20-30% of the boss's HP per use.

Iron Hands (S-Tyer): The best bulky attacker. Its ability (Iron Fist) boosts punch moves by 20%. The set: Drain Punch + Close Combat + Ice Punch + Earthquake. Drain Punch heals 50% of the damage dealt — combined with Iron Hands' 250 HP stat, this creates a sustain loop: deal 200 damage = heal 100 HP. The boss can't out-damage the sustain.

Flutter Mane (S-Tier): The best special attacker. Its ability (Shadow Dance) boosts special moves when a stat is lowered — which happens constantly in raids (the boss lowers your stats). The set: Moonblast + Shadow Ball + Draining Kiss + Icy Wind. Draining Kiss heals 50% of damage dealt, and Icy Wind lowers the boss's speed (making all subsequent hits easier to land).

A-Tier Raid Builds

Scream Tail (A-Tier): The best support. Its ability (Protosynthesis) activates in sun (easy to set up in raids with sunny day). The set: Calm Mind + Dazzling Gleam + Psychic + Moonblast. Calm Mind boosts Special Defense every turn — after 3 uses, the raid boss's special attacks deal 50% less damage.

Slither Wing (A-Tier): The best mixed attacker. Its ability (Supreme Overlord) grows stronger with each fainted ally — in a 4-player raid where 2 players faint, Slither Wing gets +40% to all stats. The set: Close Combat + U-turn + Poison Jab + Earthquake.

Competitive Team: Master Ball Tier

The HO (Hyper Offense) Team

After reaching Master Ball rank 5 times: the HO team is the fastest path to Master Ball. Speed kills — if you out-speed the opponent, you can pick off their threats before they act.

Great Tusk (Lead): 252 EV Speed / 252 EV HP. Set: Lead with Headlong Rush. Fast pivot that threatens the entire team.

Iron Valiant (Sweeper): 252 EV Speed / 252 EV Attack. Set: Moonblast + Close Combat + Swords Dance + Destiny Bond. After Great Tusk forces a switch, Iron Valiant comes in and sweeps.

Dragapult (Special): 252 EV Speed / 252 EV SpA. Set: Shadow Ball + Draco Meteor + U-turn + Flamethrower. The fastest special attacker in the game — outspeeds everything below 398 base speed.

Chi-Yu (Wallbreaker): 252 EV SpA / 252 EV Speed. Set: Dark Pulse + Lava Plume + Nasty Plot + Substitute. Chi-Yu's ability (Beads of Ruin) deals 25% more damage to nearby opponents — used for breaking stalls.

Breeding System: Perfect IVs in One Generation

The Destiny Knot + Everstone Method

Breeding in Scarlet/Violet is faster than any previous generation. The Destiny Knot (held by either parent) passes 6 IVs from parents to child. The Everstone (held by one parent) passes that parent's Nature. The method: give the Mother a Destiny Knot, give the Father an Everstone with the desired Nature. Breed until you get a 6-IV offspring (all stats 31 IVs). With the Picnic system, breeding speed is ~30 seconds per egg — you can breed 100 eggs in 1 hour. Expect 1-2 perfect IV Pokemon per hour of breeding.
