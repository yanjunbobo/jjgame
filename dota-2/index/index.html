500K+ daily players — the deepest competitive game

The gap between a 3K player and a 7K player in Dota 2 is not mechanics — it's decision-making. I climbed from 3.2K to 6.8K over 2 years, and the biggest leap wasn't in how I clicked — it was in how I thought about the game between the clicks.

The 0:00 Ritual That Changed My Lane Win Rate

Before the horn, I check the enemy support's inventory. This takes 5 seconds and tells me the entire first 2 minutes of the lane. 4 Tangoes + 1 Stout Shield = they're going aggressive. 2 Tangoes + 1 Salve = weak laning phase. This one habit improved my laning win rate by 15%.

Why Support Is the Best Role for Climbing

In coaching 30+ players, support is the highest-ROI climb. Your carry will make positioning mistakes constantly. If you're the support, you can ward the areas they walk into and they won't die. They get free farm. You effectively carry the game by covering their mistakes. The heroes I recommend for support climbing: Lich (easiest deny), Lion (Finger of Death = press R to win fight), and Rubick (Spell Steal turns enemy carries against themselves).

The Mid-Game Itemization Decision That Costs 500 MMR

If enemy has 2+ Intelligence cores with low armor: build Desolator first. -6 armor applies before your physical damage lands, meaning every auto-attack deals 40-60 extra bonus damage against 0-3 armor targets.

If enemy has high burst damage (Lion, Lina, Nyx): build BKB first. The 10-second magic immunity at level 1 BKB is enough to survive the initial burst.

Buyback: The Rule You Break at Your Own MMR

At 30+ minutes, any team fight you lose without buyback = you lose the game. The enemy takes Roshan, pushes high ground, and you lose the base while you're still dead. Always have buyback gold ready after 25 minutes unless you're actively sieging.
