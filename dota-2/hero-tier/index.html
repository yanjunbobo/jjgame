Hero tier list from OpenDota (1B+ matches)

Most tier lists are written by pros for pros. I'm writing for the 3K-6K bracket, where mechanics exist but decision-making varies wildly.

Carry: Why Phantom Lancer Is the King of Your MMR

In the 3K-6K bracket, teams don't push when ahead — they farm. This is perfect for PL. With the Diffusal rework, PL can split into 8+ illusions that each deal meaningful damage. Specific build: Treads → Wraith Band x2 → Diffusal Blade → Heart → Manta → Bloodthorn. At 25 minutes you have 8 illusions at 180+ damage each with Diffusal active.

In the 3K bracket, enemies don't focus fire the real PL — they attack whatever is closest, usually an illusion. This gives you 3-4 extra seconds of damage output while they're hitting the wrong target.

Mid: Invoker Is S-Tier If You Can Actually Play Him

A good Invoker casts 3 spells in the time a bad Invoker casts 1. The combo I practiced for 20 hours: Tornado → EMP → Chaos Meteor → Deafening Blast. This deals 1,200+ magical damage in 2 seconds and costs ~500 mana to the enemy team if full. At level 18 with 2 Null Talismans, this is a guaranteed kill on any support below 1,500 HP.

Support: Rubick Is the Carry Maker

Rubick's Spell Steal copies the level of the spell and maintains it for the full duration. A level 2 Finger of Death stolen from Lion is a level 2 Finger of Death for Rubick. Specific Rubick sequence: wait for the big spell → Telekinesis to lift the caster → Steal the spell → cast it on the enemy team while grounded.

Offlane: Axe Is the Simplest Win Condition in the Game

With a Desolator (-6 armor), Axe's Counter Helix at max rank deals 300+ damage per proc, triggering on every melee attack. Offlane strategy: max Battle Hunger first → sit in the creep wave → enemy carry tries to last hit → Battle Hunger tick + Counter Helix = they lose 50% of their HP in 5 seconds.

Data from OpenDota (1B+ matches), Patch 7.37. Last updated: May 2026.
