Terraria — Hardmode Complete Guide

The pre-hardmode prep checklist that saves worlds, the boss order that cuts 20 hours of grinding, weapons ranked by DPS, and why your world keeps getting corrupted by The Hallow.

Pre-Hardmode Prep: The Checklist That Saves Worlds

After losing 3 worlds to Corruption/Crimson spread: the #1 reason players restart worlds is not defeating Wall of Flesh — it's not preparing biomes before hardmode starts. Once The Hallow and the alternative biome spread, containing it requires enormous effort. Prevention is the only reliable strategy.

The Hellevator Network (Before You Hit the Wall)

Dig 6-block-wide vertical shafts (hellevators) every 50 blocks across your world, connecting the surface to the underworld. These block the spread of both The Hallow and Corruption/Crimson. The mechanic: both spread through blocks. A 6-block gap with empty space breaks the spread pattern. Without hellevators, your world becomes progressively more corrupted until you spend 10+ hours in post-game cleaning it.

The specific layout: at each hellevator, place a single row of Sunflower at the surface (this prevents spread from jumping the gap) and leave the shaft empty. The Sunflower is a one-time plant that permanently blocks corruption spread through that column.

The World Isolation Strategy

Alternative to hellevators: create a complete Corruption/Crimson barrier before hardmode. This means digging a 6-block-wide trench completely around your spawn area, 30 blocks deep, filled with Sunflowers. This is more labor-intensive but creates a permanently protected spawn zone. I use this method on every world now because hellevators can accidentally create shortcuts for the player that enemies also use.

Essential Pre-Wall Preparations

  • Fully upgrade pickaxe to Drax or higher (Mythril/Orichalcum anvil requires this)
  • Have 10+ Healing Potions (reach the Queen Slime for unlimited crystals)
  • Build an arena for Queen Slime (50 platforms spread vertically for bouncing)
  • Accumulate 100+ blocks of each pre-hardmode ore (Copper/Tin, Iron/Lead, Silver/Tungsten, Gold/Platinum)
  • Defeat all 3 pre-hardmode mechanical bosses at least once to unlock Hardmode NPC spawns

The Hardmode Progression Order (What To Do After Wall of Flesh)

Week 1: The Foundation

Destroy Wall of Flesh (enter Hardmode) → Find first Mimic (Pwnhammer is your priority — it's the only tool that breaks Demon Altars) → Mine Palladium (the first-tier Hardmode ore, faster than hunting Cobalt) → Build Palladium armor → Defeat The Twins (the first major Hardmode boss, drops enough souls to upgrade gear) → Mine Orichalcum (second-tier ore) → Build Orichalcum armor.

Why this order: Pwnhammer lets you break Demon Altars, which is the only way to spawn new Hardmode ore veins. The earlier you get Pwnhammer, the earlier new ore starts spawning. Mining Cobalt first is the common mistake — Orichalcum is faster to mine and the armor is better for early Hardmode survivability.

Week 2-3: The Mechanical Boss Rush

Defeat The Destroyer (the first HoE summon, drops Hallowed Bars) → Build Hallowed armor (the best pre-Plantera armor) → Defeat Skeletron Prime (unlocks the Steampunker NPC) → Defeat all three simultaneously (Queen Slime + Destroyer + Skeletron Prime in the same night = maximum soul yield for upgrades).

The optimal strategy for Hardmode boss fights: build a horizontal arena of at least 80 platforms, 30 blocks above ground. Place Heart Lanterns and Campfires for regeneration. Use the rod of discord for instant repositioning. For The Destroyer: the key is not the probes — it's the main body. When it curls into a ball, that's when it's most vulnerable.

Week 4: Plantera and the Golem

After defeating all mechanical bosses, the Jungle Temple unlocks. Inside: Plantera (the first true Hardmode power check) → Golem (drops Loot, necessary for final content). The Plantera rule: never use summon weapons. His dash attack will kill your minions. Use a weapon with high single-hit damage — the Daedalus Stormbow with Holy Arrows is the most consistent Plantera killer.

After Golem: Martian Madness event (unlocked after defeating Golem) → Solar Eclpse or Lunar Events → Moon Lord (final boss).

Weapon Tier List — Hardmode

Based on DPS testing against Mock Lord (training dummy equivalent) and boss kill speed comparisons.

S-Tier: The Endgame Carry

Daedalus Stormbow + Holy Arrows — 100+ DPS, infinite pierce, the most reliable boss killer in Hardmode. One quiver of Holy Arrows lasts through the entire Hardmode content. The bow itself is easy to obtain (Wooden Arrows + any bow + Crystal Ball + Soul of Light).

Meowmere — 95 DPS, launches a projectile that bounces 3 times. The projectile homing isn't perfect but in close quarters it deals consistent damage. Obtained from the Moon Lord (0.5% drop rate from Master Mode airdrops — patience required).

Star Wrath — 110 DPS, 3-projectile spread. The damage is split across 3 hits, meaning each individual projectile does 36 damage. Against high-defense enemies this is worse than single-hit weapons, but against groups it's unmatched.

A-Tier: Strong and Consistent

Amarok (Yelets) — 80 DPS, great for early Hardmode. The frost debuff slows enemies by 20%, giving you effective survivability boost. Best for players who haven't gotten Hallowed gear yet.

Keybrand — 85 DPS, lifesteal on hit (5% lifesteal). The healing is the key advantage — for bosses where you can't avoid damage, this sustain keeps you alive through extended fights. Dropped by Ogrim (Lunar Events).

Possessed Hatchet — 90 DPS, auto-fires when stationary. The unique mechanic: you can move while it attacks automatically. This makes it one of the easiest weapons to use while maintaining kiting mobility.

B-Tier: Situational Use

Chlorophyte Shotbow — 70 DPS, piercing shots. Chlorophyte bars are expensive (18 ore per bar), so the weapon cost is high. The piercing mechanic is valuable for crowd control but not for boss damage.

Terrarian — 120 DPS, but requires the Star Wrath and Meowmere to craft. If you're at the point where you can craft this, you've already beaten Moon Lord.

Armor Sets and Their Uses

Shroomite vs. Hallowed vs. Chlorophyte

Shroomite (Melee/Ranged): The stationary damage bonus of +100% is the highest in the game. For weapons that benefit from standing still (bows, guns), Shroomite is the best. For melee weapons that require movement, Shroomite is a trap.

Hallowed (All-rounder): The set bonus gives +15% damage and +8% critical chance. This is the consistent set — it doesn't require standing still and doesn't have narrow weapon requirements. Best for players who haven't mastered positioning.

Chlorophyte (Ranged): The set bonus gives +18% ranged damage and reduced ammo consumption. For weapons that consume expensive ammo (Holy Arrows, Meteor Shot), this set effectively doubles your ammunition capacity. Expensive to craft but worth it for endgame ranged builds.

Why You Get Stuck at Plantera (And How to Fix It)

The most common Hardmode wall: players reach Plantera with pre-Plantera gear and can't clear the Jungle Temple because their weapons don't deal enough damage. The Plantera fight itself isn't the problem — getting to Plantera is.

The fix: before entering the Temple, have at minimum: Hallowed armor or better, Daedalus Stormbow or equivalent (90+ DPS weapon), and a stack of Ironskin potions. The Temple's traps and enemies require sustained damage output, not burst. If your weapon deals 60 DPS and you need 5 minutes of continuous fire to kill a Temple Guardian, you're undergeared.

The fastest way to get Hallowed armor: farm Hallowed Mimics. Mimics spawn in the Underground Hallow after destroying a Demon Altar. Place a water candle in your arena and stand in the Hallow — Mimics will spawn around you. Each Mimic drops 3-5 Hallowed Bars. 10 Mimics = one full armor set.
