15M+ players — the MMO that respects your time

ESO is the most alt-friendly MMO ever made — your character can do every role (tank, healer, DPS) with a single build. After 400 hours, here's what actually matters for new and returning players.

Why Warden Is the Best Solo Class (And Why It's Not Close)

Warden's Polar Wind (a burst heal that also damages enemies) and the Bull Netch (a persistent heal that follows you) make solo PvE trivial. The specific solo build I use: 1 bar: Polar Wind, Bull Netch, Feral Guardian (the bear pet), Deep Fissure (the aoe dots), Ice Fortress (the shield). This build can survive any solo content including the newest DLC world bosses (which scale to 4-player difficulty).

In 50 hours of Warden solo play, I died exactly 3 times. All 3 deaths were from my own mistake (not using Bull Netch before pulling). Every death was preventable.

The Gold Farming Route: 50K Per Hour

The most reliable gold farming route: the Dark Brotherhood DLC Black Sacrament dailies. Each daily gives 2,000 gold plus sellable motifs (worth 5,000-15,000 gold each on the guild traders). Doing all 7 dailies takes 30 minutes and averages 25,000 gold in raw gold + motifs. That's 50,000 gold per hour.

The specific tip: complete the daily in the Daggerfall covenant area first (it's the shortest quest chain), then the Aldmeri Dominion, then the Ebonheart Pact. This order minimizes travel time.

Why One Bar Builds Dominate in 2026

The One Bar build (everything on one bar, instant bar swap for a burst window) is the most efficient PvP and PvE build in ESO. The specific One Bar Sosphene build (using the Sosphene monster set) deals 40,000 DPS on a single bar. I tested it against the dual-bar DW build (50,000 DPS). The one bar build wins in actual combat because 50,000 DPS on paper means nothing if you're managing two bars instead of fighting.

Based on 400+ hours of ESO. Last updated: May 2026.
