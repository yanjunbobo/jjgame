Once Human — 2026 Complete Guide

Why dual-weapon loadout is mandatory (enemy type mismatch), weapon build tier with actual DPS, enemy type system, Memory Pressures, and War Mode territory control strategy.

The Dual-Weapon Loadout System

After 300+ hours: the #1 loadout mistake is using one weapon for all situations. Stalkers (fast, low HP) die to high fire rate weapons — a single-shot sniper takes 3 seconds to kill one. Brutes (slow, high HP) die to high burst weapons — an AR takes 10 seconds of sustained fire. Using an AR against a Brute means you're empty on ammo when 3 Stalkers arrive 10 seconds later.

The Mandatory Loadout

Slot 1: AR (Assault Rifle) — For Stalkers, Rifted humans, and general PvE. The AR's 60-round magazine and 700 RPM fire rate handles swarms. The AK-7 (the best AR in the game) has 800 DPS sustained.

Slot 2: Shotgun or Sniper — Shotgun for Brutes and close-range PvP. The Remington (the best shotgun) deals 2,400 damage per shell — one shot kills a Brute. Sniper for long-range and boss damage. The SR-25 (the best sniper) deals 1,800 damage per shot — critical hits on weak points deal 3,600.

Enemy Type Reference

Humanoid Enemies

Stalker: Fast movement (runs at player speed), 200 HP, attacks in groups of 3-5. Weak point: head (2x damage). Counter weapon: AR at 10-meter range. Strategy: lead the group into a chokepoint, burst the first one, the others scatter.

Rifted: Humans infected by the alien Memory. 500 HP, use weapons. Weak point: weapon hand (disarms them, reducing their DPS by 80%). Counter: sniper headshot.

Brute: Slow movement, 2,000 HP, melee attacks deal 200 damage. Weak point: back (no armor). Counter: shotgun at point-blank range.

Environmental Enemies

Memory Crawler: Small, fast, 50 HP. Spawns in swarms of 20+. Kill them with AR — the splash damage from the AK-7's explosive rounds kills 3-4 per bullet.

Corrupted: Large mutant, 5,000 HP. Appears in Memory Pressure events. Requires a coordinated group of 4+ to kill. Strategy: tank draws aggro, 3 DPS focus fire while healer keeps tank alive.

Memory Pressures: The Event System

The Memory Pressure Scale

Memory Pressures (MP) scale from 1-10. Higher MP = stronger enemies + better loot. The rule: never do MP5+ solo. At MP5, the Corrupted spawn rate is 1 per 30 seconds — you'll be overwhelmed without a team. The loot quality at MP5 is 3x better than MP1, but only if you survive.

The Optimal MP Farm

MP3 is the sweet spot for solo players. At MP3: enemy HP is 2x MP1, loot quality is 2x MP1, and Corrupted don't spawn. You can farm MP3 indefinitely with a dual-weapon loadout. The specific farm route: circular path through the city center, hitting 3 stash locations per circuit. Each circuit = 15 minutes, 50,000 Starchrome (the currency).

War Mode: Territory Control Strategy

The Three Zones

Contested Zone (Red): High resources, high PvP danger. Contested zones give 3x the Starchrome but hostile players can attack you. Only enter with a full group of 4+.

Allied Zone (Blue): Safe resources, low PvP danger. Allied zones are controlled by your faction — other players can't attack you. Farm here when you need to build materials without PvP risk.

Neutral Zone (Gray): Medium resources, medium PvP risk. Neutral zones are the best resource per risk. Enter during off-peak hours (6 AM-10 AM server time) when fewer players are online.

The Territory Capture Sequence

To capture a territory: 1) Establish a forward base within 200 meters of the target (you need a spawn point for resupply). 2) Clear the perimeter — kill all enemy faction NPCs in the zone. 3) Hold the central point for 15 minutes — if your faction holds it for 15 minutes, the zone captures. The counter: the enemy faction will try to contest at the 14-minute mark. Always have 2 players in the zone at all times.
