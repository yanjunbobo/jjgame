Once Human is a survival MMO where the endgame is about optimization — weapon builds, territory control, and Starchrome farming efficiency. After 200 hours, here's what actually matters.

The Starchrome Economy: What Most Players Get Wrong

Starchrome is Once Human's premium currency. The fastest way to farm it: do public dungeons at World Tier 3+ with a group of 4. Each dungeon run takes 20-25 minutes and drops 800-1,200 Starchrome per run. That's 2,000-3,000 Starchrome per hour per player. Solo players get 400-600 per hour. The group bonus is massive.

The specific route I use: start at the northern-most deviation (the Foundry), clear it, then move south to the Waterfront, then to the Old Sawmill. These three locations have the highest mob density and the shortest travel time between them. Circular route = 1,000 Starchrome per 25 minutes = 2,400 per hour.

The Best Weapon Build for PvE

The SMG build with the Strobe-FA (the best SMG in the game for automatic fire) with a crit/crit damage/crit rate sub-stats is the best general PvE build. Reason: the Strobe-FA has the highest fire rate of any automatic weapon and the crit multiplier on the sub-stats gives you 50%+ crit rate, meaning half your shots deal 2x damage.

The specific build: Strobe-FA + Critical Rate mod (15% crit rate) + Critical Damage mod (25% crit damage) + Fire Rate mod (12% fire rate). This build clears Tier 3 public dungeons at 2x the speed of any other build I tested.

Why You Should Never Play Solo on PvP Servers

Once Human has open-world PvP on most servers. The moment you're flagged for PvP (which happens automatically in certain zones), any player can attack you. After 200 hours, I've found: playing solo on a PvP server is a 10x worse experience than playing on a PvE server. On PvE servers, you focus on building and farming. On PvP servers, you spend 50% of your time watching your back and losing progress to gankers.

The exception: if you're in a coordinated group of 5+ players who control a territory, PvP servers are profitable because you can control resource nodes. Solo = PvE. Group = PvP.
