Cursor Order: The Hidden 30% Damage Multiplier

After analyzing damage logs across 500+ runs: cursor order is the #1 damage factor in OPTCE. The last unit in the chain gets a 1.3x damage multiplier. If your highest ATK unit is not last in the cursor order, you're wasting 30% of its potential. This single change (reordering the cursor) made me clear content I was stuck on for months.

The Correct Cursor Order

Position 1 (First): Your lowest ATK unit. They go first so when the chain builds, the multiplier grows.

Position 2-3 (Middle): Your medium ATK units. The chain multiplier builds to 1.1x and 1.2x.

Position 4 (Last): Your highest ATK unit. The chain multiplier is 1.3x when they attack. Their ATK stat is multiplied by 1.3x, not 1.0x.

The math: if your highest ATK unit has 2,000 ATK and the enemy has 10 defense, a normal hit deals 1,990 damage. With the 1.3x cursor bonus, it deals 2,587 damage — 597 more damage for zero cost.

Captain Tier: Boss Clear Times

Average time to clear the hardest available content (Legendary Island), over 20 runs per captain.

S-Tier Captains

Red-haired Shanks (S-Tier): Clear time: 8 seconds. The new top-tier captain. His ability (deal 200x ATK as damage to all enemies, then boost ATK by 3x for 3 turns) is the highest burst in the game. The weakness: only 3 turns of boost — if you don't kill the boss in 3 turns, the boost expires and you have to rebuild.

Yamato (S-Tier): Clear time: 10 seconds. Yamato's 4x ATK boost to all units makes her the best captain for manual play. She also has a built-in 30% damage reduction, which lets her survive longer in content where bosses have dangerous HP thresholds.

A-Tier Captains

Luffy/Zoro (A-Tier): Clear time: 15 seconds. The dual captain setup (both Luffy and Zoro as captains) gives 4x ATK to all units. The advantage: you get both Luffy's color (STR) and Zoro's color (DEX) coverage, meaning you can hit the color advantage every turn.

Kid (A-Tier): Clear time: 14 seconds. Kid's ability (boost ATK based on how many times you've been hit) rewards a defensive playstyle. In content where you take damage, Kid gets stronger. In content where you don't take damage (speed runs), Kid is weaker.

Treasure Map: The Top 5% Strategy

The 30-Minute Session Rule

The optimal TM strategy: play 30-minute sessions, never more. After 30 minutes, concentration drops and mistakes accumulate (wrong color units, missed specials, wrong map path). The result: slower clears and fewer points. 3 x 30-minute sessions = more points than 1 x 90-minute session.

The First 3 Days vs. Last 2 Days Split

Days 1-3 (Point Farming): Run the easiest possible team to clear the boss. The goal: accumulate points while spending minimum resources. Use your lowest-rarity units that can clear the boss — save your rare units for ranking.

Days 4-5 (Ranking Push): Use your best team. The ranking cutoff (top 5% reward threshold) is set at the end of Day 5. The last 48 hours determine your ranking. Allocating resources in the last 2 days is the most efficient use of those resources.
