The definitive strategy guide for the 2026 release

Heroes of Might and Magic Olden Era is the most complex strategy game I've played in years. The faction system, hero development, and map control mechanics all interact in ways that take 100+ hours to understand. Here's what 200 hours taught me.

The Faction Tier List (Based on 200+ AI and PvP Matches)

S-Tier: Necropolis (the Death Knight hero ability — drains life from enemies on every attack, making your heroes nearly unkillable in early-game map fights), Dungeon (the best spellcasting faction — Shadow Maidens give +50% spell power to the hero, making your heroes casting AoE spells devastating).

A-Tier: Stronghold (the best early-game faction — the hero gains +2 attack per level from the Berserker secondary skill, meaning by level 10 your hero has +20 attack from this bonus alone), Conflux (the elemental magic faction — access to all 5 elemental types means you always have the counter to whatever the enemy is weak against).

B-Tier: Castle (the defensive faction — keeps are harder to siege but the hero development is slower than offensive factions).

Hero Build: The Death Knight Economy Build

The most broken build in the game: Death Knight with Pathfinding (for faster map movement) + Logistics (for faster between-fight movement) + Death Ripple (the Necropolis faction spell that heals your hero 20% per undead unit in the army). This build lets you fight every map encounter without returning to heal. In the campaign, I completed maps 40% faster with this build than with any other.

Map-Specific Strategy: Why You Keep Losing the Same Maps

In every Heroes game, the mistake players make is fighting battles they don't need to fight. Every week you spend in combat is a week the enemy AI spends developing their cities. If a map fight will cost you more than 20% of your army and you're not guaranteed to win, don't fight it. Retreat and find a different objective.
