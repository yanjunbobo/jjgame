HoMM Olden Era faction tier list. Castle, Inferno, Necropolis ranked. Best starting faction guide. May 2026.

⚔️ All Factions Ranked 2026

Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era features 8 distinct factions, each with unique heroes, units, and abilities. This guide ranks all factions for both single-player campaign and multiplayer balance in 2026.

S-Tier Factions

Castle (Might): The most balanced faction with exceptional early-game heroes. The Paladin line provides unmatched healing output while the Archangel is the single strongest unit in the game. Sir Roland's leadership aura benefits every army composition. The weakness is the expensive high-tier units that delay mid-game armies.

Inferno (Fire): The aggressive powerhouse faction. Fire magic dominates early and mid-game with devastating area spells. The Demon Prince is the fastest hero developer while the Efreet Sultan provides unmatched air control. Weakness is the vulnerability to ranged-heavy compositions.

A-Tier Factions

Necropolis (Death): The premier turtle faction. Death Knights provide the highest value-per-cost ratio in undead armies. The Vampire Lord is the most efficient early-game unit. The weakness is the slow movement speed limiting map control.

Stronghold (Might): The best rushing faction. Behemoths dominate melee combat while the Raid Captain's speed bonus enables aggressive openers. The weakness is the lack of ranged options leaving armies vulnerable during approach.

Rampart (Nature): The most versatile faction. The Dendroid Soldier provides unmatched defensive anchoring. The Unicorn's magic resistance makes them valuable in any army. The weakness is the inconsistent hero power curve.

Best Starting Factions

For Beginners: Castle provides the most forgiving gameplay with strong heroes and balanced units. The Paladin healing enables aggressive strategies while the tanky units forgive positioning mistakes.

For Aggressive Players: Inferno's fast hero development enables early-game domination. The Demon Prince's spell power dominates the first 3 weeks before opponents stabilize.

For Defensive Players: Necropolis's undead economy sustains longer games. The Death Knights' morale immunity makes them reliable in prolonged sieges.
