⚔️ Combat Mechanics & Army Composition

Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era's combat system rewards army composition and positioning. This guide covers combat mechanics, army building principles, and advanced tactics for decisive victories in 2026.

Core Combat Mechanics

The combat system uses a hex-based battlefield with initiative-based turn order. Each unit has movement points, attack range, and a unique ability. The key is maximizing favorable matchups through positioning before engaging.

Speed determines turn order. Faster units act first, enabling them to control engagement timing. The Speed skill from heroes is the most impactful ability in combat—always prioritize it in hero development.

Terrain affects combat. Hill terrain provides ranged accuracy bonus. Forest terrain provides defense bonus. Water terrain slows movement. Position your army on favorable terrain before committing to fights.

Army Composition Principles

Tank + Damage + Support: Every effective army has a front-line tank to absorb damage, damage dealers to eliminate threats, and support units to provide buffs or debuffs. The specific unit types vary by faction but the principle is universal.

Stack Sizing: Larger stacks are more efficient than smaller stacks due to the damage formula. However, splitting into two smaller stacks provides insurance against area damage. The balance depends on the enemy's ability to deal area damage.

Synergy Units: Units with complementary abilities provide more value than similar units. A ranged unit covering a tank beats two tanks without ranged support. Always consider how units assist each other.

Advanced Tactics

Kiting: Faster units can attack and retreat, dealing damage without retaliation. Use this against slower enemies with powerful melee attacks. The Skeleton Archer's ranged attack enables this tactic from early game.

Retreating: Sometimes the correct decision is to retreat and fight on better terms. The "tactical retreat" lets you reset positioning while the enemy advances into unfavorable terrain. A controlled retreat beats a disastrous all-in.

Spell Timing: Buff spells should be cast before engagement for maximum effect. Debuff spells should be cast when enemies commit to a position they cannot escape. Cleanse removes debuffs—save enough mana for emergency cleansing.
