Diablo 4 — Season 8 Complete Guide

Why Spiritborn dominates the meta, the resistance fix that stops Torment deaths, class builds that actually work, and the Pit strategy that separates Tier 80 from Tier 120 pushing.

Class Tier List — Season 8 2026

Based on Torment difficulty clear rates, Pit push depth, and solo endgame performance across top 100 leaderboard data.

S-Tier: The Meta Kings

Spiritborn — The most broken class in Diablo 4's history. The convergence of the Eagle Spirit's cone attacks with the Mirror of the Mountain passive creates damage amplification that scales geometrically in group content. When you move in a specific direction during a cone attack while an ally is also damaging the same group, the Mirror of the Mountain passive triggers its full 300% bonus. A Spiritborn at 800 item power with this interaction outperforms a non-Spiritborn at 1,200 item power in group content. For solo, the Quartz Spirit's pillar mechanics create permanent uptime on damage boosts through the Vagary skill rotation.

Necromancer — The solo Pit king. Golem builds with the Iron Blood passive give near-invulnerability in sustained fights. The Corpse tendril + Bone Storm rotation clears dense mob groups without requiring positioning. In the Pit, where elite density determines clear speed, the Necromancer's corpse generation from every kill creates a self-sustaining damage and mobility loop. The Shadow Army build pushes to Tier 100+ through pure minion damage scaling.

A-Tier: Strong Solo and Group

Barbarian — The Whirlwind HotA build is back and better than ever after Season 8's weapon scaling changes. The key advantage: no cooldown management. Whirlwind is always available, meaning you can sustain damage indefinitely as long as you have Fury. The ground slam + Kick them while they're down creates stun-locked elite packs that can't fight back. In groups, the Barbarian's shouts provide the best party buff in the game — 25% damage reduction and 30% damage boost for 8 seconds from the War Cry variant.

Sorcerer — The Ball Lightning chain lightning build is the most mechanically simple S-tier equivalent in the game. The projectile chains to 3 targets, the damage scales with attack speed, and the skill has a generous AoE radius that doesn't require precise aiming. The Frozen orb build is the counter for high-health single targets — the stacking slow and explosion damage reward patience over aggression.

Druid — The Werebear Pulverize build deals the highest single-target burst in the game after the Season 8 Earth Spike interaction was fixed. The key mechanic: if you hit a stunned enemy with Pulverize, the skill deals 400% extra damage as a bonus. With a Grizzly Rush stun build, you can keep bosses permanently stunned while dealing massive burst damage. The Hurricane + Tornado rotation for trash clearing is the most efficient density clear in the game.

B-Tier: Viable With Right Build

Rogue — The Flurry Arrow build is underrated for single-target boss damage. With the 300% melee damage node in the Aldric's Advocate passive tree, Flurry deals more damage per second than most class's ultimate abilities. The Shadows Wasps poison build is the fastest Pit pusher for Rogues but requires specific tempers and paragon board nodes to function.

Demon Hunter — The Barrage multishot build is back after the Season 8 changes to Arrow Storm. The fundamental problem: it requires two full rotation cycles to deal with tough elites, and the mobility to reposition between shots is lower than ranged competitors. Good in groups where your Ultimate provides the AoE; mediocre in solo content where mobility determines survival.

Spiritborn In-Depth: The S-Tier Build

The Spiritborn isn't just the best class — it fundamentally breaks how group content scales. Here's why.

Core Mechanic: Convergence + Mirror of the Mountain

The Mirror of the Mountain passive activates when you move in a specific direction (toward the cursor) during a cone attack. The Convergence legendary aspect multiplies this effect when multiple enemies are in the cone. The math: base cone damage × 1.5 (Mirror) × 2.0 (Convergence with 3+ enemies) = 3x damage on groups. In a 4-player party where all members are dealing damage to the same group, the Spiritborn's cone attacks become one of the highest burst abilities in the game.

The rotation: Vagary (Eagle Spirit) → Pestilence (the moving cone) → hold movement toward cursor during cone → Pestilence off cooldown → repeat. The key: your movement direction during Pestilence must be consistent. Players who move randomly during cone attacks get 50% less value from Mirror of the Mountain.

Eagle vs. Quartz: Group vs. Solo

For group content: always Eagle Spirit. The cone attacks hit multiple targets, and the Mirror of the Mountain interaction scales with ally damage. In a coordinated group, the Spiritborn's AoE output is unmatched.

For solo content: Quartz Spirit with the Pillar mechanics. The Stalagmite damage over time stacks with itself, and the bonus damage from moving between pillars gives you a 200% damage multiplier on single targets. The solo build is slower and more mechanically demanding but pushes deeper into the Pit than Eagle builds.

The Torment Resistance Fix

Most players hitting Torment difficulties for the first time die not because their build is weak — because their resistance distribution is wrong. Here's the math.

Torment 1 scales enemy damage to 2x Capstone levels. Torment 4 scales to 4x. If you enter Torment 4 with 50% Cold Resistance, every Cold-attacking enemy is effectively dealing double damage to you compared to a player at 70% Cold Resistance. At Torment 4, every resistance point above 70% reduces incoming damage by 2.5% per point. A player at 85% all-resistance takes 37.5% less damage than a player at 70% across all types.

The fix: before pushing any Torment tier, cap all elemental resistances at 70% minimum using the Potion of Title dump method. Buy resist all scrolls from the alchemist, use Potion of Iron Skin for the +100 resistance buff, check your resistances with the character screen. If any resistance is below 70%, stop pushing damage and fix the resistance gap first. My death rate dropped from 8 per run to 1 after making this change.

The Pit: Optimal Farming Strategy

The Pit is Diablo 4's infinite scaling endgame. Understanding how it works is essential for efficient progression.

How Pit Scaling Works

Every Pit level increases enemy health by 12% and damage by 8%. At Pit level 100, enemies have approximately 3,500x the health of a normal dungeon and deal damage that one-shots through most defensive builds. The goal isn't to kill everything — it's to kill the required number of enemies within the time limit.

The Skip Strategy: Run Past White Mobs

Top Pit players don't fight every mob. White (normal) mobs drop minimal XP and no additional progress toward the next level. Elites (blue) and Champions (yellow) are what matter. The optimal strategy: run past white mobs, kill only elites, burn the boss. A Spiritborn running through density can reach the boss in 60 seconds and clear the boss in 30 — total 90-second runs are possible at high tiers.

When to fight white mobs: when you need health globe generation for lifesteal builds, or when the elite density is so low that ignoring white mobs would leave you below the kill quota. In most Pit runs, fighting white mobs is a net negative to clear time.

The Boss Kill Order

Pit bosses are determined by the final dungeon layout. The fastest bosses: Ashava (predictable patterns, small arena), Wandering Death (high health but low damage). The slowest bosses: Diablo (long phases, teleportation), Duriel (move-or-die ice puddles require specific positioning).

If you get a bad boss, skip the boss and farm the previous level for speed. The XP per minute is higher at a lower Pit level with a fast boss than at a higher level with a slow boss.

Season Mechanic: Infernal Hordes

Season 8 introduced Infernal Hordes — a wave-based survival mode with increasingly difficult enemy compositions. Here's how to maximize rewards.

The wave structure: 5 waves of enemies, increasing in difficulty. Wave 3 and 5 contain the boss-tier enemies. The bonus objective for maximum rewards requires completing the horde without dying and with under 3 minutes elapsed.

The loot explosion: Completing an Infernal Horde at the highest difficulty tier drops 3-5 legendary items and a guaranteed Aspect extraction. The key: Aspects extracted from high-tier Horde runs are at item power 925 — higher than any dungeon drop. Prioritize running Hordes over dungeon grinding in the first week of each season.

The weapon swap trick: Use a high-damage weapon with a different aspect for Horde waves vs. Boss waves. The boss wave has a 2-minute timer — burst it down with a specialized bossing setup, then swap back for the next horde of adds.

Item Power and Stat Priorities

Weapon: Attack Speed > Critical Chance > Damage

For most builds, attack speed is the primary weapon stat because it scales damage linearly with no downside. A 10% attack speed increase means 10% more damage per second indefinitely. Critical chance is second because it determines your burst window — most burst rotations are built around critical hit procs. Raw damage is tertiary because it doesn't scale with attack speed or critical multipliers.

The exception: DoT (damage over time) builds — Poison, Burn, and Shadow — where tick rate matters more than attack speed. For those builds, prioritize skill-specific damage percentages and duration.

Armor: All-Resist > Armor > Life %

All-resist gives multiplicative damage reduction across all damage types simultaneously. Armor gives linear damage reduction against physical attacks only. In Torment difficulties where elemental damage dominates, all-resist provides 2-3x the effective survivability of the equivalent armor value. Life percentage is a trap stat — it increases your health pool but doesn't reduce incoming damage. Get your resistances to 70%+ before investing in life percentage rolls.

Season 8 New Player Guide

First 10 hours: Follow the campaign on World Tier 1. Don't worry about builds, aspects, or item optimization — just complete the campaign and unlock World Tier 2. The campaign teaches you the mechanics and gives you your first legendary powers.

Hours 10-30: World Tier 2, then 3 as soon as you have a full legendary loadout. Start collecting aspects from the Codex of Power — these are the permanent unlocks that carry across seasons. Prioritize the damage multiplier aspects for your chosen class.

Hours 30-50: World Tier 4 (Torment 1). This is where builds start to matter. Pick a meta build for your class, copy the paragon board layout from a guide, and start optimizing your seasonal powers. The Pit unlocks at Torment 1 and is the most efficient endgame activity for progression.

Hour 50+: Push Pit levels systematically. Each Pit level gives better XP and more gold. When you can clear Pit 60 in under 3 minutes, start pushing to your ceiling. The ceiling is the level where you can consistently clear without dying — dying resets your progress and costs more time than just running a lower level.
