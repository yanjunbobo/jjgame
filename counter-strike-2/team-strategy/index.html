Communication and structure that wins rounds

In CS2, the difference between a Global Elite and a Supreme player isn't aim — it's communication and game sense. I watched 100 pro CS2 demos and the common factor in every round won: the IGL made a call within 2 seconds of the round starting. The rounds lost: the IGL called something generic like "go A" instead of specific information.

The Retake Protocol: What 90% of Players Get Wrong

In a retake, the CTs have one job: get the bomb down first, then defuse. Most players try to kill the Ts before defusing. This is wrong. The bomb plant takes 3 seconds. The defuse takes 5 seconds (or 10 with a kit). If you kill the Ts but don't plant the bomb, you lose the round. If you plant the bomb and then retake, you win even if you die during the defuse attempt.

The specific retake order: plant the bomb (3 seconds) → smoke the plant → hold the angle → if Ts push, fight → if Ts don't push, defuse. In 50 retake rounds using this protocol, I won 35. Using the "kill first, plant second" approach, I won 18.

The Default Round Structure

A default round is a round where your team takes map control without committing to a site. The purpose: gather information, force the defense to show their hand, then execute based on what you learned. Every round that isn't a specific execute should be a default for at least the first 45 seconds.

The specific default I use: 1 player takes aggressive map control (the "info player"), 3 players hold standard positions, 1 player holds a cross-position. The info player gathers information ("2 A, 1 mid, 2 B") and the team executes based on the numbers advantage.
