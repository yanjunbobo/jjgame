The platform fighter that rewards execution

Rivals of Aether is the platform fighter for players who want a genuine competitive alternative to Smash. The roster is smaller (15 characters), the netcode is excellent, and the skill ceiling is as high as Melee. Here's what 200 hours taught me.

The Character Tier List (Based on 200+ Ranked Matches)

S-Tier: Orcane (the best combo character — his down-air combo chains into itself infinitely at low percentages, meaning any hit at 0% can lead to a kill at 80%), Zetterburn (the best beginner character — simple kit, high damage, easy kills with the fireball confirms).

A-Tield: Forsburn (the best trap character — his smoke clone forces opponents into bad positions, and his down-special one-shots at 120%), Ranno (the best spacing character — his projectiles are the best in the game for controlling space).

The key insight about Rivals tier lists: any character can win at any level. The tier list matters at the highest level of play. For 95% of players, the best character is the one you can execute consistently.

The Wall Jump Tech That Separates Platinum from Diamond

The most important technical skill in Rivals: wall jump buffering. If you press jump within 5 frames of touching a wall, you automatically wall jump. This sounds simple — it enables complex movement patterns. The specific combo: fast-fall → wall jump → fast-fall → wall jump = infinite vertical recovery that covers 80% of the stage. In my first 100 hours, I didn't know how to do this. After learning it, my recovery win rate went from 40% to 80%.

Why Zetterburn Is the Best Starter

Zetterburn teaches the fundamentals without bad habits. His fireball confirms (fireball hit → dash attack) teach the concept of "punish windows." His down-special (the spark) teaches positioning. His up-special (the recovery) teaches the importance of resource management. After 50 hours with Zetterburn, I switched to Orcane and immediately understood why Zetterburn is the bootcamp — it gave me the fundamentals that transferred.
