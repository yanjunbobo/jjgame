DI Guide — How to Escape Combos and Survive Longer

DI (Directional Influence) in Rivals of Aether is the single most impactful defensive skill. Players who DI correctly survive 30-40% longer per stock. Here's the complete breakdown of how DI works and when to use each direction.

📊 DI System Explained

How DI works in Rivals:

When hit, you hold the control stick in ANY direction. The direction you hold adds 10-15% knockback deviation from the original hit's trajectory. At low percent, this can mean the difference between a 3-hit combo and a 7-hit combo. At high percent (above 80%), good DI determines whether you die or survive to 120%.

The Golden Rule:

DI AWAY from the attacker during combos. DI TOWARD the stage during kill setups.

This sounds contradictory but it's not: during a combo, the attacker wants to send you offstage. DI away = you stay in the combo longer but you stay alive. During a kill setup (like a backthrow at 100%), DI toward the blast zone is useless — DI toward the nearest platform or stage edge to survive longer.

⚠️ Character-Specific DI Notes

Against Zetterburn (Fire)
His up-smash at 80%+ kills at center stage. DI down-forward (toward the nearest side) to survive until 100%+. His kill percent at ledge is 60% — if you're at 50% and near ledge, you're already in kill range.
Against Orcane (Water)
His down-throw into fair is a true combo at 0-60%. The only escape is DI out of the throw before he can follow up. If you DI forward (away from him) during the throw animation, you'll escape the follow-up window.
Against Ranno (Poison)
His combo game is the best in the cast. Once he gets a grab at low percent, assume the combo will deal 40%+ before you can act. Don't DI away — DI toward him to reduce the knockback and potentially interrupt his next hit.
