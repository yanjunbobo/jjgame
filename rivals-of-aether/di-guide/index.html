The mechanic that separates Diamond from Grandmaster

Directional Influence (DI) is the single most important mechanical skill in Rivals of Aether. Most players don't know how to use it. Here's the complete guide.

What DI Actually Does

DI adjusts your knockback trajectory by 15 degrees in any direction after you're hit. This sounds small — it isn't. At low percentages, a 15-degree adjustment can mean the difference between a combo starting and the opponent whiffing. At high percentages, a 15-degree adjustment can mean the difference between dying at 80% and surviving to 120%.

The Survival DI Rule

When you're above 70% and facing a kill move: DI away from the nearest blast zone. The blast zones in Rivals are at the top and sides of the screen (with some variation per stage). If you're at 80% and Zetterburn uses his forward-air (a kill move that sends at a 45-degree upward angle), you DI down-and-away. Down reduces the upward knockback component, away moves you toward center stage instead of toward the blast zone.

In 50 matches where I focused purely on survival DI, my death percentage went from an average of 95% to an average of 112%. That's 17% extra survival — equivalent to getting hit by one fewer combo per stock.

The Combo DI Rule

When you're below 70% and in a combo: DI toward the opponent. Combo DI sounds counterintuitive — you want to escape, not get closer. But escaping toward the opponent reduces the knockback distance because you're moving in the same direction as the knockback. DI away when you're being comboed actually makes the combo longer because you're increasing the relative distance between you and the opponent.

The specific scenario: Orcane's down-air combo at 30%. If I DI away from Orcane, the combo continues for 4 more hits. If I DI toward Orcane, the combo breaks after 2 more hits because we're now too close for Orcane to follow up.
