Movement Guide — Wavedash, Waveland & Competitive Movement Explained

Movement in Rivals of Aether is what separates casual from competitive play. Unlike Smash Bros, Rivals has no L-cancelling — the skill ceiling is purely in movement mechanics. Here's the breakdown of every movement technique and when to use it.

🎯 Core Movement Techniques

Wavedash — The Most Important Technique
How: Jump, then immediately air dodge diagonally into the ground. Result: You slide along the ground while actionable. This is your primary repositioning tool. The slide distance depends on your character's weight — lighter characters slide further. Practice: Jump + dodge down-diagonal as close to the ground as possible. The timing window is 5 frames.
Waveland — Extension of Wavedash
How: Air dodge into the ground at an angle that's NOT down-diagonal. Instead of sliding, you land and immediately have access to all ground options. This is the "safe" version of wavedash — used when you need to shield or grab immediately after repositioning.
Fast Fall — Air Speed Control
How: Hold down while in the air. Fast fall increases your downward speed by 2x. Essential for: combo extensions (catching people who DI out), approaching from above, and mixed approaches. Every top player uses fast fall 5+ times per stock.
Parry — The Defensive Pillar
How: Tap shield right before an attack lands. Result: The attacker staggers for 15 frames, you get a guaranteed punish. In high-level Rivals, parrying is how you convert from defense to kill. The timing window is 7 frames — tighter than Smash but consistent once you learn the feel.

⚠️ The 5 Movement Mistakes

Mistake 1: Only Wavedashing Forward
Predictable movement loses games. If you always wavedash toward your opponent, they'll shield and punish. Mix: wavedash forward, wavedash back (retreat), wavedash in place then attack. The goal is to make your movement unreadable.
Mistake 2: Not Using Jump to Escape Pressure
In Rivals, you have 2 jumps. Most beginners use jump to approach but never use it to escape. If you're in pressure, jump out. Your second jump is an escape tool — holding onto it for "the right moment" usually means you die before using it.
Mistake 3: Overusing Air Dodges
Air dodge has 30 frames of end lag in Rivals (more than Smash). If you air dodge to escape a combo, the opponent can read it and punish on landing. Use air dodge sparingly — it's a movement tool, not a panic button.
Mistake 4: Not Parrying in Neutral
Parry is not just for punish — it's a neutral tool. At high level, players parry approach aerials to gain stage control. If you never parry, you're giving up free stage positioning every time the opponent approaches.
Mistake 5: Wavedashing Into Shield
Wavedashing toward a shielding opponent = free grab for them. Always wavedash at an angle that puts you OFF the opponent's shield. If you want to approach with wavedash, wavedash just past them so you can shield-grab their punish attempt.
