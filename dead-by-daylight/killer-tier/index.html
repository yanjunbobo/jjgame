Killer Tier List — S-Tier Killers & How to Play Them | 2026

Dead by Daylight has 35+ killers and every one is viable in the right hands. But the meta shifts with every patch, and some killers dominate at every MMR bracket while others require specific skill levels to shine. Here's the definitive 2026 tier list.

📊 Killer Tier List (May 2026)

S-Tier: The Blight, The Nurse, The Spirit
The Blight: The most consistent S-tier. His dash attack covers massive distance and bounces off walls for creative plays. When a survivor tries to chain a safe loop, The Blight bounces around the obstacle and hits them. Skill floor: high. Skill ceiling: nearly unlimited. A Blight who hits every bounce = 4K at any MMR.
A-Tier: The Artist, The Onryō, The Legion, The Knight
The Artist: Corvid connections lets you tag survivors and send birds to interrupt loops. The Onryō (Sadako): Condemned mechanic forces survivors to manage TVs or lose. The Legion: Fast map pressure with Feral Frenzy. The Knight: His guards can hunt survivors while he patrols gens.
B-Tier: The Trapper, The Wraith, The Hillbilly, The Hag, The Shape
These killers are M1-based (basic attack) with no special mobility. They rely on mind games and map knowledge. The Shape (Myers) gets stronger as the match progresses — by endgame he's effectively S-tier. The Trapper requires setup before the match starts, making him weak in early chase.

⚠️ The 5 Killer Mistakes

Mistake 1: Tunneling (Focusing One Survivor)
Tunneling is effective but boring and toxic. In solo queue, it's your choice. But at high MMR (Red rank), survivors run anti-tunnel perks (Decisive Strike, BT, Off the Record) that make tunneling extremely inefficient. A good rule: if you've hooked someone twice and they're still in the game, they're getting hard-camped. Focus others.
Mistake 2: Breaking Pallets Immediately
Breaking a pallet takes 2.33 seconds. Survivors want you to break pallets because it gives their team time to do gens. Instead: stand at the pallet, fake a hit, wait for the survivor to drop the pallet and run (faster than breaking). When they drop and run, you're already in the chase again. Break pallets only when you're being looped by multiple survivors simultaneously.
Mistake 3: Not Patrolling Gens
The average survivor completes a gen in 80 seconds. The average killer finds the survivor in 15 seconds. That means you have a 65-second window per gen to get a down. If a gen is being worked and you can't find the survivor, assume they're on a gen. Patrol gens in the last 30 seconds of any chase — if you lose the survivor, check the nearest active gen.
Mistake 4: Not Using the Panic Button at the Right Time
Every killer has a special power (except M1 killers). The mistake is using it too early in the chase when you could use your normal attack. Your power should be used when: the survivor is at a safe loop, you need to catch up, or you need to hit through a pallet. Using your power at the start of every chase = wasting it.
Mistake 5: Letting Survivors Dictate the Loop
In a loop, the survivor is always one step ahead because they're running away. To break the loop: predict where they'll go, take the shortcut, and cut them off. This is called "taking the outside" (going around the outside of the loop while they go inside). If you take the outside correctly, they have nowhere to go but into you.
