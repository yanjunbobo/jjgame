The stealth system and consistent escape mechanics

Survivor Escape Guide — Stealth System & Consistent 4-Man Escapes | 2026

Dead by Daylight survivor gameplay is 70% decision-making and 30% mechanical skill. The difference between a 0-escape survivor and a 4-escape survivor isn't reaction time — it's understanding the stealth system, killer detection ranges, and gen timing. Here's the complete framework.

📊 The Detection System Explained

Killer detection has 3 tiers:

  1. No detection (24+ meters): The killer cannot see or hear you. You're completely safe. Stop moving if you hear the terror radius — standing still makes zero sound.
  2. Audio cue (8-24 meters): The killer hears your footsteps, breathing, and repair sounds. They know someone is nearby but don't know exactly where. Stop moving to become undetectable.
  3. Visual detection (8 meters or less): The killer sees you directly. At this point, you've already been spotted — start running, not hiding.

The most important skill: hearing the terror radius and stopping 1 second before entering the 24-meter zone. If you hear the killer and you stop immediately, you have a 90% chance of remaining undetected.

⚠️ The 5 Survivor Mistakes

Mistake 1: Doing Gens in the Open
Gens have a sound cue that travels 16 meters. If you're doing a gen and the killer is anywhere within 16 meters, they hear it. The rule: if you hear the killer's terror radius while on a gen, stop and look around. If you hear the killer's TR and see no one, they're either right around the corner or using a undetectable perk (Silent Hill, Iron Maiden). Always have an escape route in mind before starting a gen.
Mistake 2: Always Going for the Save
In solo queue, going for the save when the killer is camping = suicide. A hook camp grants you 0 points, costs your gen progress, and often results in double hook (both you and the hooked survivor die). The correct play when someone is hard-camped: do gens. 3 completed gens > 1 saved teammate who dies on next hook anyway.
Mistake 3: Using Dead Hard at the Wrong Time
Dead Hard gives you 0.5 seconds of i-frames (invincibility frames) plus a speed boost. Most survivors use it too early in the chase (giving the killer information) or too late (they get hit anyway). The correct use: Dead Hard as a PANIC button, not a PLANNED button. When you HEAR the killer's m2 (Mori animation), Dead Hard through it.
Mistake 4: Not Knowing When to Throw the Pallets
Throwing a pallet = the pallet is gone (unless you're running Plunderer's Instinct). The rule: if the killer is right on top of you and you need 0.5 seconds to create distance, throw the pallet. If you're at a strong loop and the killer is 5 meters away, DON'T throw — run the loop instead. Every loop you run = pallet saved = future chase survival.
