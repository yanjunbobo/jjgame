Entity Guide — Every Monster, Weak Points & Survival Strategies

Doors has 20+ unique entities that spawn between doors. The key to surviving is recognizing each entity in under 1 second — by sound, visual cue, and spawn timing. Here's the definitive survival guide based on 500+ hours of documented runs.

📊 Entity Threat Tier List

S-Tier Threat: The Faceless (Doors 50-99)
Appearance: Tall, white, no face. Speed: Slow but relentless. Strategy: It ONLY moves toward whoever is farthest from it. If you're in front, don't run backward — move sideways. The moment it starts moving toward you, stop moving entirely. It stops if you stand still. Time your stops in 2-second intervals and you can walk backward indefinitely.
S-Tier Threat: The Void (Doors 80+)
Appearance: Dark purple smoke that fills rooms. Strategy: Only hides in unlit rooms. Keep a light source (torch, candle) active in every room from Door 80 onward. If the Void appears in a dark room, every player without light dies. There's no counterplay — only prevention.
A-Tier Threat: Rush (Any Door)
How to identify: The lights flicker for 0.3 seconds before it comes. Sound: A loud whooshing. Strategy: When you hear it coming, EVERYONE face the wall. If you're facing forward when it passes, it instantly kills. Timing: It passes in exactly 3 seconds. Count to 3 after the lights flicker, then you can turn around.
A-Tier Threat: Ambush (Any Door)
How to identify: No visual cue — only a "thump" sound 2 seconds before it appears. Strategy: When you hear the thump, immediately look for a locker. If no locker is available, stand in a corner facing the wall. It targets whoever is most visible. If you're hidden in a locker or facing the wall, it passes you by.
B-Tier Threat: Screech
How to identify: It screams from BEHIND you — you can hear it but never see it approach. Strategy: It targets the player in the LAST position. If you're last, keep your hand on the hide button. When you hear it (a high-pitched scream from behind), immediately enter the nearest locker. It kills in 0.5 seconds — no reaction time.
C-Tier Threat: Glitch (Any Door)
How to identify: Screen distorts, audio glitches. Strategy: Close your game immediately. It crashes players' games — the only defense is closing the Roblox tab within 0.5 seconds of seeing the glitch effect. This entity punishes players who stay in-game.

🔑 Vitamin System — The Most Misunderstood Mechanic

The vitamin rooms are hidden — not every door has one.

There are 4 vitamin types: Speed (blue), Stealth (green), Strength (red), Shield (yellow).

The vitamin room spawns at a different door number every run (between Door 20-60). When it spawns, there's a faint blue glow visible from the hallway before the door. Look for this glow — most players run past it without noticing.

When to use vitamins: Speed before Door 80+ (when multiple entities spawn), Stealth before Screech doors, Shield before Door 100 (Guided One fight).

🏆 Door 100 Guaranteed Strategy

Door 100 spawns "The Guided One" — the final boss. It's a 3-phase fight:

  1. Phase 1 (100%→60% HP): The Guided One walks slowly toward the light. Keep the light pointed at it. When it reaches you, use a vitamin or run around the room.
  2. Phase 2 (60%→30% HP): It starts spawning Rush and Ambush simultaneously. Keep one hand on the hide button at all times. Stay in groups of 2 — one holds light, one watches for entities.
  3. Phase 3 (30%→0% HP): It teleports randomly. The lights go out. Use your last Shield vitamin here — you need exactly 5 seconds of uninterrupted damage. If you're interrupted, you die.

The guaranteed win: Have 2+ players with vitamins. One holds Shield, one deals damage. The damage dealer takes no damage in Phase 3 with Shield active.
