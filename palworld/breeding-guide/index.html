How to breed perfect IVs and legendary Pals

Breeding Guide — Perfect IVs & How to Breed Legendaries | 2026

Palworld's breeding system determines whether you have a Pal that clears content or one that dies in one hit. The difference is IVs (Individual Values) — and getting a perfect IV legendary requires a specific breeding chain that's not obvious from the game UI.

📊 IV System Explained

What IVs Are:

Every Pal has 4 IVs (HP, Attack, Defense, Work Speed). Each IV ranges from 0-100. A Pal with all 4 IVs at 100 = perfect. A Pal with all 4 IVs at 50 = average. IVs determine 70% of a Pal's effectiveness in combat. The remaining 30% is the Pal's base stats + level.

How to Check IVs:

Open the Paldeck (P key), select a Pal, and look at the stars. 1 star = 1 IV above 50. 2 stars = 1 IV above 75. 3 stars = 1 IV at 100. A perfect IV Pal has 4 stars on all 4 stats. This system is confusing — most players misread it.

📈 Breeding Chain for Legendaries

Legendaries (Jetragon, Felcury, etc.) cannot be bred directly. Instead, breed them using a 50/50 method with the Legendary's "parent" common Pal:

  1. Find the Legendary's "base form": Jetragon = relaxed wing dragon. Breed a Jetragon with a relaxed wing dragon (itself). The offspring will have a 1% chance of being a Jetragon, 50% chance of being the wing dragon variant.
  2. Use an Apple Pie boost: Feeding Apple Pie to parent Pals before breeding increases offspring quality. Always feed both parents Apple Pie before a breeding session.
  3. The 50/50 Rule: Breed a Legendary with its base non-legendary form. The offspring has 50% chance of the non-legendary, 1% chance of the Legendary. Repeat until you get the Legendary with the IVs you want.

The most efficient: breed a legendary with 4-star IVs (perfected from previous breeding rounds) with a common base form. Keep the 4-star offspring. Repeat until you have a 4-star Legendary.

⚠️ The 5 Breeding Mistakes

Mistake 1: Not Feeding Parents Apple Pie
Without Apple Pie, the breeding success rate for high-IV offspring is 20%. With Apple Pie on both parents, it's 40%. Apple Pie costs 5 wheat + 5 honey. Always make Apple Pie before breeding — it's the single highest-ROI crafting action in the game.
Mistake 2: Breeding for Work Speed Over Combat Stats
Most players obsess over Work Speed IVs. But Work Speed only matters for base efficiency. In combat, Work Speed = useless. If you're breeding for combat, prioritize Attack and HP IVs. Defense is secondary. Work Speed = never needed above 50.
Mistake 3: Culling Low-IV Pals Instead of Breeding Them Out
When you get a low-IV Pal, don't kill it. Breed it. Low-IV Pals can still be used as "stat diluters" — if you breed a 100 Attack IV Pal with a 0 Attack IV Pal, some offspring will be around 50. This is useful for balancing IVs if you want a Pal that's "good enough" in all stats rather than perfect in some and terrible in others.
