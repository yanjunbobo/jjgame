Optimal Pal assignments for maximum base efficiency

Best Base Pals — Optimal Work Skill Assignments | 2026

The base building system in Palworld is deceptively deep. Each Pal has specific work skills (gathering, watering, farming, etc.) and matching the right Pal to the right job is the difference between a base that produces 100 resources/hour and one that produces 1,000. Here's the definitive guide.

📊 The 4 Critical Work Skills

Farming (Kindling / Grain)
Optimal Pal: Foxparks (kindling) or Melp (grain). Foxparks produces 300% more kindling than any other Pal. Melp (Fire Pal) is the only Pal that produces grain at S-rank efficiency AND works in the farming spec. For late game: Lamball is the best all-around farmer due to high work speed + farming S-rank.
Mining (Mining / Handcraft)
Optimal Pal: Surfside (Water) or Quivern (Dragon). Surfside has S-rank mining and works at night without sick day penalties. Quivern has the highest raw mining output but has stamina issues. For early game: Jormuntide (Water) is S-rank mining AND can be used as a mount for water traversal.
Transporting (Moving / Drunk)
Optimal Pal: Wumpo (Electric) + Robonaix. Wumpo has the highest transport speed and a massive carrying capacity (50 units). The problem: Wumpo takes a "sick day" every 5 minutes. Robonaix (Robot) never takes sick days and has A-rank transport speed. Best combo: 2 Wumpos + 1 Robonaix = always active transport.
Generating Electricity (Electric)
Optimal Pal: Univolt (Electric) + Grizzbolt (Electric). Grizzbolt produces 150 electricity compared to Univolt's 100. But Grizzbolt needs Paldium to breed, so Univolt is your early-game workhorse. After mid-game (Level 30+), switch all electric stations to Grizzbolt.

⚠️ The 5 Base Building Mistakes

Mistake 1: Overcrowding Pals in the Base
Each Pal has a sanity threshold. When a base has too many Pals (over 15), sanity drops and work efficiency drops by 50%. Keep your base at 12-15 Pals maximum for optimal efficiency. The maximum base cap is 4 bases = 60 Pals total. Spread across 4 bases rather than cramming into 1.
Mistake 2: Not Assigning Pals to Specific Stations
Unassigned Pals default to "wandering" which wastes their work potential. Every Pal should be manually assigned to a specific station. The exception: breeding Pals should NOT be assigned to any station — breeding is their full-time job and assigning them reduces breeding success rate.
Mistake 3: Building Base in the Wrong Biome
Some Pals only work in specific biomes. Fire Pals (Foxparks, Pyrin) get sick day penalties if placed in snowy biomes. Ice Pals (Foxcicle) get sick day penalties in desert biomes. Build your base in a temperate grassland biome (the starting area is ideal) for the fewest sick day penalties.
Mistake 4: Ignoring Defense Pals
Base defense matters. An base with 0 combat Pals will lose Pals to world bosses (Alpha Pals) that spawn nearby. Keep at least 2 Lyleen (Water Healer) + 1 Anubis (Electric DPS) for base defense. These are the only Pals that can be stationed AND fight effectively without being distracted by other tasks.
