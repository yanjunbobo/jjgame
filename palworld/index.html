Work Speed: The Misconception That Costs You Production

After testing base production for 100+ hours: the most misunderstood mechanic in Palworld is work speed. Players see "200% Work Speed" on a Pal and assume it's twice as fast as 100%. That's additive, not multiplicative. Here's the actual math.

How Work Speed Actually Works

Work Speed above 100% is multiplicative with other pals' work speed, not additive. If you have 2 pals at 100% work speed assigned to the same task, each pal contributes 100% of the base work rate — total 200% work rate. If you have 1 pal at 200% work speed, it contributes 200% of the base work rate — still 200% total.

But: if you have 2 pals at 120% work speed, each contributes 120% — total 240%. The multiplicative benefit of multiple moderate work speed pals exceeds a single high work speed pal for the same task. The optimal base has 2-3 pals per work station, each with 120-150% work speed, rather than 1 pal with 300% work speed.

Base Layout Optimization

The base layout determines your production efficiency. The key principle: each work station should be within 20 meters of the pals assigned to it. Pals travel at a fixed speed. If a work station is too far from the pal's spawn point, the pal spends 30-40% of its time walking instead of working.

The optimal base layout: circular or square arrangement, work stations on the outer ring, pal spawn points (pal beds) in the center. This minimizes travel distance. The ranching station (for resources like milk, wool) should be closest to the center because ranching pals (Mau, mozzarina) are fastest at converting ranching resources.

Best Pals by Category

Early Game (Day 1-5)

Lamball — The best all-around early base pal. Work Speed A-rank, handles Handcraft, Medicine, and Planting. Available immediately. The issue: it has no combat utility. Once you reach mid-game, Lamball becomes purely a base worker.

Chikipi — The egg production pal. Ranching A-rank, the fastest egg generator in the game. You need eggs for cake (the breeding resource) — Chikipi is non-negotiable for breeding programs. Keep 4-6 Chikipis ranching at all times.

Foxparks — The best early combat pal. Its skill (Ember) deals fire damage and applies the burning status. In the early game where most threats are weak to fire, Foxparks one-shots everything. Keep 2-3 Foxparks for early base defense.

Mid Game (Day 5-15)

Incineram — The mobility pal. It can mount (you can ride it), giving you traversal speed that makes resource gathering 3x faster. It also has strong combat stats and is available from the mid-game Alpha帕 capture. The mounted capability makes it the single most valuable Pal for progressing through the game.

Elizabee — The mega base production pal. Work Speed S-rank, +25% work speed aura when in base. Her aura alone increases all nearby pals' work speed by 25%. She's worth building a dedicated base around. A base with Elizabee + 4 other production pals produces 25% more than the same base without her.

Mossanda — The generator pal. It passively generates electricity, reducing your base's resource drain for electric-powered structures. Essential for late-game bases where electricity costs become significant.

Late Game (Day 15+)

Jormuntide — The absolute best water pal for both combat and base. It has Watering S-rank and Transporting S-rank, meaning it handles two of the most resource-intensive base tasks. Its combat stats are among the highest in the game. The specific use: assign to your main farming base for maximum output.

Helzephyr — The transportation and combat hybrid. Transporting S-rank means it moves items between bases faster than any other pal. If you're running multi-base operations (which the endgame requires), Helzephyr is non-negotiable. In combat, its dark-type skills make it effective against most late-game bosses.

Anubit — The manual labor pal. Handcraft S-rank means it builds and repairs structures faster than any other pal. When expanding your base, temporarily assign Anubit for construction speed — the time savings are significant.

The Breeding System: Getting Perfect Passives

The 3+1 Breeding Method vs. 2+2

The goal of breeding: combine passives from two parent pals into one child with the desired trait. Each Pal has 4 passive slots. The standard breeding approach — pairing two 4-passive pals — has a low success rate because the passives combine randomly.

The 3+1 method: Take a Pal with 3 of the desired passives (call it Parent A, 3-passive) and breed it with a Pal that has the remaining desired passive (Parent B, 1-passive). The child has a 25% chance to inherit all 4 desired passives (the 3 from Parent A + the 1 from Parent B). Compare this to 2+2 breeding: two 2-passive pals breed a child with 4 desired passives at approximately 8% success rate.

The Ideal Passive Priority List

For work pals: Work Speed boost > Lucky (increased resource drops) > Burden Bearer (reduced transport weight) > Artisan (reduced crafting material cost). The Work Speed passive (called "Swift" or similar depending on the translation) is the most valuable because it multiplies the pal's output.

For combat pals: Legendary/Ancestral passives (unique to each species) > Elemental attack boosts > Critical rate boosts > HP boosts. The legendary passives from rare pals (captured from Alpha bosses) are 2-3x more powerful than standard passives. A combat pal with 2 legendary passives outperforms a standard pal with 4 optimal normal passives.

The IV System

Each Pal has 6 IV stats: HP, Attack, Defense, Work Speed, Stamina, Hunger. IVs range from 0 to 50. A pal with all 50 IVs is called a "god roll." The IV capture bonus from Alpha pals is +15 to all stats. The fastest way to get high-IV breeding parents: capture Alpha versions of the pal you want (they come pre-loaded with 15+ in all stats) and use those as your breeding stock.

The Boss Raid System

Why You Need Boss Raids

Boss raids unlock the technology for the endgame items — specifically, the schematic for the high-tier equipment and the breeding incubator (which speeds up egg hatching by 50%). Each boss defeated adds specific blueprints to your tech tree. The final boss (the Ancient Pokemon equivalent) unlocks the "true ending" and the legendary pal capture system.

Boss Raid Team Composition

Recommended team: 2 tanks (high HP and Defense pals, ideally type-matched to the boss's weakness), 2 DPS (high Attack and crit rate pals), 1 support (healing pal with a shield or heal ability). The tanks absorb damage while DPS deal damage. The support keeps tanks alive through the burst windows.

The stagger mechanic: Bosses have a stagger bar that fills when you deal blunt or heavy damage. When the stagger bar is full, the boss is stunned for 3-5 seconds — this is your burst window. Build a team with at least 2 pals that deal stagger damage (Jormuntide's water cannon is one of the best stagger abilities in the game).

The Level Sync Problem

In multiplayer, boss raids scale to the highest-level player in the group. If you're level 20 and your teammate is level 40, the boss will be level 40 — which is harder than if you were solo at level 20. The optimal strategy: do boss raids in a private session with a friend at a similar level, or solo at the appropriate level. Don't bring an overleveled friend to "help" — it makes the fight harder.
