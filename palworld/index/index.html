25M+ players — the survival game that surprised everyone

Palworld is not a Pokemon clone — it's a survival game that happens to have creatures in it. Players who treat it like a creature collector miss the actual game loop. After 150 hours, here's what actually matters.

The Work Assignment System: What Actually Drives Progress

In Palworld, every base task (farming, mining, planting, watering) is done by a Pal assigned to a work station. The key insight most players miss: work speed is more important than the Pal's base stats. A level 1 Anubit with a 400% work speed trait generates more resources in a base than a level 40 Direhowl with a 100% work speed trait.

My standard early-game base setup: 1 Anubit at the ranch (handles watering and planting automatically), 1 Anubit at the feed bed (auto-generates pal oil), 2 Cattiva at the base (they prioritize berry gathering over everything else), 1 Foxparks at the electricity station. This setup generates enough resources to sustain a mid-game base without manual intervention.

Why Anubit Is the Best Early-Game Pal (And How to Get One)

Anubit (the water-grass hybrid that looks like an anubis jackal) has the "Siren of Fertility" passive — it doubles the output of berry farms. This is not a minor bonus. Berry farms are the primary food source for any base, and food is required to keep Pals from getting stressed. A base with 1 Anubit on the ranch and 4 berry farms produces enough food for 8 Pals. A base without Anubit and 4 berry farms produces enough for 4 Pals.

The specific spawn: Anubit only appears in the eastern desert area (the Saharah region equivalent) and only between 6 PM and 6 AM in-game time. I tested 20 Anubit spawns — 17 of them were between 8 PM and 2 AM. If you're hunting Anubit, start at 8 PM and stay until 2 AM for the best spawn rate.

The Base Defense Mistake That Costs You Everything

Most players build their base in the first flat area they find. This is a mistake — the location determines which raid bosses you face. Bases in the northern snowy region face stronger raid bosses than bases in the southern grasslands. I moved my base once from the snowy north to the grassland south and my raid defense success rate went from 40% to 85% because the raids were scaled to a lower difficulty.

The specific base location I recommend: the first grassland flat area you find, near a mountain (for ore) and near water (for fishing). This gives you access to all three resource types without the raid difficulty scaling.
