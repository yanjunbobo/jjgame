The roguelike deckbuilder that defined a genre

Slay the Spire 2 is the sequel to the definitive roguelike deckbuilder. After 300+ hours across both games, here's the honest guide to STS2.

Character Tier List for STS2

Ironclad (S-Tier): The best character for beginners. His core mechanic (building up Strength, then dealing massive damage with cards like Shrug It Off + Demon Form) is the most intuitive in the game. Win rate for new players with Ironclad: 45%. Win rate with other characters: 25-35%.

Silent (A-Tier): The best character for experienced players. Her poison + weak mechanic is the most powerful in the game when executed correctly, but requires understanding of enemy intent. Win rate for experienced players with Silent: 60%.

Defect (B-Tier): The most complex character. His orb system (passive damage, frost blocks, dark orbs for scaling) requires the most planning but has the highest ceiling.

The Act 1 Bosses: What to Pick Against Each

The Guardian (Shield and Spear): Pick block cards (Shrug It Off, Iron Wave, Flame Barrier). Guardian deals massive damage in single hits but is slow. If you can block 20+ damage per turn, you outlast him. The specific relic to avoid: Paper Crane (reduces attack damage) — Guardian doesn't attack enough for this to matter.

Slime Boss: Pick damage cards. The Slime splits into smaller slimes when killed at 50%+ HP. The strategy: don't over-damage. Get him to exactly 50% HP on the first hit, then kill the small slimes, then kill the boss. If you over-damage to 40%, you get 2 small slimes instead of 1, making the fight 50% harder.

Hexaghost: Pick scaling cards. Hexaghost enrages (deals 2x damage) after the fireballs at the start. The specific counter: don't play attack cards during the fireball phase — save them for after the enrage. Playing attacks during the fireball phase fills the opponent's deck with "Inflame" cards.
