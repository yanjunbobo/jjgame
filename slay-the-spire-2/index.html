🃏 Card Tier List & Act 1 Strategy

Slay the Spire 2 brings new mechanics and rebalanced cards to the roguelike deckbuilder formula. This guide covers card tiers across all characters, Act 1 boss strategies, and common pitfalls to avoid in 2026.

Ironclad S-Tier Cards

Inflame: The best starter card in the game. +2 Strength per use with permanent duration. The card economy is unmatched—every draw cycle generates value. Always take in Act 1 unless you have perfect alternate scaling.

Hemokinesis: 30 damage for 2 energy. The self-damage is negligible at 6 HP. The damage-to-energy ratio is the best in Ironclad. Enables Act 1 boss kills with appropriate support.

Demon Form: 3 energy for +2 Strength per attack. The scaling is unmatched for long fights. The 3-turn setup is worth the investment against Act 1 bosses and beyond.

Silent S-Tier Cards

Adrenaline: 0 cost for +1 Energy and +1 Dexterity next turn. The card draws and energy generation enables broken combos. Always take Adrenaline regardless of deck state.

Blade Dance: 1 cost for 3 attacks for 4 damage each. The synergy with Dexterity is the core of Silent's aggressive strategies. The Wraith Form combo enables burst damage windows.

Dagger Spray: 1 cost for 8 damage to ALL enemies. The best AoE damage in Silent's kit. Handles hallway fights that would otherwise require multiple turns.

Act 1 Bosses

The Guardian: Requires block scaling or high burst. Inflame + Hemokinesis + Bash is the reliable kill setup. Avoid taking damage on turns 1-3 to preserve HP for the final phase.

The Hexaghost: Requires self-damage prevention. Piercing Wail at 2 copies handles the divide mechanic. The 6-damage self-burn needs Ignorance or self-healing to survive.
