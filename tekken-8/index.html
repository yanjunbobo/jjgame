📐 Frame Data — The 3 Numbers That Matter

Tekken 8 is a frame game. Understanding frames separates Green ranks from Blue ranks:

  • Startup frames: How many frames before the move hits. 10 frames = ~166ms. 15 frames = ~250ms. A jab is 10 frames — if you block a 15-frame move, you can jab (10 frames) and still hit them.
  • On-block frames: How many frames you're minus after the enemy blocks. -10 or worse = they can launch you. -9 to -6 = they can do a mid. -5 to 0 = they get a jab.
  • On-hit frames: How many frames you're minus after the enemy gets hit. Negative on hit = they can retaliate. Positive on hit = you're safe and keep pressuring.

The most important rule: if you're -10 on block, you MUST block. Any jab will punish you. If you're +6 on block, you can continue pressure.

⚠️ The 5 Mistakes That Keep You in Green Rank

Mistake 1: Not Using the 10-Frame Punish (you're leaving 200+ free damage per game)
Every character has a 10-frame jab (typically 1,1). When an enemy does a move that's -10 or more on block, you MUST jab punish. df1 strings from your character are usually 12-14 frames. In Green rank, players block only 60% of punishable moves — punish those and you win 15% more games.
Mistake 2: Whiff Punishment (you're not punishing whiffs)
The biggest gap between Green and Blue ranks: whiff punishment. When an enemy whiffs a move, there's a 10-20 frame window where they're vulnerable. Learn your character's 12-frame punish (usually df1 or f2) and use it on every whiff you see.
Mistake 3: Overusing Low Attacks (low pokes get CH launched)
In Green rank, players use too many unsafe lows. Every low poke is -12 or worse on block. If you're spamming db4 (a common low kick), you're getting launched every 4th time by a patient opponent.
Mistake 4: Not Labbing Matchups (you're walking in blind)
After every loss, lab the character that beat you for 10 minutes. Focus on: (1) their 10-frame punish, (2) their key mid-check tools, (3) their throw break. This alone moves most Green rank players to Yellow.
Mistake 5: Bad KBD (Korean Backdash) Mechanics
KBD is: b, n, b (tap back, neutral, tap back quickly). In Green rank, most players do it at 40% speed, making them move backwards but still punishable. Practice KBD until you can do 5 in a row without stopping. It's the foundation of defense.

🔥 Heat System — How to Use It Correctly

Tekken 8's Heat system replaces Rage Art. Here's the data-optimized usage:

  • Heat Burst: Push during opponent's pressure to break their rhythm. It's +8 on block — use it to create your own pressure window.
  • Heat Engagers: Each character has a unique Heat Engager (special move that starts Heat). Most are mid, safe on block, and lead to combos. Use them to start Heat and maintain pressure.
  • Heat Dash: After a Heat Engager combo, you can Heat Dash for additional mixup. The damage from Heat adds 20-30% to your combo.
  • Don't save Heat for Rage Art: Using Heat in rounds 1-2 deals 30-40% more total damage than saving it for a clutch round 3. Heat system rewards aggression.
