👊 Best Combos & Punish Guide

Tekken 8 combo execution separates intermediate from advanced players. This guide covers universal combo principles, character-specific optimal combos, and punish windows for the most common moves in 2026.

Universal Combo Principles

Every combo in Tekken 8 follows a consistent structure: launcher → filler → wall-carry → ender. The launcher determines maximum damage potential. Fillers maintain the combo while building wall carry. Enders determine whether the combo kills or resets to neutral.

The most important concept is combo route selection. Against standing opponents, use full combos. Against wall-splat situations, use high-damage wall combos. Against KND (knockdown) situations, use ground-hit combos or safe mid options.

Frame data is critical for combo selection. Characters with 10-frame punish tools can reach specific combos that 12-frame punish users cannot. Know your character's punish range before practicing combos.

Punishment Guide

10-Frame Punishment: Use your fastest mid launcher for crimes worth 10 damage. At -10, use your fastest mid to catch their pressure attempt. At -12, your df1 or equivalent becomes a punish.

12-Frame Punishment: The standard punish for most unsafe mid moves. Most df1 variants are 12-frames. This launches for a full combo in most situations.

15-Frame Punishment: The punish for very unsafe moves. Use when opponents throw out extremely negative moves expecting you'll hesitate.

Whiff Punishment: The most technically demanding punishment. Watch for the recovery frames of whiffed moves and capitalize with your longest range launcher.

Optimal Combos by Character

Devil Jin: df2 → electric → hellsweep → 4 → f,n,d,df2 → 1,2,1 → b1+2 → 1+2. This combo does 88 damage and wallsplats for excellent carry. The hellsweep ender provides a mixup on wake-up.

Bryan: df2 → ff2 → b1+2 → 1+2,1,2+4. This does 85 damage and is the most reliable Bryan combo. The b1+2 ender leaves the opponent at + frames.

King: ff1+2 → 1,2 → giant swing (1+2 break) → df1+2. The chain throw does 90+ damage. Practice the break sequence: 1+2 for giant swing, 1 for muscle buster, 2 for power bomb.

Steve: df2 → 1 → qcf1 → 1,2 → b1+2 → pfp → 1+2. This does 78 damage and leaves Steve at plus frames. The pfp stance enables continued pressure.

Wall Combo Optimization

At the wall, prioritize damage over carry. Use your character's wall-specific combo that maximizes hits at the wall. The wall stun duration from hits 1-3 determines maximum combo length before the wall bounce.

Wall bounce combos deal more total damage than wall splat combos. Know which of your moves causes wall bounce and incorporate them into wall combos.
