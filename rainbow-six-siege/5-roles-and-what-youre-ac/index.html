80M+ registered players — the most tactical FPS

Rainbow Six Siege is the only competitive FPS where the gunfight is only 30% of the game. The other 70% is information gathering, utility usage, and team coordination. After 800+ hours in Siege, here are the things I wish I knew in my first 100 hours.

The 5 Roles (And What You're Actually Supposed to Do)

Entry Fragger: First into site, takes the most risk. Your job is not to get kills — it's to gather information. If you die as entry but your team gets 2+ kills from that info, you did your job. Best agents: Jett (Dash), Raze (Satchel).

IGL: Calls the round, manages economy, makes mid-round calls. The specific IGL mistake I made in my first 200 hours: calling eco rounds when we had a 60% chance of winning with full buy. In Siege, the worst outcome is a 50/50 round you didn't commit to — you lost the equipment AND the round.

Lurker: Creates distraction and takes unexpected angles. If you're alive as lurker and the enemy team is rotating, you've already done your job. If you die and force the rotation, you did it even better.

Map-Specific Callouts That Win Rounds

Bank (Attacker): "One Lobby" = 1 enemy in lobby entrance. "One in Tellers, one on CCTV" = two specific callouts. Always use the shortest callout that uniquely identifies the location.

Border: The most valuable callout on Border is "Vending, East, West" for the armory area. Teams forget about the armory flanks. A single operator holding the armory flank can win 3+ rounds by themselves.

The Economy System: Why You Keep Losing After 3 Rounds

Siege has a loss bonus that caps at 4 rounds. The exploit: if you're on a 3-round loss streak, full buy on round 4 even if your team disagrees. The math: 4 losses + full buy round 4 = you can afford full buy on round 5 even if you lose round 4. If you eco on round 4 and lose, you can't afford full buy on round 5.

Based on 800+ hours of Siege across all ranks. Last updated: May 2026.
