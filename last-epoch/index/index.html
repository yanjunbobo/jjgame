5M+ players — the ARPG that learned from Diablo's mistakes

Last Epoch is the ARPG that Diablo 4 should have been. After 150 hours, here's the complete guide.

Class Tier List for Endgame (Based on 150+ Hours)

S-Tier: Forge Knight (the best tank — his Molten Shell ability absorbs 100% of damage for 3 seconds, and his Forge Fire passive reduces enemy damage by 20%), Spellblade (the best DPS — combines melee and spell damage for hybrid builds that deal 2x the damage of pure builds).

A-Tier: Marksman (the best single-target DPS — the Acolyte of Precision specialization gives +50% critical hit chance, making crit-focused builds devastating), Sorcerer (the best AOE — Frost Wave deals 500% damage to all enemies in a line).

Why Multiclassing Is the Key to Power

Last Epoch's multiclassing system lets you combine 2 specializations. The specific combination I use for the ultimate tank: Forge Knight (primary) + Paladin (secondary). This gives: Forge Knight's Molten Shell + Paladin's Divine Bastion (blocks 100% of damage from the next 3 hits). With this combination, I survived the level 80 endgame boss that 90% of players couldn't beat. The Paladin's healing skill (Righteous Palisade) combined with Forge Knight's armor reduction gives me 50% more effective HP than a pure Forge Knight.

The Endgame Loop: What to Do First

Last Epoch's endgame is based on the Monolith of Eternity (a timeline-based dungeon system). Each timeline has a specific boss and modifiers. The specific order to complete timelines: Start with the Era of the Universes (the tutorial timeline), then move to the Era of Legends (gives the best legendary drop rate), then cycle through the other timelines based on the loot you need. This order maximizes your power progression for the minimum time investment.
