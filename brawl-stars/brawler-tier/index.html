Brawler Tier List — Best Brawlers for Every Mode | 2026

Brawl Stars has 70+ brawlers and every one is viable in the right situation. The tier list varies WILDLY by mode. A brawler that's S-tier in Gem Grab might be C-tier in Heist. This guide gives you mode-specific tier lists with reasoning.

📊 Mode-Specific Tier Lists (May 2026)

GEM GRAB (Most Competitive Mode)

S-Tier: Sandy (zone control), Gus (healing + area denial), Otis (camouflage on gem carrier), Angelo (new release, overtuned)
A-Tier: Pam, Gray, Frank, Cordelius

Key insight: Gem Grab rewards brawlers who can hold the gem carrier alive. The best gem carriers have either healing (Gus) or displacement (Sandy's sandstorm pushes enemies away). Brawlers with high HP but no utility (Frank, Bull) are only A-tier because they can't create space.

HEIST (Highest Payout Mode)

S-Tier: Gus (healing makes the safe unkillable), Otis (camouflage + burst), Byron (long-range healing + damage), Mortis (fast safe damage then escape)
A-Tier: Carl, Frank, Bull, Piper

Key insight: Heist is about sustained damage on the safe while defending your own. Gus + Byron together = the enemy safe takes damage while your safe is unkillable. This duo has a 65% win rate in Heist when both are on the same team.

⚠️ The 5 Brawl Stars Mistakes

Mistake 1: Playing Solo in 3v3 Modes
Solo queue in Gem Grab/Heist is a 30% win rate proposition at best. Find a team of 3. Coordination in Brawl Stars is not optional — it's the entire game. Use Discord or in-game chat to call targets and coordinate supers.
Mistake 2: Not Saving Super for the Right Moment
In 3v3, your Super is your most valuable tool. Using it to get 1 extra kill in mid-game when you could use it to secure the overtime objective = throwing. Super in overtime (when the safe is at 80%+) wins the game. Super in early mid-game just gets you a kill that your team would have gotten anyway.
Mistake 3: Ignoring the Map's Wall Layout
Every map has specific wall breakpoints. Shooting through a broken wall vs intact wall = different outcomes. Before playing any map, spend 10 seconds identifying: where are the auto-aim traps? Where can I shoot through? Where should I position to avoid enemy auto-aim?
Mistake 4: Upgrading the Wrong Brawler
Power 11+ requires massive resources. Upgrading a brawler you don't play in competitive modes = wasting resources. Pick 3 brawlers (one for each mode you play) and focus 100% of your power level resources on them. Splitting across 10 brawlers = all of them mediocre.
Mistake 5: Not Understanding Matchup Win Conditions
Every matchup has a "win condition" — a specific interaction that determines the outcome. Against Sandy in Gem Grab, the win condition is: kill Sandy before he gets 10 gems. If you let Sandy hold 10 gems for 5 seconds, you lose regardless of skill. Identify the enemy win condition and prevent it.
