⚽ Career Mode Young Player Guide

FC 25 Career Mode requires strategic young player development to build championship teams. This guide covers youth scouting, development planning, and squad building strategies for long-term success.

Youth Scouting System

Scout Assignment: Send 3 scouts to regions with high youth rating. Argentina, Brazil, and Western Europe consistently produce 70+ potential players. Assign scouts for 90-day assignments for maximum discovery chance.

Youth Rating Thresholds: 80+ potential is world-class tier. These players become 90+ overall with proper development. 75-79 potential is good starter quality. 70-74 is rotation player ceiling. Below 70 should be sold immediately for profit.

Scout Quality: Invest in 4-5 star scouts. The quality directly impacts discovered player potential. Better scouts find better players at the same budget cost.

Development Planning

Position Switching: Train young players in secondary positions during early years. A winger trained as a CAM at age 18 can become a 90+ rated CAM by age 25. Position flexibility increases their eventual ceiling.

Playing Time Requirements: Players under 70 overall need 50%+ match time to develop. 70-79 overall needs 30%+ time. 80+ overall can maintain rating with minimal appearances. Balance youth development against winning matches.

Training Focus: Prioritize the player's primary attribute category in training. A striker needs +2 finishing per season minimum. Weak foot and skill moves development should be secondary to core attributes.

Squad Building Strategy

Team Chemistry: Build squads within continental and national clusters. South American players in European clubs require careful management to maintain morale. Prefer players from the same region for easier chemistry building.

Contract Management: Offer 5-year contracts to players under 24. This prevents contract expiration during peak value years. Use release clauses to protect high-potential players from transfer offers.

Youth vs Veterans: Maintain 3-4 experienced players for leadership. Young squads without veterans suffer in big matches. Use veterans in leadership roles while developing youth for long-term sustainability.

Financial Management

Transfer Budget Allocation: 60% for established stars, 30% for promising youth, 10% for emergency transfers. This balances immediate competitiveness with long-term planning.

Youth Investment: Recruit 10+ youth players per season. Keep the 3-4 best and sell the rest. This creates a consistent profit stream while developing stars for your squad.

Wage Budget: Keep total wages below 60% of revenue. This ensures financial stability and enables contract renewals for key players.
