🏆 Year 1 Spring Priority Order

  • Day 1: Clear 1 path to the mountain (Robin), 1 path to the beach (Willy). Meet everyone.
  • Day 2-5: Mine for copper (levels 1-15). Upgrade to steel axe + hoe by day 10.
  • Day 6: Plant 15-20 potatoes (15-day growth = harvest day 21). Fill remaining space with cheap crops.
  • Day 12: Plant remaining spring crops. Spring Onion sells for 1g each but the beach Willy gives you free bait.
  • Spring 28: Plant corn for Summer (keeps growing through Summer into Fall).

⛏️ Mining Priority — Level Guide (Year 1)

  • Levels 1-40: Focus on ores. Copper (5-15), Iron (25-40). By level 40, you should have enough iron for the Furnace.
  • Levels 41-80: Gold ore (40+) and Red Cabbage seeds (find in Skull Cavern). Gold tools = 2x efficiency.
  • Levels 81-120: Iridium (80+). The Iridium Band is the best item in the game — gives +25% ore chance +8 Defense.
  • Skull Cavern: Use staircases (crafted or from panning). 100% strategy: bomb the walls, find stairs, go as deep as possible. Iridium starts at level 100.

⚠️ Mistakes That Slow Your Year 1 (r/StardewValley Analysis)

Mistake 1: Foraging Too Much (you waste the first 2 weeks on nothing)
In Spring Year 1, foraging berries are worth 2g. Your time is better spent mining or fishing for early money. Foraging becomes valuable only with the Botanist profession in Year 2.
Mistake 2: Not Completing the Greenhouse Until Year 2
The Greenhouse unlocks at 100% CC (or year 2 if you don't finish). With 120 Ancient Fruit seeds inside, you earn 1M+ gold in year 2. Finish the CC before Spring 28.
Mistake 3: Fishing Wrong (you ignore the easiest early money)
At Level 10, pick Fisherman (+25% fish price) + Trapper (crab pot fish value x3). River fishing in Spring gives you 500-1000g/day for 20 minutes of play. It's the most efficient early-game money.
