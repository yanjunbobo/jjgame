Stardew Valley — Year 1 to 100k Gold Guide

The crop profit analysis that shows why Cranberries beat Pumpkins, the keg system that makes late game trivial, and the Skull Cave method that gets you to Iridium by Winter Year 1.

Crop Profit Per Day Analysis: The Data That Changes Everything

After 500+ hours tracking every crop's return, the single most important number in Stardew Valley farming is profit per day per tile. This accounts for both the sale price and the grow time. Most players optimize for total harvest value — that's the wrong metric. You want the highest daily return per square of farm space.

Year 1 Profit Strategy: The 100k by Winter Plan

Spring Year 1: Foundation Building

Days 1-7: Clear the entire farm of debris. Plant Parsnips as your starter crop (20 parsnips = 400g profit). Fish every morning for early gold — the minnow method (fishing with bait at the mountain lake) generates 200g/day with zero time investment. By Day 7, you should have 5,000g+.

Days 8-28: Pivot to Potatoes (31.7g/day profit) for the remaining Spring. A 100-tile potato field generates 3,170g per harvest. Buy the Fertilizer skill from Pierre as soon as possible — the quality fertilizer increases the chance of silver/gold crops by 50%, adding approximately 25% to your total revenue.

Spring total: ~15,000g if you optimize fishing + farming combined.

Summer Year 1: The Greenhouse Rush

The Greenhouse unlocks after you reach 100% Perfection (or after collecting 20 Qi Walnut rewards in Year 2). However, you can get a mini-greenhouse (the Junimo Kart bundle) in Year 1 by completing the Pantry bundles. Focus on:

Blueberries (37g/day) — fill your entire Summer plot. The key: buy as many seeds as possible in the first 3 days of Summer. The price is stable but the stock at Pierre's refreshes daily — visit Pierre every day for the first week to buy out his stock.

Fish (if farming isn't enough): During Summer, the Mountain Lake produces Salmon and Rainbow Trout at 100g per fish. The Lobster Trap (crafted) in the ocean generates 300g/day passively. Build 2 Lobster Traps by Day 15 and let them run through Summer.

Fall Year 1: Cranberry Rush

100% of Fall farmland = Cranberries. 100 tiles of Cranberries = 2 harvests (Day 1 and Day 8) = 15,840g revenue - 12,000g seed cost = 3,840g profit. That's 96g/day across your entire Fall farm.

The additional profit lever: Pumpkins (47.7g/day) alongside Cranberries. The optimal split: 60 Cranberries + 20 Pumpkins. The Pumpkins take longer but generate higher total revenue per harvest and can be turned into Pumpkin Pie (300g vs 720g for raw Pumpkin).

Fall Year 1 total: ~30,000g if you combine farming + foraging + fishing.

Winter Year 1: The Grind That Sets Up Year 2

Winter has no crops (without Greenhouse). This is the mining season:

Days 1-15: Skull Cave floors 40-80, specifically for Iridium Ore. The optimal method: use staircases (crafted from Stone) to skip floors with bad layouts. On floors 40+, Iridium spawns with 10% frequency. Each Iridium Bar sells for 500g or can be used for the best sprinklers.

Days 15-28: Fishing in thexinite Mine for Golden Walleye (200g each) and琥. The Mountain Lake produces Squid (150g) during Winter between 6pm and 2am. Set up a Golden Pumpk farm at the sewer (unlimited Pumpkin seeds from the traveling cart).

Winter Year 1 total: ~25,000g from mining + fishing + remaining Barn animal products.

The optimal Year 1 keg strategy: Plant 50 Wheat in Fall (harvest Day 1, regrows in 4 days, two harvests possible) and turn all of it into Beer. Wheat costs 10g per seed, Beer sells for 210g. 50 Wheat × 10g = 500g investment → 50 Beer × 210g = 10,500g revenue. The processing time for Beer is 1-2 in-game days, meaning you can turn your entire Wheat harvest into Beer before Winter starts.

The Keg vs. Preserves Jar Decision

Kegs: Higher value multiplier, but require input every day. Best for: Wheat, Hops, Corn (high-value inputs that benefit most from processing). The cost is 400g and takes 1 clay to craft.

Preserves Jar: Lower multiplier but processes automatically. Best for: crops you don't want to baby-sit (Cranberries, Berries). The jar processes any crop automatically overnight. The cost is 200g and takes 1 coal + 40 wood to craft.

The combined strategy: 10 Kegs + 10 Preserves Jars. Kegs handle Wheat and Hops (the high-multiplier crops). Preserves Jars handle Cranberries and Blueberries (the bulk crops). This combination processes an entire full-size farm's output in 2-3 in-game days.

The Skull Cave: Iridium Farming Method

The Skull Cave is the fastest path to late-game power. Here's the systematic approach that gets you to Iridium in Winter Year 1.

The Optimal Descent Strategy

Step 1: Craft Staircases. One Staircase (99 Stone + 1 Wood) skips any floor instantly. You need 50+ for a successful Iridium run. Craft these in Fall when mining is active.

Step 2: Floor-by-floor scanning. The Skull Cave floors 1-40 contain Copper, Iron, and Gold. Floors 41-80 contain Iridium and Radioactive ores. Floor 80+ contains a guaranteed Iridium node. The optimal strategy: use Staircases to skip floors 1-40 (they have the lowest Iridium rates), then spend time on floors 41-80 where Iridium appears with 10-15% frequency per rock.

Step 3: The mega resource node trick. On floor 80+, the Iridium node is guaranteed. Place a staircase next to the ladder on floor 80 — when you enter, immediately place the staircase to go back down to 80 again. Each visit to floor 80 gives you 8-12 Iridium Ore. 10 visits = 80-120 Iridium Ore = 8-12 Iridium Bars.

The Spelunker + Treasure Hunter Combo

Wear the Miner's Lucky Lunchbox (found in Mines floors 20+) and the Lucky Ring. This combination gives 50% increased spawn rates for all ore nodes. The Miner's Lucky Lunchbox specifically increases the spawn rate of Magma Geodes (which contain Iridium) by 33%.

The Barn Animal System for Late Game Income

Coop vs. Barn: The Profit Comparison

Ranch animals (Barn): Cows and Pigs produce Milk and Truffles. A fully upgraded Barn with 8 Pigs generates 2,000g+ per day in truffles. The Pig is the highest single-income animal in the game.

Coop animals (Coop): Chickens and Ducks produce Eggs and Duck Eggs. A fully upgraded Coop with 12 Dinosaurs (unlocked by reaching floor 80 in Skull Cave) produces Dinosaur Eggs, which sell for 500g each via Mayonnaise Machine.

The optimal setup for Year 2: One full Barn (8 Pigs, 8 Cows) + one full Coop (8 Dinosaurs for Egg profit). This setup generates 5,000g+ per day passively, making the rest of the game trivially funded.
