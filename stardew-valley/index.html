🌾 Year 1 Optimization & Perfection Guide

Stardew Valley's Year 1 sets the foundation for your entire farm. This guide covers optimal crop rotations, artisan good strategies, fishing locations, and the specific milestones needed for perfection by Year 2 in 2026.

Crop Rotation Mastery

Spring: Plant Strawberry on day 13 (from Egg Festival) for 3 harvests before Summer ends. Save 500g for Strawberry seeds—the ROI is 200%+ compared to other Spring crops.

Summer: Blueberry dominates. 80g base price with 3 berries per harvest at 13-day growth. Plant 12 plots minimum. Hot peppers are the backup if you miss the Strawberry window.

Fall: Cranberries at 75g with 2 harvests per plant. Plant 12+ plots. Pumpkin for the Fall ambient but cranberries are strictly better for profit.

Winter: No crops are profitable in Winter. Focus on mining (level 120 skull cavern is the winter goal), fishing for masterangler achievement, and preparing spring crops.

Artisan Good Profit Maximization

Wine vs Jelly: Wine is strictly better for ancient fruit and starfruit (3x value). Jelly is better for berries (2x vs 1.5x for wine from cheap fruits). Always use starfruit for wine, berries for jelly.

Age Sellar's Cheese: Sell goat cheese immediately but age regular (normal milk) cheese in casks for 4 weeks minimum. Gold quality milk aged 4 weeks sells for 740g vs 200g fresh. The time investment is worth 3.7x return.

Perfection Checklist Year 1

By end of Year 1 Winter 28, you should have: completed all non-timegated achievements, unlocked the kiln and furnace, reached mining level 10, caught every fish except legendaries, and planted 50 ancient fruit seeds for Year 2 conversion. If you're missing any of these, you're behind on perfection.
