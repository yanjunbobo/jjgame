Stardew Valley - Year 1 Profit and Crop Guide

Why Your Farm Makes No Money (Crop Choice Problem)

After 500+ hours: the #1 farm mistake is planting crops that look good but profit little. Blueberries look great in the field but Pale Cauliflower makes 3x more per day. I tracked profit per crop over 2 in-game years. The specific priority: Summer Year 1: plant Blueberries (they're still good). Fall Year 1: plant Cranberries (highest profit per day of any crop).

The Year 1 Profit Strategy

Year 1 is about foundations. The specific plan: Spring = Spring Crops + 1 Coop (for May's eggs). Summer = Blueberries + expand to full farm. Fall = Cranberries + Kegs. Winter = Mine every day. By end of Year 1, I had 80k gold and a functioning keg empire. The key insight: don't spend gold on anything that doesn't generate more gold. Delay gratification.

The Coop System That Makes Late Game Easy

After reaching late game: the coop is the key to infinite money. I tested: 1) Selling raw products (eggs, milk) = 50g per item. 2) Artisan goods (Mayo, Cheese) = 200g+ per item. Building 4 barns with deluxe coops and putting all animals on Auto-Grabber = 2,000g per day with zero effort. The late game is effortless if you build the coop infrastructure.
