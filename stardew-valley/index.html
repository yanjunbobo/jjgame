🌾 Year 1 Money Guide

Stardew Valley's economy rewards strategic planning over reactive play. This guide covers the most profitable crop rotations, artisan good strategies, and Year 1 milestones for maximum profit in 2026.

Spring Crops

Potatoes: 3-day growth with 120g base price. With Fertilizer quality bonus, average 160g per harvest. The 6-day growth cycle enables 2 harvests before summer. Net profit: ~2400g per plot per season.

Cauliflower: 12-day growth with 175g base price. The quality bonus can push to 231g at gold quality. The 12-day window means only one harvest. Net profit: ~2800g per plot.

Green Beans: Only viable with bean pole starter. 14-day growth produces 3 harvests per plant. Net profit: ~1800g per plot but with three harvests the total is higher.

Summer Crops

Blueberries: The summer money maker. 13-day growth with 80g base price. Produces 3 berries per harvest. With Deluxe Speed-Gro, the 10-day cycle enables more harvests. Net profit: ~6000g per plot per season.

Melons: 28-day growth with 250g base price. Gold quality pushes to 337g. The single harvest is risky but the per-plot profit is exceptional. Net profit: ~4200g per plot.

Artisan Goods

Jelly: Made from any fruit. Base value is 2x fruit value. Blackcurrant jelly nets 480g per jar. The processing time is 3 days with preserves jars.

Wine: Made from any fruit. Base value is 3x fruit value after 1 in-game week. Starfruit wine nets 2250g per batch from starfruit. The 2-week processing time requires significant capital.

Age Cheese: Goat cheese sells for 400g+ at gold quality. The aging cask brings it to 1200g+ with silver-quality base. Theaging mechanic adds significant value to high-volume cheese production.
