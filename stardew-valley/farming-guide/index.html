🗓️ Optimal Year 1 Farming Schedule

Spring Year 1: 15 Parsnips Day 1, remaining plots with Potato (Spring Mix). Save 400g for Summer seeds.

Summer Year 1: 20 Blueberry (if 1,600g) or Tomato (if on tighter budget). Fill greenhouse with Ancient Fruit seeds (from Seed Maker or Artifact Cave).

Fall Year 1: 20 Cranberries + Pumpkin. Save one plot for Wheat (for Winter beer brewing).

Winter Year 1: Mine to level 100, complete community center. If you unlocked greenhouse, plant Ancient Fruit there.

⚠️ The 5 Farming Mistakes (r/StardewValley Analysis)

Mistake 1: Planting Without Fertilizer
Basic Fertilizer (from Pierre, 10g) increases yield chance by 33%. At 1,000 planted crops per year, that's 300 extra yield = 100K+ gold. Always fertilize your high-value crops.
Mistake 2: Not Using Deluxe Speed-Gro on Corn/Tomato
Corn takes 14 days normal, 9 days with Speed-Gro. Tomato harvests every 4 days. Speed-Gro on these crops = +50% harvests per season. Cost: 40g, Benefit: +200g per crop.
Mistake 3: Leaving Land Empty
In Summer Year 1, each empty plot costs you ~60g/day in lost profit. Even if you can't afford Blueberry, plant Wheat (12g/day profit) to at least have something.
Mistake 4: Not Using the Greenhouse Until Year 2
The Greenhouse unlocks at 100% CC completion. Ancient Fruit inside = 1M gold in Year 2. Complete the CC before Spring 28.
Mistake 5: Harvesting Without Jumba
Once you have 15 crops or more, buy the Scarecrow from Jumba (1,000g). Without it, you miss 10-20% of harvests because crops don't auto-harvest when you're not looking.
