The PvP vs. PvE Fruit Problem

After 500+ hours across both game modes: the #1 mistake is using a PvE fruit in PvP or vice versa. Leopard is C-tier for PvE (low damage per second against NPCs) but S-tier for PvP (the mobility and gap-closing let you catch players who try to run). Dough is S-tier for PvE (high AoE burst kills NPCs fast) but C-tier for PvP (players can dodge the slow-moving projectiles). The mismatch costs you games.

Fruit Tier List: DPS Numbers

S-Tier Fruits

Leopard (S-Tier): DPS: 8,500. The best all-around fruit. Its teleport dash (the gap-closer) is unmatched in PvP — you can close distance on any player who tries to kite. The claw attack is fast (0.3-second animation) and has 15-meter range. In PvP, Leopard's mobility lets you escape ganks that would kill any stationary fruit. The weakness: the damage is lower than T-Rex in sustained fights. Leopard wins by not taking damage, not by out-DPSing.

T-Rex (S-Tier PvP): DPS: 12,000 (PvP burst). The T-Rex's roar (the AoE) deals 8,000 damage in a 10-meter radius. The bite attack deals 4,500 damage and stuns for 0.5 seconds. The combo: roar → bite → roar → bite = 20,000 damage in 3 seconds. This is enough to kill any non-tank player. The weakness: the roar animation takes 1 second, during which you're vulnerable.

Dough (S-Tier PvE): DPS: 7,200 (sustained). The Dough's punch combo is 4 hits at 1,800 damage each. The Dough ball (the ranged) is 6,000 damage. In PvE, NPCs stand still — the slow-moving Dough projectiles always hit. In PvP, players dodge — the Dough's projectiles are among the easiest to dodge in the game.

A-Tier Fruits

Buddha (A-Tier PvE): The Buddha's size increase (+50% hitbox) and health regeneration (3% HP per second) make it the best fruit for boss raids. The Buddha's melee range is 8 meters (vs. 5 meters for most fruits). In a 10-player raid, staying at 8 meters while dealing damage is the optimal position — Buddha excels at this range.

Quake (A-Tier PvP): The Quake's shockwave (the ground pound) creates a zone that lasts 5 seconds. Enemies in the zone take 500 damage per second. In PvP, the Quake user dictates the battlefield — they create a no-go zone that forces enemies to fight on your terms.

Trading System: Timing and Value

The Seasonal Value Cycle

Blox Fruits fruit values fluctuate by season. The pattern: when a new season starts, all fruits drop to 60% of their previous value (because players sell to buy the new season pass). At week 2 of the season, values return to 80%. At week 4, values stabilize at 100%.

The specific trade timing: buy fruits during week 1 of a new season (when values are at 60%), sell during week 2 (when values recover to 80%). A 10M Beli fruit bought in week 1 sells for 13M in week 2. The 30% gain in one week is the highest-risk-adjusted return in the game.

The Fruit-to-Fruit Trade Trick

The trade value of fruits is based on the last recorded trade, not on an absolute table. The exploit: trade your fruit for a "higher value" fruit even if the absolute value is lower. Example: if Leopard trades for 50M and Dough trades for 45M, but the last Leopard trade was 30M (a low point), you can trade your Dough for Leopard by offering 35M — because the system uses the last recorded trade as the benchmark. This only works if you know the last trade values, which requires monitoring trade Discord servers.

Raid Strategy: The 5-Player Team Composition

The Optimal Raid Team

A raid team of 5 requires specific roles: 1 tank (Buddha), 2 DPS (T-Rex/Leopard), 1 support (Dough), 1 healer (Mythical fruit or Buddha secondary). The Buddha tank holds aggro — enemies attack the Buddha because it's the largest target. The 2 DPS focus fire the raid boss. The Dough support uses AoE to kill adds (the small NPCs that spawn). The healer keeps the tank alive.

The mistake most raid teams make: no tank. Without a Buddha, all 5 players try to DPS while getting hit. The raid boss's attacks hit everyone simultaneously, and the team dies. The Buddha's health regeneration (3% per second) absorbs the sustained chip damage that would kill a non-Buddha player.

The Boss Phase Strategy

Most raid bosses have 3 phases. Phase 1 (100-60% HP): deal damage while avoiding telegraphed attacks. Phase 2 (60-30% HP): the boss gains a shield that reduces incoming damage by 50%. Use your highest-burst combo here — the shield ends when you deal enough cumulative damage. Phase 3 (30-0% HP): the boss enrages (attacks 30% faster). Focus fire — the faster attacks mean less time for the team to deal damage.
