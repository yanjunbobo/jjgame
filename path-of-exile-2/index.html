💀 Class Overview & Passive Tree

Path of Exile 2 features six character classes with interconnected passive trees. This guide covers class identity, optimal starting points, and early passive tree priorities for new players in Early Access 2026.

Class Overview

Warrior: The melee powerhouse class. High base life and armor make Warrior the tankiest option. The passive tree focuses on physical damage, weapon specialization, and armor scaling. Ideal for players who enjoy standing in the middle of combat.

Ranger: The ranged damage dealer. High dexterity and evasion make Ranger the most mobile class. The passive tree focuses on projectile damage, accuracy, and attack speed. Ideal for players who enjoy kiting and positioning.

Witch: The spell-casting class. High intelligence and energy shield provide both offense and defense. The passive tree focuses on spell damage, mana management, and elemental effects. Ideal for players who enjoy controlling the battlefield.

Scion: The hybrid class with access to all three starting areas. Lower base stats but maximum flexibility. The passive tree enables combinations impossible for specialized classes. Ideal for experienced players who know exactly what they want.

Templar: The holy warrior class. Strong in both melee and spell support roles. The passive tree focuses on lightning damage, healing, and armor penetration. Ideal for players who enjoy hybrid playstyles.

Mercenary: The precision striker. High accuracy and critical strikes define the Mercenary playstyle. The passive tree focuses on crossbow mastery and critical multipliers. Ideal for players who enjoy burst damage and precision.

Passive Tree Early Priorities

Always grab life nodes first. The first 10 passive points should prioritize +40 life per notable. Unallocated life nodes mean vulnerable characters regardless of damage output.

After life, focus on your primary damage type. Strength nodes for physical damage, dexterity for projectiles, intelligence for spells. The specific nodes depend on your ability choice but the principle remains constant.

After damage, grab your class-specific keystones. Each class has 2-3 keystones that define their playstyle. Research your class's keystones early to plan your tree path efficiently.

Keystone Highlights

Blood Magic: Removes mana costs but requires life for all abilities. Builds around this keystone must prioritize life regeneration and life pool. Powerful for high-spend builds but requires defensive compensation.

Elemental Overload: Converts critical strike chance to consistent elemental damage bonus. Eliminates the need for crit investment but removes critical strike multiplier. Works best with abilities that naturally apply multiple hits.
