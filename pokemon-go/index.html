Why Type Advantage Matters More Than CP

After tracking 500+ raid results: the #1 factor in raid success is type advantage, not CP or level. A level 30 Charizard with Fire-type moves against a Grass-type boss deals more damage per second than a level 40 Dragonite with neutral moves. Here's the math: type advantage = 1.6x damage multiplier. Level 40 vs. level 30 = approximately 1.3x more damage. The type advantage is 30% more impactful than 10 levels of CP.

The Type Chart Quick Reference

The 18 types with their offensive matchups. The most important pairs for raids:

  • Fire vs. Grass, Bug, Steel, Ice → 1.6x damage
  • Water vs. Fire, Ground, Rock → 1.6x damage
  • Electric vs. Water, Flying → 1.6x damage
  • Ice vs. Grass, Dragon, Flying, Ground → 1.6x damage
  • Ground vs. Fire, Electric, Poison, Rock, Steel → 1.6x damage
  • Rock vs. Fire, Ice, Flying, Bug → 1.6x damage

Always check the raid boss's type before choosing your team. Having the correct type counter is equivalent to having 10+ more levels on your Pokemon.

Raid Attacker Tier List: 2026 DPS Rankings

Based on actual DPS calculations against neutral targets, accounting for move database changes in 2026.

S-Tier: The Best in Class

Shadow Mewtwo (Psychic) — The best overall attacker in Pokemon GO. Confusion is the fastest-charging psychic move in the game, and Psystrike is the highest-damage psychic charged move. With Shadow Ball as the secondary, it covers Ghost-type matchups. The weakness: you need Elite TM to get the optimal moveset, and Shadow Mewtwo is only obtainable from Giovanni or special events.

Kyogre (Water) — The best Water-type attacker. Waterfall + Surf is the highest DPS water combination. It single-handedly handles Fire, Ground, and Rock raid bosses. The 2026 buffs to Waterfall made it even better. Primal Kyogre (accessible via special research) has 20% higher stats.

Groudon (Ground) — The best Ground-type attacker. Mud Shot + Earthquake is the optimal ground combo. It deals super-effective damage to 5 types (Fire, Electric, Poison, Rock, Steel), making it one of the most versatile raid attackers in the game.

Rayquaza (Flying/Dragon) — The best Flying-type attacker and best Dragon-type attacker. Dragon Ascend (the new 2026 move) gives it the highest dragon DPS. Air Slash + Aerial Ace is the flying combo. It also handles Bug, Grass, and Fighting raid bosses.

A-Tier: Strong and Consistent

Shadow Salamence (Dragon) — Dragon Tail + Outrage. The Dragon-type DPS is second only to Shadow Dragonite, but the availability is worse (only from Giovanni). The Shadow bonus gives it approximately 20% more DPS than the non-shadow version.

Shadow Tyranitar (Rock/Dark) — Smack Down + Stone Edge. The best Rock-type attacker after the 2026 Smack Down buff. Also covers Ghost and Dark matchups with Bite + Crunch.

Zacian (Fairy) — The best Fairy-type attacker. Snarl + Play Rough deals massive fairy damage. Counter to Dragon and Fighting bosses.

The Mega Evolution Problem

Mega Evolution provides a 10% damage boost to your entire team while active. The strategy: Mega Evolve your best attacker first, then invite friends. The 10% boost applies to all 6 players in the raid. A coordinated group of 6 with Megas active deals 60% more total damage than 6 players without Megas.

The IV System: What Actually Matters

The 0/0/0 vs. 15/15/15 Problem

IVs (Individual Values) range from 0 to 15 for Attack, Defense, and HP. Most players think 15/15/15 is always best. That's only true for raids. For PvP, the breakpoints matter more than the total.

In Great League (CP cap at 1500), a 15/15/15 Pokemon might be 1520 CP — over the cap. A 0/15/12 at 1498 CP is legal and functionally equivalent in stats, but under the cap. The 0 Attack IV gives you "extra level room" — you can power up one more time without hitting the cap. Against opponents with max IVs, the 0 Attack IV means you always get the first Charged Attack in mirror matchups (the breakpoint trick).

The Breakpoint Calculator

Breakpoints are damage thresholds. When your Attack stat crosses a certain value, your fast move deals +1 damage. Against a specific opponent with specific IVs, there's an Attack IV that gives you the +1 damage on every fast move. This is called the "winning breakpoint."

The practical application: for Great League, an Attack IV of 0 is often optimal because it gives you maximum level room while achieving the same effective damage as 15 Attack IV against opponents who are also at the level cap. The defense IV and HP IV matter more in Great League — those determine whether you survive the opponent's charged move by 5 HP or die by 5 HP.

PvP Meta: The Three Leagues

Great League (0-1500 CP)

The most popular PvP format. The most important stat is the level cap — every Pokemon has a different "pivot point" where its max CP falls under 1500. Pokemon that just barely fit under 1500 (like Lickitung at level 39) have more bulk than Pokemon that fit at level 41 with high Attack IVs.

S-Tier Great League Pokemon: Lickitung, Azumarill, Bastiodon, Umbreon, Registeel, Skarmory, Medicham. These Pokemon have the best bulk-to-CP ratio and dominate the meta.

Ultra League (0-2500 CP)

The intermediate format. The core Pokemon are slightly larger and have more flexible IVs. The meta is dominated by Legendaries that just fit under 2500 CP (like Giratina at level 20).

S-Tier Ultra League Pokemon: Giratina (Origin), Swampert, Charizard, Gengar, Obstagoon, Politoed, Cresselia.

Master League (No CP Cap)

The format where 15/15/15 is always optimal (because there's no cap, you want maximum stats). The meta is dominated by maxed Legendaries and Pseudo-Legendaries.

S-Tier Master League Pokemon: Mewtwo, Zacian, Groudon, Kyogre, Palkia, Dialga, Giratina (Origin).

F2P Progression: Building a Competitive Team Without Spending

The Stardust Economy

Stardust is the limiting resource for F2P players. The optimal spending priority:

  1. Best Raid attackers first (6 Pokemon covering 6 types) — these are your long-term investments
  2. PvP League leaders (1 Pokemon for Great League, 1 for Ultra League)
  3. Elite TM savings (don't spend on non-optimal movesets until you have Elite TMs)

The daily task trick: complete 3 excellent throws in a row for the "Great Throw" task → 3,000 Stardust. Do this every day = 90,000 Stardust per month from tasks alone.

The Lucky Pokemon Trick

Lucky Pokemon have guaranteed minimum 12 IVs in every stat and cost 50% less Stardust to power up. The trick: trade Pokemon from 2016 or earlier (high lucky rate), or use the " Guaranteed Lucky" mechanic — every Pokemon you've caught before 2018 becomes Lucky when traded to a new friend.

The optimal strategy: stockpile old Pokemon (from 2016-2018) in storage. When you need a specific attacker, trade the old Pokemon to a new friend. The lucky rate for 2016 Pokemon is approximately 30%, and Lucky Pokemon cost 50% less Stardust to power up. A Lucky level 40 Groudon costs 75,000 Stardust instead of 150,000.
