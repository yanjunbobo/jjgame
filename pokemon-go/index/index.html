The most misunderstood mechanic in Pokemon GO

The most misunderstood mechanic in Pokemon GO: IVs (Individual Values). Most players think IVs matter more than they do. Here's the truth.

IVs: What Actually Matters

IVs range from 0-15 for each of Attack, Defense, and Stamina. A 100% IV Pokemon vs. a 0% IV Pokemon: at level 40, the difference in stats is approximately 10%. At level 30 (where most players stop powering up), the difference is approximately 5%. The 5% stat difference matters in Great League PvP (where a single stat point can flip a matchup), but in raids it never matters.

The practical rule: don't worry about IVs for raid attackers. Power up the highest-level Pokemon you have, regardless of IV. A level 35 0% IV Machamp deals more damage than a level 25 100% IV Dragonite because level matters more than IV in the damage formula.

Why Machamp Is Still the Best General-Purpose Attacker

New players think Mewtwo or Rayquaza are the best attackers. They're not — they're best for specific situations. Machamp is the best general-purpose attacker because: it costs the least dust to power up (30,000 dust for a full team of 6) and its Counter + Dynamic Punch moveset deals super-effective damage against more raid bosses than any other type.

The specific calculation: a full team of level 35 Machamps (Counter + Dynamic Punch) vs. a Level 25 Mewtwo (Psycho Cut + Psystrike). Machamp team DPS: 22. Mewtwo team DPS: 28. But Machamp team cost: 180,000 dust. Mewtwo team cost: 1,200,000 dust. Per dust, Machamp is 4x more efficient.

The Max Level 50 Grind: What Actually Takes Time

Level 50 requires 520,000 XP and approximately 400,000 stardust for full powering. The fastest XP route: doing raids (15,000 XP per T5 raid) combined with Lucky Eggs (2x XP from all sources for 30 minutes). Activate a Lucky Egg before every raid. 5 raids with a Lucky Egg = 150,000 XP in 2 hours. Without a Lucky Egg: 75,000 XP. Always use Lucky Eggs for raids.
