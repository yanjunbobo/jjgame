The voyage strategy for 100K gold per hour

Gold Guide — Most Efficient 100K+ Gold Per Hour | 2026

Sea of Thieves has no character levels — every player's power comes from cosmetics. Therefore gold efficiency (gold per hour) determines how fast you unlock the cosmetics you want. The "best" voyage depends entirely on your crew size and PvP willingness.

📊 Voyage Efficiency by Type

Best for Solo Players: Merchant Voyages
Solo = 1 player. Merchant voyages involve delivering animals (chickens, pigs, snakes) and cargo crates. The gold-per-hour for solo Merchant runs: 40-60K/hour. The risk: low (cargo is non-combat). The reward: consistent but modest. The trick: sell at the nearest outpost immediately. Every minute you hold cargo = risk of losing it to other players.
Best for Duos: Order of Souls (Ghost Fleet)
Order of Souls is a voyage of 3-5 waves of ghost ships. With 2 players (one sails, one shoots), each wave takes 5-8 minutes. Each ghost ship wave = 15-25K gold. Total voyage: 50-100K in 45 minutes = 70-100K/hour. The risk: you're stationary during combat, making you a PvP target. But in a duo sloop, you're fast enough to escape most threats.
Best for Crews of 3+: Skull Forts
Skull Forts (the large skull icon on the map) are the highest-value world event. The key: the person who FIRST lands on the island gets credit. If you're on a server where a Skull Fort just spawned, SPRINT to it. The fort itself yields 30-50K in loot, but the real money is the Key of Ocean's Fortune (50K from selling). Skull Forts take 60-90 minutes with a full crew of 4.
The Hidden Gem: Athena's Fortune Voyages
For players who've reached Pirate Legend (50 Athena's rep), the Hourglass PvP voyages are the highest gold/hour: 100-200K per hour. But they require winning against another crew. If you're confident in combat, the Hourglass is unmatched. If not, Ghost Fleet with a 3-man crew is the safer high-value option.

⚠️ The 5 Gold Mistakes

Mistake 1: Holding Treasure Too Long
Every minute treasure is on your ship = a minute it can be stolen. Most players don't realize that other crews can SEE emissary flags from far away. If you're flying a Grade 5 Gold Hoarders flag, you're visible to everyone on the server. Sell every 30 minutes minimum. Don't wait for "one more voyage" before selling.
Mistake 2: Ignoring Emissary Running
An emissary flag (Gold Hoarders, Order of Souls, etc.) gives +20-50% gold on every sale. Always run an emissary before starting a voyage. The exception: Tall Tales (story missions) — these don't benefit from emissaries and taking one risks losing your flag when you die to PvP.
Mistake 3: Selling at the Furthest Outpost
Most players run to the nearest outpost automatically. But some outposts (Dagger Tooth, Plunder Outpost) have better commissions (daily quests with bonus gold). Always check the Voyage table before selling — sometimes selling at the "wrong" outpost gives you a +50% commission bonus on your next voyage.
Mistake 4: Not Checking the Message in a Bottle
Message in a Bottle (bottles floating in the water) contain voyages from the Reaper's Bones faction. Completing these voyages = Reaper rep AND gold. But the Reaper's Bones emissary flag makes you visible to ALL players on the server (not just nearby). If you're low on supplies or near hostile players, skip the bottle.
