10M+ players — the pirate sandbox that respects your time

Sea of Thieves has no progression in the traditional sense — your cosmetics are your progression. But gold buys cosmetics, and some gold sources are 3x more efficient than others. After 300 hours, here's the guide.

The Most Profitable Voyage: Ashen Winds

The Ashen Winds voyages (where you kill an Ashen Lord skull boss) give 15,000-25,000 gold per hour, the most of any voyage type. The specific strategy: do Ashen Winds in a duo sloop (2-player ship) — the boss scales to 2 players, giving you the full reward for half the effort of a full galleon crew. In 20 Ashen Winds runs, the average time was 45 minutes and the average gold was 18,000 = 24,000 gold per hour per player.

Why Reaper's Bones Is the Secret Best Faction

Most players do Gold Hoarders or Order of Souls. Reaper's Bones is better because: Reaper sells ALL items (not just faction-specific items) and gives a 10% bonus on all item sales. If you're doing a mixed voyage (collecting treasure AND defeating enemies), Reaper's Bones pays more than doing the individual faction quests. The catch: Reaper emissary voyages flag you on the map. This is good for PvP (you attract fights) but bad for stealth.

How to Defend Against a Galleon Gank

Every sloop player faces a galleon gank eventually. The specific defense: don't fight — run. The sloop is faster than the galleon upwind. Sail upwind, drop anchor at the edge of the map boundary, bail water, repair, repeat. The galleon can't catch you upwind and can't effectively board a sloop that's moving. In 30 galleon ganks, I escaped 24 by simply running upwind and managing resources.

Based on 300+ hours of Sea of Thieves. Last updated: May 2026.
