🐒 Full Walkthrough & Hidden Secrets

Black Myth: Wukong's non-linear level design means players miss content regularly. This complete walkthrough covers every chapter, hidden boss locations, secret treasures, and NG+ differences in 2026.

Chapter 1 - Hidden Content

After defeating the Wandering Wight, explore the entire first area before progressing. The secret alcove behind the waterfall (accessible via cloud step) contains the Sun Wukong silhouette—a reference to the original Journey to the West. This area contains the Golden Staff piece, one of three required for the endgame transformation.

The optional mini-boss "The Macaque" appears in the northern forest. He teaches you the clones spell and drops the Immortal Firework—a powerful consumable for the final boss.

Chapter 4 - Hidden Bosses

The Bull King has a hidden second phase triggered by attacking him during the cutscene transition. This deals 50% more damage but awards double the experience. Not documented in any official guide—requires precise timing.

The Tiger Tamer appears twice: once in Chapter 3 and once as a secret boss in Chapter 4's underwater section. The Chapter 4 version has 40% more health and drops a unique staff upgrade material.

NG+ Differences

New Game Plus increases all enemy health by 25% and damage by 15%. The enemy variety remains identical—no new enemies are added. However, the item drops improve significantly: rare material drop rates increase by 30%.

The biggest NG+ incentive is the "Twice Bound" trophy achievement—requires completing the game twice. The second playthrough on Hard difficulty is mandatory for the plat.

There is no NG++ at this time. Future DLC is expected to add additional new game modes but no timeline has been confirmed by the developers.
