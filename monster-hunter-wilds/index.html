🔥 Top 10 Questions Hunters Ask

1. What is the best weapon for beginners?

Sword & Shield offers the most forgiving gameplay with quick sheathe time, versatile combos, and excellent defensive options. Long Sword is second best due to abundant guides and community knowledge.

2. How do I unlock all 14 weapons?

All weapons are available from the start — simply visit the Smithy. Each weapon tree has multiple upgrade paths. Focus on one weapon type early to maximize upgrade efficiency.

3. What's the current meta weapon?

Charge Blade dominates solo speedruns due to the Condensed Spinning Slash (CSS) animation cancel. Long Sword is the most popular for general play. Dual Blades excel in multiplayer.

4. How do monster weaknesses work?

Each monster has elemental weaknesses (fire, water, thunder, ice, dragon) and status weaknesses (poison, sleep, paralysis, blast). Target weak zones for maximum damage — usually the head or specific body parts.

5. When should I farm High Rank vs Low Rank armor?

Skip Low Rank armor entirely if you're past early game. Start crafting High Rank armor as soon as you unlock it — the defense increase is massive and skills are significantly better.

6. What are the endgame content types?

After completing the main story, pursue: Apex Monsters ( tempered versions), Event Quests, Investigation Quests, and the Elder Dragon Seung. Each provides different crafting materials.

7. How does the Seikloot system work?

You can now freely change armor skills using the Technical Merchant system. This fundamentally changes endgame — wear any armor and reskill it to your desired build.

8. What's the fastest way to level weapons?

Focus on completing Investigation Quests with rare rewards. The combined melding system lets you convert excess materials into weapon-specific drops. Always do your daily Rotating Quests.

9. Are there gender differences in stats?

No. Male and female hunters have identical stats, attack power, and defense. Choose whichever appearance you prefer — cosmetic only.

10. How does multiplayer scaling work?

Monster HP scales: 1 player = 100%, 2 = 220%, 3 = 300%, 4 = 360%. Solo hunting is often faster for skilled players. Join multiplayer for difficult monsters or farming efficiency.

2. Weapon Analysis & Recommended Builds

⚔️

Charge Blade

S-Tier • Solo Speedrun Meta • Highest Skill Ceiling

The Condensed Spinning Slash (CSS) animation cancel is the defining technique of Wilds. By canceling the CSS recovery into an AED/SAED, Charge Blade achieves the fastest solo kill times recorded. The new Weapon Art: Shield Thrust also allows for quick energy building without committing to full combos. In multiplayer, its ability to tenderize and cut tails quickly makes it invaluable.

Why It's #1

  • • CSS animation cancel deals 300%+ more DPS than standard combos
  • • SAED (Super AED) now pierces through multiple monsters
  • • Highest burst damage potential in the game
  • • Excellent monster part tenderness for team damage

Long Sword

S-Tier • Most Popular • Excellent DPS & Mobility

The Long Sword remains the most popular weapon for good reason. The redesigned Spirit Stance system makes meter management more intuitive while the new Iai Spirit Slash counters are more forgiving. Long Sword users deal consistent damage while maintaining maximum mobility — critical in Wilds' larger, more open environments.

Key Strengths

  • • Spirit gauge mechanics revamped — easier to maintain SSS meter
  • • Iai counters now have larger hitbox windows

🔸 A-Tier Weapons

🌪️ Dual Blades — Best DPS Against Moving Targets

The Demon Mode 2.0 returns with the new Demonic Rampage finisher extending DPS windows significantly. In Wilds' open areas, Dual Blades excel at chasing mobile monsters. The stamina consumption has been rebalanced — Demon Mode now drains 15% less.

Best Set: Brachydium Rush (4pc) + Attack Boost 4, Critical Eye 4, Stamina Surge 3

🏹 Bow — Safe Ranged DPS

Bow's Power Coat + Haste Coating combo remains devastating. The new Dragon Piercer 2.0 actually works now — but requires mastery of charge timing. The dash dance mechanic has been refined for smoother kiting.

Best Set: Zinogre Spirit (4pc) + Focus 5, Constitution 3

⚡ Switch Axe — New Zero Sum Discharge

The Zero Sum Discharge has been redesigned — you now have iframe during the explosion. The Phial system now has elemental variants that affect the axe mode. Underrated in the current meta but has the highest potential ceiling with proper build.

Best Set: Magia Soul (4pc) + Attack Boost 5, Critical Eye 4, Focus 3

🪵 Insect Glaive — Aerial Momentum

The new Aerial Momentum mechanic makes vault attacks significantly more rewarding. Kinsect extracts now last 50% longer. The bug companion AI has been improved — they'll follow your target more reliably.

Best Set: Hazard Spirit (4pc) + Attack Boost 4, Airborne 3, Fortitude 3

🔹 B-Tier Weapons

🗡️ Great Sword

The new Power Sheathe mechanic allows sheathing in 0.3 seconds after a tackle. True Charge Slash damage increased by 20%. Still outclassed by Charge Blade and Long Sword for raw damage, but the easiest S-tier weapon to pick up for beginners.

🔨 Hammer

Stunning power unchanged. The new Guff Combo finisher deals massive KO damage. Still the best head-sniping weapon in the game. A solid choice for beginners who want to learn monster patterns via bonk.

⚔️ Sword & Shield

The most beginner-friendly weapon. New Perfect Rush combo does competitive damage if you land all hits. Shield now has a parry window (0.2s) for deflecting attacks. Excellent for learning the game.

🛡️ Lance

The Guard Tackle now has i-frames. Twin Spire combo provides decent DPS. Still the king of defensive play but damage ceiling is too low for endgame content without perfect play.

🔻 C-Tier Weapons

💥 Gunlance

The new Shell Burst deals splash damage but animation lock is punishing. Wide GL shelling type still viable for support play but outclassed in every other regard.

🎵 Hunting Horn

Dooting is back but the song system requires proximity. New Echo Note mechanic allows self-buffing mid-combo. Fun but inconsistent — your buffs depend on not dying.

🏹 Heavy Bowgun

Wyvern Ammo is back but base damage reduced. The new Special Scope mechanic allows charge zoom. Still purely a support weapon — requires team to shine.

5. Crafting & Upgrade System

Weapon Upgrade Trees

Each weapon has multiple upgrade paths. Use the Smithy to compare branches. Key resources:

🔨 Iron Path (Basic)

Iron Ore + Earth Crystals. Unlocks all base weapon types. Upgrade to Tier 2 at Smithy.

⚙️ Alloy Path (Standard)

Machined Alloy + Polymer. Requires High Rank materials. Most weapons have this path.

💎 Apex Path (Rare)

Apex Shards + Elder Dragon material. Only for upgraded versions. Highest base damage.

✨ Rarity 12 (Max)

Genesis Crystals + seikloot fragments. Final upgrade tier. Requires HR 100+.

6. Endgame Mechanics

🎯 Apex Monsters

Tempered versions of existing monsters with increased HP, damage, and new attack patterns. Drop Apex Shards for Rarity 11+ weapons.

  • • Apex Zinogre, Rajang, Diablos, Tigrex available
  • • Appear in Tier 3+ Event Quests
  • • Guaranteed Apex Shard on capture

🌀 Investigation Quests

Infinite repeatable quests with varying conditions. Better rewards than standard hunts.

  • • 1-5 star difficulty ratings
  • • Bonus conditions multiply rewards
  • • Track via Hunter's Notes menu

🏆 Event Quests

Time-limited quests with exclusive rewards. Rotate weekly.

  • • Colosseum quests for weapon training
  • • Limited armor pieces (cosmetics)
  • • Bonus material drops for crafting

👑 Elder Dragon Seung

The true final boss. Requires HR 80+ and full augmented armor.

  • • Multiple phases with unique mechanics
  • • Drops Genesis Crystals
  • • Achievement: "Monster Hunter" for solo clear

Augmentation System

At HR 100+, you can augment equipment using Elder Dragon material and Zenny:

Defense Augment

+10 defense per level. Max 3 levels.

Adds decoration slots. Rare, expensive.

7. Beginner Tips & FAQ

🌟

New to Monster Hunter Wilds?

Start with Sword & Shield. It's forgiving, versatile, and teaches you monster patterns without the punishment of long weapon combos. Once comfortable, transition to Long Sword (highest player count = most guides/videos available) or Charge Blade (highest DPS ceiling).

Q: How do I increase my damage output?

Focus on: 1) Hitting weak points (head, wounds), 2) Maximizing affinity (crit chance), 3) Using elemental weakness weapons, 4) Learning combos for consistent DPS instead of button mashing.

Q: Should I capture or kill monsters?

Always capture when the quest says "Capture" or when monster is limping (crying, moving slowly). Capturing gives 20% more materials and sometimes exclusive items.

Q: What's the best way to farm Zenny?

Complete high-difficulty Event Quests with bonus conditions. The "Goldenfish" optional quest in the Wastelands gives 50,000+ Zenny per run with good conditions.

Q: How do I join multiplayer sessions?

Use the Seikloot Community Board in camp. SOS flares appear on your map when other players post them. You can join via the Hunter's Notes menu or during quests via the menu.

8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Ignoring Armor Skills

Many beginners focus only on weapon damage. In Wilds, Weakness Exploit + Critical Eye synergy often outperforms raw attack increases. Always optimize your full build, not just the weapon.

❌ Overfarming Low Rank Content

Wilds' progression is fast. Spend minimum time on Low Rank armor — High Rank gear is only slightly more materials but 3x better stats. Move to next tier as soon as available.

❌ Skipping Sidequests

Several critical weapon upgrades (particularly for Charge Blade and Switch Axe) are locked behind NPC Investigation sidequests. Check your Hunter's Notes regularly.

❌ Not Using the Training Area

The Training Area has damage calculators, combo trainers, and target practice. Spend 15 minutes there before learning a new weapon instead of wasting time in real hunts.

❌ Selling Excess Materials

You never know what you'll need later. Keep at least 20 of each monster material. Use the Item Box Organize function to auto-sort by category.
