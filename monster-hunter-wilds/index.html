2. S-Tier: Top Performers

⚔️

Charge Blade

S-Tier • Solo Speedrun Meta • High Skill Ceiling

Why it's #1

The new Condensed Spinning Slash (CSS) animation cancel breaks the game. By canceling the CSS recovery into an AED/SAED, Charge Blade achieves the fastest solo kill times in the game. Weapon Arts integration makes it feel smoother than ever.

Recommended Build

Rathalos Blessing (4pc)
+Attack Boost 6
+Critical Eye 4
+Weakness Exploit 3
// Swap Rathalos for Val Habet if you need Blast

⚠️ Warning: CSS animation canceling requires precise timing. Practice in the Training Area before attempting in hunts. Misinput can cause massive DPS loss.

🗡️

Long Sword

S-Tier • Most Popular • Excellent DPS & Mobility

Why it's S-Tier

Still the most popular weapon for good reason. The new Spirit Stance changes make meter management more forgiving while maintaining Long Sword's signature counter-focused gameplay. Top-tier mobility and reach.

Recommended Build

Narga Soul (4pc)
+Attack Boost 5
+Critical Eye 5
+Spirit Generosity 3
// Max out Affinity for consistent white sharpness

3. A-Tier: Strong Choices

🌪️

Dual Blades

A-Tier • Best DPS Against Moving Targets

The Demon Mode 2.0 is back and stronger than World. Dual Blades excel at punishing mobile monsters with constant uptime. The new Demonic Rampage combo extends DPS window significantly.

Best Set: Brachydium Rush (4pc)
+Attack Boost 4 • +Critical Eye 4 • +Stamina Surge 3
🏹

Bow

A-Tier • Safe Ranged DPS • Coat System

Bow's Power Coat + Haste Coating combo is back and stronger. Maintains safe range while outputting competitive DPS. New Dragon Piercer 2.0 actually works now — but requires charge timing mastery.

4. B-Tier: Viable Options

🪵 Insect Glaive

New Aerial Momentum mechanic makes vault attacks more rewarding. Vines still provide excellent utility. DPS gap vs top tiers narrowed.

🔨 Hammer

Stunning power unchanged. Guff Combo new finisher deals massive KO damage. Still the best head-sniping weapon in the game.

5. Beginner Weapon Recommendations

6. Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Ignoring Armor Skills

Many beginners focus only on weapon damage. In Wilds, Weakness Exploit + Critical Eye synergy often outperforms raw attack increases. Always optimize your full build, not just the weapon.

❌ Overfarming Old Zones

Wilds' open world rewards efficient grinding. Focus on Sandplain Ibushi for early armor materials — it has the best material-to-power ratio until High Rank.

❌ Skipping Sidequests

Several critical weapon upgrades (particularly for Charge Blade and Switch Axe) are locked behind NPC Investigation sidequests. Check your Hunter's Notes regularly.
