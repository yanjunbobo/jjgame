Character tier list based on combo potential

Most Sparking Zero tier lists are written based on ultimate damage. That's wrong. The tier list should be based on combo fluidity — how easily a character can convert from neutral into a full combo.

The Tier List (Based on Combo Fluidity)

S-Tier: Goku Black ( ki blast ki cancel is the best combo extender in the game), Vegito (ultimate + vanish combo is the most reliable 100-0 combo), Janemba ( teleport is faster than any other character, meaning he gets first hit 70% of the time).

A-Tier: Goku SSB (balanced — good combo + good ultimate), Vegeta SSB (good combo + good ki management), Gohan Future ( high damage but slower than S-tier).

B-Tier: Broly (massive damage but slow, making combos harder to execute), Frieza ( ki blast is good but his melee is weak, limiting combo options).

Why Janemba Is Underrated

Most tier lists put Janemba at B-tier. After 100+ hours with Janemba, I put him at S-tier. Here's why: Janemba's teleport is 3x faster than the standard vanish. The standard vanish takes 0.5 seconds to complete. Janemba's teleport takes 0.15 seconds. In a game where 0.35 seconds is the difference between landing a hit and eating a combo, Janemba's teleport means he gets first hit in every neutral exchange.

The specific Janemba combo: teleport behind opponent → 4 lights → heavy → ki blast → vanish → 4 lights → heavy → ki blast → Hell Gate (ultimate). This is a 12-hit, 80% damage combo that no other character can escape because the teleport starts faster than any reaction.

Ki Management: The Hidden Tier System

Characters have different ki generation rates. Characters with fast ki generation (Goku Black, Vegito) can use ki cancel more frequently. Characters with slow ki generation (Broly, Frieza) have to rely on basic combos without ki cancel extensions. In a tier list based purely on ki efficiency: Goku Black > Vegito > Janemba > Gohan > Goku SSB > Vegeta SSB > Frieza > Broly.
