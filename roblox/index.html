Roblox — Complete Guide 2026

Why DOORS stays the #1 horror game, the Blox Fruits market timing strategy, how developers actually make $100K+ per month, and every major game's core mechanic explained.

Top 10 Most Played Roblox Games (2026)

Ranked by monthly active users as of Q1 2026.

1. DOORS (15M+ MAU)

The procedural horror game where you progress through 500 procedurally generated doors while avoiding entities. Each door run takes 30-90 minutes. The reason it stays #1: every run is different. The entities are the same (Rush, Ambush, Screech, Halt, etc.) but their placement and timing varies per run. This creates genuine tension rather than memorizable patterns.

DOORS mechanic breakdown: The core loop is: enter room → encounter entity → survive → progress. The skill expression comes from reaction speed (Rush requires you to fit behind a locker or bed before it arrives), prediction (Ambush requires you to know it's coming based on room layout), and resource management (Vitamins give +20% speed for 30 seconds — use them at Door 80+ for maximum value).

2. Blox Fruits (12M+ MAU)

The One Piece-inspired action RPG. Players collect fruits (consumable abilities with cooldown-based powers), level up through combat, and trade fruits for profit. The Blox Fruits economy is the game's defining feature — the in-game fruit market follows real economic patterns of supply, demand, and speculation.

The fruit market cycle: New fruits released at season start trade at 2x their base value within 6 weeks due to demand. After 3 months, prices stabilize. The optimal trading strategy: buy fruits at season start when demand is high, hold for 6-8 weeks, sell at the peak before the next season's fruit release. This cycle generates 20-40% returns on investment.

3. Brookhaven (10M+ MAU)

The open-world roleplay game. Brookhaven is unique among Roblox top games because it's a social sandbox rather than a progression game. Players own houses, drive vehicles, and roleplay scenarios. The lack of grinding makes it accessible to casual players — there's no "wrong" way to play.

4. Adopt Me! (8M+ MAU)

The pet trading and adoption simulation. Players collect pets (from common to legendary), trade them with other players, and decorate their houses. The economy is based on supply and demand — legendary pets from discontinued events (such as the Aussie Egg pets) are worth thousands of Robux.

5-10. The Remaining Top Games

5. Tower of Hell (5M+ MAU): Obby (obstacle course) with randomly generated towers. The skill is completing the obby before the timer runs out.

6. Piggy (4M+ MAU): Horror survival game where players alternate between survivor and killer roles.

7. Murder Mystery 2 (3M+ MAU): Werewolf-style game with a detective system. The sheriff (protector), innocent (survivor), and murderer (killer) roles create asymmetric gameplay.

8. Royale High (3M+ MAU): Dress-up and social simulation. The value comes from limited-edition accessories and the trading economy.

9. Build a Boat for Treasure (2M+ MAU): Obstacle course combined with building mechanics. Players build a boat and navigate it through obstacles to collect treasure.

10. Shindo Life (2M+ MAU): Naruto-inspired combat RPG with a Blox Fruits-like fruit (spinner) system and tier-based progression.

The Vitamin Economy

Vitamins (Speed, Strength, and Sealed fruits) are the only consumable items in DOORS. Each gives a 30-second buff. The most valuable: Speed at Door 80-150, where the room density is highest and mobility determines survival. The common mistake: using Speed at Door 10 when you're nowhere near the danger zone.

The save strategy: Use Speed at Door 100+ only. Before Door 100, the entity threat is manageable without vitamins. The vitamin supply is finite per run (you find them in rooms) — wasting them early means having none when you need them most.

Blox Fruits: The Economy and Fruit Market

Fruit Tier List by PvP Effectiveness

S-Tier: Dough (parry mechanic, highest block value in game, melts through defense) — Leopard (speed-based kit, hardest to hit, highest combo potential) — Buddha (defense transformation, largest hitbox, easiest to play).

A-Tier: Ice (freeze mechanic, strong control), Venom (DoT stacking, good for sustain fights), Quake (knockback control, keeps enemies at bay).

B-Tier: Flame (overrated — the damage is lower than S-tier but the mobility is good), Light (frame-perfect teleport, high skill ceiling), Dark (burst damage, weak to S-tier fruit players).

The Market Timing Strategy

Trading Blox Fruits is equivalent to trading financial assets. The patterns: fruits released at season start have a 6-week hype cycle before prices peak. Fruits that get a major buff (game update that makes them stronger) spike 50-100% within 48 hours of the patch. The strategy: monitor Roblox patch notes, buy the buffed fruit within 24 hours of the patch (before the market realizes the full impact), sell at the 2-week peak.

The specific trade to make: When a new fruit is released, buy 5 of it at the initial price. Within 6 weeks, the price will double. Sell 4, keep 1 for personal use. This is the lowest-risk high-return trade in Blox Fruits.

How Roblox Developers Make $100K+ Per Month

The Revenue Breakdown

40% — Game Passes: Permanent access passes that provide in-game advantages (+20% damage, +2x gold, exclusive areas). The "damage boost" pass is the consistent seller — players always want to be stronger. Set it at $9.99 Robux (approximately $7 USD developer payout after Roblox's cut).

30% — Private Server Subscriptions: Monthly subscriptions ($9.99/month) for private server access. This is the highest-LTV revenue stream because subscribers renew every month. A game with 1,000 active subscribers = $7,000/month recurring revenue.

20% — Sponsored Content: Brand deals within games. Roblox has a formal sponsorship program where brands (Nike, Gucci, Chipotle) pay developers to create branded experiences. The rates range from $10,000 to $500,000 per brand activation depending on the game's size.

10% — Direct Item Sales: Limited-edition accessories sold directly. The developer creates a cosmetic item, sets a quantity (1,000 copies), and sells at $4.99-$19.99. Limited supply creates urgency and resale value.

The Developer Tiers

Casual Developer (< $1,000/month): One person, small game, basic monetization. The focus is on building an audience first, monetization second.

Professional Developer ($1K-$50K/month): 3-10 person team, dedicated updates every 2 weeks, active community management. This is where Blox Fruits developer (Blox Fruits LLC) sits — they reportedly earn $5M+ per month.

Studio Developer ($50K-$1M/month): Full studio with dedicated art, engineering, and marketing teams. Games like Adopt Me! (义乌网络) are estimated at $50M+ annual revenue.
