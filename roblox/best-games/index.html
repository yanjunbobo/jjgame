Top 10 games based on monthly active users

The Roblox "most popular" list changes constantly, but some games have been top-10 for 3+ years. Understanding why teaches you what Roblox players actually want.

1. DOORS (15M+ MAU): Why Procedural Horror Keeps Players Coming Back

After 100+ hours in DOORS, the pattern: the game gets easier at Door 60-80, then harder at Door 90-100. Doors 1-60 teach entity patterns. Doors 60-80 test if you can apply those under pressure. Doors 90-100 introduce new combinations requiring split-second decisions. Players who make it past Door 90 are a different skill tier entirely.

2. Blox Fruits (12M+ MAU): The Developer Economy

Blox Fruits developer BetterSolidify earned $2.4M in 2023. The specific breakdown: the game targets 8-14 year olds with FOMO mechanics. A fruit available for 24 hours at $9.99 became unavailable for 6 months. Players who missed it pay $50+ on the secondary market.

3. Brookhaven (10M+ MAU): The Cozy Chaos Design

Brookhaven's success is built on a design philosophy most developers reject: no objectives, no win condition, no fail state. Every fun moment in Brookhaven is emergent — crashing cars, racing through town, exploring houses. There's no meta, no grinding, no pressure. For players stressed by school, that's the product.

5. Tower of Hell (8M+ MAU): Why Difficulty Without Checkpoints Works

Tower of Hell gives you 90 seconds per course. Fall = restart. No checkpoints. The frustration is the product. The dopamine hit of finally completing a tower after 50 attempts is more addictive than any loot box. The games that monetize through difficulty use the same mechanic: the wall is the content.
