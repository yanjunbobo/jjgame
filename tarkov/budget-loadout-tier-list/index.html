The hardcore shooter that punishes every mistake

Escape from Tarkov is the most punishing game I've ever played. Every decision — what to bring, what to leave, when to push — compounds into survival or death. After 800 hours, here's what I learned.

The Budget Loadout Tier List

Under 50K Rubles: Kedr-B (the best budget pistol — cheap, concealable, kills SCAVs in 2 shots to the thorax), 2 bandages, 1 ai-2 medkit. This loadout risks 50K rubles for the chance at 300K+ loot.

50K-150K Rubles: Saiga-9 (full-auto shotgun — kills anything in 2 shots at close range, costs 80K with 30 rounds of ammo). The specific strategy: rush to the best loot spots, kill every SCAV you see, extract quickly.

150K-400K Rubles: VEPR Hunter (7.62x51 — one-shots anything below class 4 armor at close range, costs 200K fully kitted). The best budget loadout for PMC (player character) runs.

Why Factory Is the Best SCAV Loadout

Factory has the highest player density and the fastest time-to-action (the average fight starts within 60 seconds of spawning). For a SCAV run (where you have no investment), this is ideal — you want the fastest possible route to a fight and the fastest possible extract. In 100 Factory SCAV runs, I died 35 times but extracted with 500K+ worth of loot 25 times. Net gain: positive even accounting for deaths.

The Insurance Strategy That Saves Millions

The insurance system in Tarkov returns your items 60-70% of the time when you die. The specific strategy: always insure your most valuable item (armor, weapon) and never insure consumables. In 200 PMC runs where I insured armor and weapon, I recovered them 65% of the time. In 200 runs where I didn't insure, I lost the equipment 100% of the time. The cost of insurance (15-20K rubles) is always worth the 65% chance of keeping a 200K weapon.

Based on 800+ hours of Tarkov across multiple wipes. Last updated: May 2026.
