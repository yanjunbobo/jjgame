The loot tier system that maximizes survival

The key to Tarkov's economy is not combat — it's knowing which rooms contain the most valuable loot and how to get there fastest. Here's the complete guide.

The Loot Tier System

Tier 1 (Best): Ultra Medical (on Interchange — guaranteed LedX, 2+ GPU, and rare meds), Goshan register (on Interchange — guaranteed 2-5 million rubles in cash/items), TerraGroup offices (on Labs — the only Labs loot that makes Labs profitable).

Tier 2 (Good): Mechanical Keys (any map — they unlock rooms with guaranteed high-value loot), Weapon boxes near extracts (random but high-value), Techlight/Technic on Customs (GPU spawn).

Tier 3 (Worth it): Every other lootable container. The specific rule: if a room has been looted (empty containers), move on. If it hasn't, spend 30 seconds checking every container.

Interchange: The Ultra Medical Route

Ultra Medical is inside the Emercom medical tent on Interchange. The specific route: enter through the power station entrance (least camped), move through the mall toward Emercom, check every bag on the way (bags often have meds), hit Ultra Medical last. If Ultra Medical has been looted (the door will be open and containers empty), extract immediately and try another map. If it's full, you've just made 10-15 million rubles.

Why Key Tool Strategy Matters

Keys in Tarkov are single-use — once you use them, they're consumed. The specific decision tree: only use keys on SCAV runs (where you pay nothing for the key if you die). On PMC runs, only use keys you've found in-raid (not purchased). Purchased keys cost 50K-500K and being killed with one is a net loss. Found keys are free — using them has no downside.
