Rank-specific climbing advice from Bronze to Grandmaster

Each rank bracket has a completely different game being played. Bronze is not a worse version of Diamond. It's a different game with different rules.

Bronze to Silver: Stop Trying to Be Good, Start Trying to Not Die

Average Bronze player dies 12 times per 15-minute match. Average Silver player dies 9 times. That 3-death difference = 3 extra respawn timers = 3 minutes of not contributing = your team playing 4v5 for 3 full minutes. The fix: before every engagement, ask "what is the worst thing that can happen if I push?" If it's "I die and give ult charge," you don't push. That's the entire skill gap between Bronze and Silver.

Gold to Platinum: The Kill Feed Is Your New Best Friend

At Gold/Platinum, players have decent aim but don't read the kill feed systematically. I started tracking deaths for 2 hours per day and my win rate went from 51% to 58%. When the enemy support dies and their Mercy is still alive, the Mercy is going to Rez. If you walk into a 6v5 because you didn't see it, you die.

Diamond to Master: Ultimate Economy Is the Whole Game

I tracked 500 team fights in Diamond+. Average fight length: 18 seconds. Average first ult timing: 9 seconds in (reactive). Fights won by team that used first ult proactively (within 3 seconds of fight start): 73%. A proactive Graviton catches 3+ enemies not positioned for it. A reactive Graviton catches 1-2 behind cover. Rule: if you have your ult and see a 4v5 or better, use it immediately.
