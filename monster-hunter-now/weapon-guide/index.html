Weapon Guide — Great Sword vs Long Sword vs Dual Blades | 2026

Monster Hunter Now simplifies the weapon roster to 6 options. The question every new player asks: which weapon should I main? The answer isn't "best weapon" — it's "best weapon for your playstyle and available time." Here's the definitive breakdown.

📊 Weapon Tier List (PvE / Hunt Performance)

S-Tier: Great Sword (GS)
The highest DPS weapon in MHN when mastered. Its True Charged Slash deals 400+ damage on a perfect charge. But it requires 3-4 seconds of stationary charging — in mobile GPS hunts, this means you're vulnerable. Best for: players who hunt in safe areas (parks, home) where they won't be interrupted during charge.
S-Tier: Long Sword (LS)
The most popular weapon in MHN — 40% of players use it. Its Spirit Combo deals sustained damage without the GS charge vulnerability. The Helm Breaker (air attack into charged slash) is easier to execute on mobile than GS charges. Best for: players who want consistent DPS without risking full-charge interruptions.
A-Tier: Dual Blades (DB)
The fastest attack speed in MHN. Its Demon Mode gives +30% attack speed but locks you into attack animations. Best for: mobile players in dense urban areas where hunts are interrupted frequently — short attack windows are better than long ones when you might need to stop suddenly.
B-Tier: Sword & Shield, Hammer, Bowgun
S&S: Jack of all trades, master of none — decent DPS, decent utility. Hammer: KO damage is unique but Headlocks prevent running away from GPS zones. Bowgun: ranged safety but requires ammo management that's tedious on mobile.

⚠️ The 5 Weapon Mistakes

Mistake 1: Charging GS in Unsafe Areas
A GS charge takes 4 seconds. If you're hunting in a park and someone walks by, your character is stuck in animation. Cancel your charge with the dodge button if interrupted. Practice "tap-charge-tap" (short charges) for outdoor hunts.
Mistake 2: Not Using Weapon-Specific Gems
Each weapon has specific gem slots. GS wants Attack gems (4-slotted), LS wants Spirit Gauge gems (4-slotted). Using random gems instead of weapon-optimized gems = 30% less DPS from gems alone.
Mistake 3: Fighting Nergigante Without Poison Resist
Nergigante's dive bomb applies a bleed that deals 50 damage/second. Without poison resist gems (3 slots, each gives +5 poison resist), you lose 20% of your hunt time to survivability issues.
Mistake 4: Solo Hunting vs Co-op Decision
In MHN, solo hunts give 100% rewards. Co-op hunts give 50% rewards per player (total 150% if 3 players). For rare materials, co-op is better. For XP farming, solo is more efficient. Don't default to co-op without knowing which reward structure you're optimizing for.
