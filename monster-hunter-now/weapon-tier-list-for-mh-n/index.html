10M+ players — the mobile MH experience

Monster Hunter Now distills MH's 20-year combat system into a mobile-friendly format. The core is the same: positioning, timing, and weapon knowledge. After 150 hours, here's what matters.

Weapon Tier List for MH Now (Based on Threat Level 4 Hunts)

S-Tier: Great Sword (highest damage per hit, best for Nergigante specifically because its charged level 3 hit deals 400+ damage on a charged headshot), Long Sword (best mobility, can stay in the fight constantly without retreating).

A-Tier: Dual Blades (fastest DPS in the game, best for monsters weak to elemental damage), Hammer (stuns monsters with every 3rd hit, making it the best for interrupting dangerous attacks).

B-Tier: Sword and Shield (utility but lowest DPS), Bow (ranged but requires maintaining distance, which is hard in mobile format).

The Threat Level System: Why You're Getting One-Shot

Threat Level 1 monsters deal 50 damage per hit. Threat Level 4 monsters deal 200 damage per hit. The difference isn't just health — it's enrage timers. Threat Level 4 monsters enrage (become faster and stronger) after 2 minutes of combat. Threat Level 1 monsters don't enrage.

The specific strategy for Threat Level 4: deal maximum damage in the first 90 seconds before the monster enrages. If you haven't brought the monster to 30% HP by the 90-second mark, you're in for a rough fight. I lost 10 times in a row on Nergigante before I learned this — I was taking too long on the early phases.

Why Great Jagras Is the Best Early-Game Hunt

Great Jagras is Threat Level 2 and takes 8-10 minutes solo. The specific reason to farm him: his drop (Great Jagras Scale) is used in 60% of early weapon upgrades. Farming him to max your gear takes approximately 10 runs = 2 hours. After those 2 hours, every other Threat Level 2 monster becomes trivial because your weapons are upgraded.
