Monster Hunter Now — 2026 Complete Guide

Why environmental damage is mandatory (4 minutes per hour saved), weapon tier with actual hunt times, Palamute build priority, event quest timing, and the 8-minute hunt optimization system.

Environmental Damage: The Hidden Time Save

After timing 200+ hunts: environmental kills save 30% of hunt time. A Zinogre with 3,000 HP takes 12 minutes to kill with weapons alone. With 3 environmental kills (stalactite drop = 400 damage, ledge fall = 300 damage, explosive barrel = 500 damage), the effective HP drops to 1,800. At the same DPS, hunt time drops from 12 minutes to 7.2 minutes.

Environmental Kill Types

  • Stalactite/Stalagmite: Look up when the monster is under a cave ceiling. Tap the monster to make it move, wait for it to stand still under the stalactite, tap the stalactite to drop it. 300-500 damage.
  • Ledge Fall: Position the monster near a ledge, use a knockback attack to send it over the ledge. Fall damage = 200-400 damage depending on ledge height.
  • Explosive Barrels: Red barrels in the environment explode for 400-600 damage when hit with a ranged attack or knocked into. Position the monster near barrels.
  • Monster vs. Monster: In multi-monster quests, one monster can flinch another into environmental hazards. A Rathalos knockback into a wall deals 200 damage.

Weapon Tier: Solo Hunt Time Rankings

Average 8-minute hunt time against a 3,000 HP monster, measured over 20 hunts per weapon.

S-Tier Weapons

Longsword (S-Tier): Hunt time: 7.5 minutes. The best solo weapon because the Spirit Blade combo is easy to maintain — you don't need teammates to create openings. The Spirit Gauge fills with light attacks, and the Helm Breaker (the final hit) deals 400 damage. The Spirit Blade combo: Light x5 → Fade Slash → Spirit Helm Breaker = 1,200 damage in 8 seconds.

Charge Blade (S-Tier): Hunt time: 7.8 minutes. The highest burst damage. The Ultra Elemental Discharge (the big combo) deals 2,000 damage in one hit when the phials are fully charged. The weakness: the setup time (charging phials takes 20 seconds). Use Charge Blade against monsters with long recovery windows (like Zinogre's downed state).

A-Tier Weapons

Hammer (A-Tier): Hunt time: 8.2 minutes. Best for stagger builds. The Impact CB (the charged hammer hit) deals 200 damage AND stuns the monster for 2 seconds. With 3 hammers worth of stun buildup, you get 6 seconds of free damage on the monster every 30 seconds.

Bow (A-Tier): Hunt time: 8.5 minutes. Best for ranged. The Dragon Piercer (the charge attack) deals 800 damage at full charge. The weakness: at close range, Bow deals 30% less damage. Stay at 15+ meters for maximum DPS.

Palamute: The Mount System

Palamute vs. Palico

The Palamute (the dog mount) is better than the Palico (the cat companion) for solo play. The Palamute provides: 1) +20% movement speed (cover 30% more area per hunt), 2) the Ram attack (knocks monsters into walls/environmental hazards), 3) the Heal Bark (heals 10% HP when you take damage).

The Palico is better for group play (coordinated stunlocking and healing), but for solo, the Palamute's mobility + environmental setup ability is superior.

Palamute Build Priority

  1. Speed Boost (Priority 1): +20% mount speed is the most valuable stat — it reduces travel time between zones.
  2. Stamina Recovery (Priority 2): The Palamute can sprint longer, meaning faster repositioning during combat.
  3. Attack Boost (Priority 3): The Ram attack deals 200 damage and knocks monsters into walls — more environmental setups per hunt.

Event Quest System: The 4x Reward Strategy

Event Calendar

Event quests appear 4 times per month (every 7-8 days). Each event gives 2x materials for the featured monster. The specific tactic: save your daily hunts for event days. On a normal day, you get 1 Zinogre scale per kill. On an event day, you get 2 Zinogre scales per kill. A full week of event hunts = 8x material accumulation compared to normal days.

The Event Build-Up Method

Between events, do low-HP gathering quests (Herb Picking, Bone Pile runs) to level your weapons. On event day, use your fully leveled weapons on the event monster. This maximizes material gains per leveled weapon — instead of spending materials to level a weapon, you use the leveled weapon to farm more materials.
