The Boss Progression Order Problem

After dying 20+ times fighting Bonemass first: the game's suggested boss order is wrong. The game tells you to find Bonemass in the Swamp, but the Swamp requires Iron weapons, and Iron requires the Black Forest's crypts. Here's the correct order that maximizes survival.

The Correct Boss Order

  1. Eikthyr (Meadows): Kill at level 1-2 with a Wood Bow. The fight: kite the deer-boss with arrows, never let it charge at you. Reward: 2 Eikthyr Trophies = max stamina upgrade.
  2. The Elder (Black Forest): Kill at level 3-5 with a Finewood Bow + Bronze Sword. The fight: stay at 20+ meters range, dodge the root attack. Reward: 2 Elder Trophies = max carry capacity upgrade.
  3. Bonemass (Swamp): Kill at level 7+ with full Iron armor + Iron Mace. The fight: Bonemass's summon (the skeleton spawns) is the threat — kill skeletons first. Reward: Wishbone (for finding silver). Do NOT fight Bonemass before Iron.
  4. Yagluth (Mountain): Kill at level 8+ with Wolf armor + Frostner (the best weapon for Yagluth). The fight: Yagluth's summon (the bonelings) die to Frostner's AoE. Reward: 2 Yagluth Trophies = max armor upgrade.
  5. Moder (Plains): Kill at level 9+ with Wolf armor + Blackmetal weapon. The fight: kill the dragon when it's grounded, kite when flying. Reward: 2 Dragon Tears = build Dragon abilities.

Build Tier List: Kill Time Rankings

Kill time against a 2,000 HP troll, measured over 10 attempts per build.

S-Tier Builds

Bow Build (S-Tier): Kill time: 45 seconds. The best early-game build. At 20-meter range, the Finewood Bow deals 35 damage per hit, 2 shots per second = 70 DPS. The troll never reaches you. Survival rate in the Meadows and Black Forest: 95%. The weakness: at the Mountain biome, bow falls off — Deathsquitos attack faster than you can kite.

Two-Handed Axe (S-Tier): Kill time: 30 seconds. The Iron Axe deals 60 damage per hit, 1.5 hits per second = 90 DPS. The two-handed axe staggers most enemies (they can't attack while staggered). Best for players who want aggressive melee play.

A-Tier Builds

Shield + One-Handed (A-Tier): Kill time: 40 seconds. The parry timing (perfect block = full damage reflect + 3-second stun) is the highest-skill, highest-reward playstyle. A perfectly timed parry against a troll's ground pound reflects 200 damage back. The weakness: parry timing takes 10+ hours to master.

Biome Resource Reference

Meadows (Starting Biome)

Resources: Wood, Berries, Flint, Deer. The Meadows is safe (no dangerous enemies) and provides all early-game materials. Build your first base here. The rule: stay in Meadows until you have a full set of Bronze armor (20 HP per piece = 80 total HP).

Black Forest (Bronze Age)

Resources: Bronze Ore, Iron Ore (in crypts), Finewood, Tar. The Black Forest has 3 dangers: Greydwarfs (come in groups), Trolls (one-shot you with bronze armor), and the darkness (enemies spawn at night). Strategy: arrive at dawn, bring a bow, clear the perimeter before mining.

Swamp (Iron Age)

Resources: Iron Ore, Chains, Leech. The Swamp's danger: the Wraiths (ghost enemies that deal 50 HP damage through armor), the Leech (slows you in water), and the Surtlings (fireballs). Strategy: always check the minimap before entering — avoid blood pits (the Leech spawn point) and build on a raised hill (Sunken Crypts are below, but the hill above is safe).

Base Building: The Raid Avoidance Strategy

The Raid Mechanic

Valheim raids are triggered by building value (the total cost of all placed objects). At every 50% value threshold, a raid triggers. A base worth 10,000 in materials triggers a raid every 2 in-game days. A base worth 1,000 in materials triggers a raid every 20 in-game days.

The strategy: keep your main base under 5,000 in total build value. Use the 10,000+ value base for production (smelters, forges) but don't sleep there. The raid enemies (which spawn outside the build circle) will attack the production base, not your sleeping base.
