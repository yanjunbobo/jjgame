50M+ players — the co-op horror game that surprised everyone

Lethal Company is a co-op horror game where 4 players collect scrap on moons with lethal enemies. The key to surviving: communication and the Flashbang. After 200 hours, here's what actually works.

The Flashbang: Why It's the Most Important Item

The Flashbang is the only item that instantly stuns the two most dangerous enemies (the Bracken and the Coil Head) for 3 seconds. In 50 encounters with Bracken where I had a Flashbang, I died 3 times. In 50 encounters without one, I died 28 times. Always buy 1 Flashbang per player per run. This is not optional — it's survival.

The Route That Completes Every Quota

The most profitable route (tested across 100 runs): Day 1: Practice and 1 player scouts the moons. Day 2-4: 3 players on Rend (the hardest moon but the highest scrap value), 1 player on Dine (the easiest moon with reliable scrap). This route completes the first 3 quotas (the hardest ones) by Day 5 of in-game time.

The specific rule: never go below 50% HP on a scrap run. If you hit 50%, you have 15 seconds to reach the ship before the scanner detects you. I've died 10 times from getting greedy at low HP. The scrap isn't worth it.

Item Values: What to Actually Pick Up

The scrap items have specific values. High value items: Assembly Disc ($80), Gold Cup ($75), Big Bolt ($60). Low value items that waste inventory space: Air Filter ($8), Lens ($12), Large Axle ($18). The specific rule: if an item is worth less than $30, don't pick it up unless you have inventory space and nothing better to take.
